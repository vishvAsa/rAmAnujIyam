## रहस्य-त्रय-जीवाताव् अलीकम्
<details open><summary>मूलम्</summary>

मेल् ऎऴुदिय रहस्यत्रय-जीवादुव् ऎऩ्गिऱ ग्रन्थत्तिलेय् इरुक्किऱव् अवर्गळुडैय मुगवचनम् ऎऴुदुगिऱदु, ऎप्-पडिय् ऎऩ्ऱाल्:  

> "विष्णुवे मोक्षम् प्रसादिप्पर्;  
अवर्-उडैय पत्निय् आऩ लक्ष्मि  
अदऱ्कुप् पुरुष-कार-मात्रम् ऎऩ्ऱु नम्माचार्यर्गळ्-उडैय सम्प्रदायम् इरुप्पदु।  
> वेदान्ताचार्यरोव् ऎऩ्ऱाल्  
> लक्ष्मिक्कु मोक्षोपायत्वञ् जॊल्लिक् कॊण्ड् इरुक्किऱार्"  

ऎऩ्ऱु रहस्यत्रयजीवादुविले सॊऩ्ऩार्गळ्।  
इन्द मुखवचनत्तिले अहोबिलमठत्तार् तॆऩ्ऩाजार्यर्गळैत् तङ्गळुक्क् आचार्यर्गळ् ऎऩ्ऱुम्,  
वेदान्दाचार्यरैत् तङ्गळ् आचार्याऩ्ऱ् ऎऩ्ऱुङ् कॊण्डार्गळ् ऎऩ्ऱु  
मिगवुम् वॆळियागक् काणप् पडुगिऱदु।
</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

They have been upholding and preserving this tradition। Their introductory statement, found in the aforementioned treatise titled *Rahasyatraya Jeevathu*, is recorded as follows: 

> "It is the established tradition of our *Acharyas* (spiritual preceptors) that Vishnu alone grants *Moksha* (liberation), and that His consort, Lakshmi, serves merely as an intermediary (*Purushakara*) in this process। 

In contrast, the *Rahasyatraya Jeevathu* notes that *Vedantacharya* posits that Lakshmi herself serves as a means (*Upaya*) for attaining *Moksha*।" 

From this introductory statement, it becomes abundantly clear that the Ahobila Matha regards the *Thennacharyas* (Southern School Acharyas) as their own spiritual preceptors, while not acknowledging *Vedantacharya* as their Acharya।
</details>

## तिरुपताव् अर्चकायोत्कोचः - अपवादः
<details open><summary>मूलम्</summary>

पिऩ्बु तिरुमलैयिले नाऱ्पदु वर्षत्तिले वडगलैयार्  
अर्च्चगऩुक्कु त्रवियङ्गळैक् कॊडुत्तु  
अर्च्चगऩैक् कैवसमाक्किक्कॊण्डु  
आराधनारम्बत्तिले "तुडङ्गण्डि श्रीसैलेस तयाबादरम्"  ऎऩ्ऱु अऩादियाय् अर्च्चगऩ् सॊल्लुगिऱवऴक्कत्तिले अत्तैत् तळ्ळि  
"तुडङ्गण्डि रामा नुजदयाबात्रम्"  ऎऩ्ऱु अर्च्चगऩ् सॊल्लुवदागक् कट्टुप् पाडि पण्णियिरुन्दार्गळ्।  

अर्च्चगऩुन् द्रव्यत्तै वाङ्गिक्कॊण्डु अप्पडि।ये सॊल्लुवदाग निऩैत्तिरुन्दु, अन्द समयत्तिले पॆरुमाळ् अवऩ्मेले आवेसिक्क अवऩ् मॆय्मऱन्दु "तुडङ्गण्डि श्रीसैले सदयाबात्र" मॆऩ्ऱु वऴक्कप्पडि सॊल्लिविट्टाऩ्। अप्पोदु वड कलैयार् पङ्गप्पट्टुप्पोऩार्गळ्। पिऩ्बु अन्द वर्च्चगऩै नीवऴक्कप् पडिक्कु ऎऩ्सॊऩ्ऩाय्? ऎऩ्ऱु केट्टविडत्तिल्, अन्द वर्च्चगऩ् पॆरुमाळॆऩ्मेले यावेचित्तु उडम्बु तॆरियादुबोऩबडियाले वऴक्कप्पडिक्कु सॊऩ्ऩेऩॆऩ्ऱु सॊऩ्ऩाऩ्। पिऩ्बॊरुक्काल् वड

செய்ய, பெரியவாச்சாம்பிள்ளை யருளிச்செய்தது :- தூப்பில்பிள்ளை? நீர் அனு கூலஸஹவாஸத்தைப் பண்ணாமல் அஹங்காராதிகளாலே இதர ஸஹவா ஸத்தைப் பண்ணிக்கொண்டு ப்ரயோஜனாந்தரபரராய் வித்யா நிமித்தமாக மந்த்ராந்தரங்களை யுபதேசம் பண்ணிவித்துக்கொண்டு ஹயக்ரீவோபவாஸம் பண்ணி, அத்தாற்பெற்ற வித்யாதிசயத்தை யுடையராயிராநின்றீர்; உபா ஸனையாற்பெற்ற வித்யாதியத்திற்கு ப்ரயோஜனம், இதரமதநிரஸனமும் பூர்வசார்ய ஸம்ப்ரதாய பரிபாலனமு மாகையாலே அத்தைச்செய்யாதே பூர்வாசார்ய ஸம்ப்ரதாயஸித்தமான வர்த்தங்களைக்கண்டித்து விச்வாமித்ர ஸ்ருஷ்டியாக நவீனமான வர்த்தங்களை ப்ரவர்த்திப்பியா நின்றீர்; மஹாபசாரமாகையாலே பெருமாள் திருவுள்ளம் மிகவுங்கலங்கிற்று. ஆகையாலே யத்தைத் தவிரவேணுமென்று (பெரியவாச்சாம்பிள்ளை) அருளிச்செய்தார். அது அதற்குத் தூப்பில் பிள்ளை விண்ணப்பம்:- அடியேனப்படிசெய்தேனோ? ஸ்ரீ பாஷ்ய க்ரந்தங்களைத்தானே பிரவசனம் பண்ணிக்கொண்டு அத்தை யனுஸரித்தே பின்னையுஞ் சிலக்ரந்தங்களையும் பண்ணி லோகத்திலே ப்ர வர்த்திப்பித்துக்கொண்டு போந்தேனென்று விண்ணப்பஞ் செய்தார்.

அதற்கு பெரியவாச்சாம்பிள்ளை யருளிச்செய்தது:- வாரீர் தூப்பில் பிள்ளை! ஸ்ரீ பாஷ்ய விஷயமன்று காணுமிப்போது நாஞ்சொல்லுகிறது, அதுவிஷ யத்திலேதான் நீர் அனுகூலமாகச்செய்தீரோ? அதுவுமில்லை,ஸ்ரீ பாஷ்ய க்ரந்தங்களிலே சிறிது குற்றங்களுண்டென்று சிந்தித்து நீ ரெழுதத்துடங் நீ கினவளவிலே பாஷ்யகாரர் உம்முடைய ஸ்வப்நத்திலே சீறியருளி முக் கோலாலே புடைத்த பின்பன்றோ பயபக்திகளுண்டாய் அனுதாபம் பிற து அபராத க்ஷாமணார்த்தமாக யதிராஜஸப்ததி விண்ணப்பஞ் செய்த தும், பாஷ்ய க்ரந்தங்களைத் தானே ப்ரவசநம்பண்ணத்துடங்கினதும், ஆகையாலது கிடக்கட்டும். நம்மாழ்வார்நாதமுனிகளுக்கு திவ்ய ப்ரபந்த விஷயமாகவும், ரஹஸ்ய விஷயமாகவும், ப்ரத்யக்ஷமாக ஸாதித்த வர்த்த விசேஷங்களைக் கண்டித்து வேறே சிலவர்த்தங்களைக் கல்பியாநின்று கொண்டு வ்யாக்யானங்களைப்பரக்க வெழுதா நின்றீர்; அத்தையன்றோ நாம் தவிறச்சொல்லுகிறோமென்றருளிச்செய்தார்.

In response, Periyavachchan Pillai graciously declared: "Oh Thoopil Pillai! Instead of seeking the company of congenial souls, you have—driven by ego and similar vices—associated with incompatible individuals. Pursuing goals other than the ultimate spiritual end, you have sought initiation into mantras other than the prescribed ones for the sake of acquiring knowledge; specifically, you observed a vow dedicated to Lord Hayagriva, and thereby attained an extraordinary depth of scholarship. Since the true purpose of such scholarship—acquired through spiritual discipline—is the refutation of opposing doctrines and the preservation of the traditions established by our Preceptors, you have failed to fulfill this duty. Instead, you have challenged the tenets established by our Preceptors and, much like Viswamitra creating a parallel universe, have propagated novel concepts of your own invention. As this constitutes a grave transgression, the Divine Mind of the Lord has been deeply distressed." 

Therefore, Periyavachchan Pillai commanded him to desist from such conduct. To this, Thoopil Pillai humbly submitted his plea: "Did I truly act in such a manner? I have merely expounded upon the texts of the *Sri Bhashyam*; adhering strictly to its principles, I have authored certain additional treatises and have been propagating them for the benefit of the world."

To this, Periyavaccan Pillai graciously replied: "Come here, Thuppil Pillai! What I am speaking of now does not concern the *Sri Bhashya*. Indeed, did you conduct yourself in a manner favorable to that subject? You did not; for was it not only after you began to write—thinking that there were certain flaws within the *Sri Bhashya* texts—that the *Bhashyakara* (Ramanuja) appeared in your dream, rebuked you sternly, and struck you with his triple staff? 

Was it not *after* that incident that you were filled with reverential awe and remorse, composed the *Yatiraja Saptati* as an act of atonement for your transgression, and began to deliver discourses on the *Bhashya* texts yourself? Therefore, let that matter rest. However, regarding the specific spiritual truths—concerning both the *Divya Prabandham* and the esoteric doctrines (*Rahasya*)—that Nammazhvar personally revealed to Nathamuni, you have contradicted them; instead, you have fabricated alternative interpretations and are now extensively writing commentaries based on them. Is it not *this* conduct that I am now pointing out as erroneous?"

To this, Thuppil Pillai submitted his plea: "Did Nammazhwar himself ever provide commentaries for his own esoteric doctrines (*Rahasya*) or his Divine Hymns (*Divya Prabandham*)? It is only if he had done so that it would be incumbent upon this humble self to follow suit. Are not the commentaries we possess the works graciously composed by the Acharyas who came after him? Since certain interpretations found within those commentaries did not align with scriptural authority (*Pramana*), was it not precisely those specific points that I rose to critique and refute?"

To this, Periyavachan Pillai graciously replied: "It is not as you perceive it! In the early dawn of the Kali Yuga, the *Thiruvaimozhi* and other works—which Nammazhwar had propagated through the medium of Madhurakavi Azhwar—fell into oblivion. Subsequently, Periya Perumal (the Presiding Deity), utilizing Nammazhwar (who had manifested in his *Archa* form), graciously revealed these works directly (*Sakshatkara*) to Nathamuni. Nathamuni, in turn, graciously transmitted them through the sacred lineage (*Sampradaya*) to the succeeding Acharyas. The *Poorvacharyas* (early preceptors) merely codified these teachings into written texts so that they might become accessible to the dull-witted minds of this later age; they did not, however, compose any original commentaries of their own accord. Therefore, you must desist from propagating interpretations that deviate from this established tradition."

To this, Thuppil Pillai submitted a further plea: "Would the Azhwar ever graciously reveal teachings that stand in contradiction to scriptural authority? I humbly request that you enlighten this servant regarding this matter."

Periyavachan Pillai graciously replied: "Since the Azhwar, having attained the state of liberation (*Mukti*), possesses a direct, intuitive realization (*Sakshatkara*) of all truths, and since he propagated these very truths—which he had thus realized—through the medium of Nathamuni, it is incumbent upon us to rely upon the scriptural authorities that align with his teachings, while simultaneously refuting and discarding any interpretations that do not accord with them. We must utilize that which is clearly perceived and established to validate and reconcile those aspects that remain unseen or obscure." One must not use the unseen to destroy the seen; [as exemplified by] Alavandar, Emperumanar, Thirukurugaippiran Pillan, and Arulalapperumal Emperuman.

Have you heard the deeds of Nara, Bhatta, Nanjiya, etc.? Will the world know a Brahmana that they do not know? All of them, because the scriptures belonging to the Pratya Yakshas of Azhwar, who were not ignorant of the Kevala scriptures, were powerful, they received the foundation that Azhwar had revealed through the Nadamundi in philosophy and practice through the Yupadesa lineage and decided that it was the truth. They rejected the verses that did not belong to it like the Kapiladhi verses, and they embraced the beneficial verses and taught the ancient tradition to everyone under their own Sripada. Therefore, the essence of the discipline was the same. 

In this way, the Purvacharya, as a noble characteristic of the ancient tradition, determined the meaning with the help of the Brahmanas and the thirty verses, and left behind a new path. To this, the sage, having felt sympathy through the dream and the knowledge of the sages, realized that all the explanations given by the ancient teachers were from the lineage of the Azhwars, and that the wise men in the world had made explanations according to their own wisdom. Now, through the dream and the truth that you have revealed to my servant, I have come to know the truth. He said, "I have come to know the truth." 

He then brought the new scriptures that he had made before, and looking at those who had placed Periyavachampilla before him, he ordered that no one should ever publish these scriptures anywhere. Until then, he had surpassed the country.
