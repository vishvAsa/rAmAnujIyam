## 36. Śri Abhinava Vāgeesha Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakāla Swāmi (Current Âchārya)

**Srimat Abhinava Vāgīśa Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakāla Mahā Desikan (1992 – present)**

![Śri Abhinava Vāgeesha Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakāla Swāmi](https://www.parakalamatham.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/3600-Brahmatantra-Swatantra-Jeer-Swami-CLEAN-1.png)

> श्रीमन्नूतन रङ्गनाथयतिराडाप्तात्म विद्यालयम् ।  
> श्रीमन्नूतन लक्ष्मणार्ययतिराट् संप्राप्त तुर्याश्रमम् ।  
> श्रीवासेन्द्र कटाक्ष सन्ततसुधा लक्ष्यात्म योगाञ्चितं ।  
> श्रीमन्नूतन वागधीशयमिनं भक्त्या श्रयामो गुरुम् ।।  
> नव्य वागीशयोगीन्द्रं हयास्यपदसेविनम् ।  
> ब्रह्मतन्त्र स्वतन्त्रार्यम् भजाम स्वात्मसंपदम् ।।३६।।

* **Period of Adorning the Peetam**: Ascended the Maha Peetam in the year 1992 C.E.
* **Tirunakshatram**: Mesha, Revathi

**Biography:**
Srimad Abhinava Vageesha Brahma Tantra Swatantra Parakala Maha Desikan Swamy was coronated as the 36th Peethaadhipati in 1992. HH is an eminent Disciple of the Celebrated Srimad Abhinava Ranganatha Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakala Swamy (33rd Pontiff) having mastered all Vishishtadvaitic lore under his guidance. He has mastery over both the Vedantas, and is above all a Mantrasiddha MahApurusha. The nonagenarian Acharyan is still working incessantly to provide moral and spiritual guidance to tens and thousands of his disciples in India and abroad.

For a more detailed account of his life and accomplishments as well as some memorable photographs from the past, please see “Vageesha Priya” the multi-language Souvenir brought out at his Shatabhishekam Celebrations in 2014.

## 33. Śri Abhinava Ranganātha Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakāla Swāmi

![Śri Abhinava Ranganātha Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakāla Swāmi](https://www.parakalamatham.org/wp-content/uploads/3300-Brahmatantra-Swatantra-Jeer-Swami-CLEAN.png)

> श्रीकृष्ण ब्रह्मतन्त्रोत्तमगुरुकरुणाप्तचक्राङ्कभाष्यम्  
> वैराग्याचारवार्घो वरदपदमुखे लक्ष्मणे न्यस्तभारम् ।  
> श्रीवागीशात्ततुर्यं शठरिपुयतिराड्वेदचूडार्यमूर्तिम्  
> नूतनं श्रीरङ्गनाथं कलिरिपुमनघं ब्रह्मतन्त्रं श्रयामः ।  
> श्री तुरगवदन पादूसन्ततपरिचरणमात्र परमार्थः ।  
> जयतु श्रीमानभिनवरङ्गेन्द्रब्रह्म तन्त्र परकालः ।।३३।।

* **Period of Adorning the Peetam**: 1925-1967  
* **Tirunakshatram**: Aashāda, Punarvasu  

**Biography:**  
Born in Thaarana-Parthiva (Aashada Punarvasu 1884) as a result of the prayers of Sri Lakshmi Nrsimha, Sri Ramachandra, Rangacharya, a scion of the family of Hareetha to which Sri Bhagavadh Ramanuja belonged, early moved out to Mysore from his place of birth at the age of seventeen prompted by his unquenchable thirst for the knowledge to crown his knowledge of Shastras which he had already attained at an early age. Sri Krishna Brahmatantra Pakala Swami (31st Jiyar) discerned this legitimate greed for knowledge, and bestowed on him Sri Chakrankanam and Sri Bhasyartham and consigned him to the care of Sri ‘Pandita-ratnam’ Kasthuri Rangacharya Swami, who had already won for himself as great a name in India as his renowned teacher Sri Ranganatha Brahmatantra Parakala Swami (30th Jiyar). Along with the study of Darshanams, Nyaya, Sahithya, Mimamsa, Vaisheshika and Vishishtadvaitha Vedantha, Sri Rangacharya conducted his regular studies of Sahitya, Alankara, Vyakarana, etc. under the respective teachers in Maharaja’s Sanskrit College.  

On one occasion in Pushya-Makara, during a solar-eclipse in the early hours of the morning, when there was an unparalleled and terrible downpour accompanied by a biting chill, amidst the thousands who gathered on the banks of the Kaveri, in ‘Gauthama Kshethra’, there was this solitary Grihastha with his grihini and the sacred fire in the pot! protecting it with all his resources as a fond mother does her baby threatened by a ruffian for its jewels. Such has ever been the unwavering, unflinching appalling earnestness, devotion and detachment of this Acharya to the practice of Dharma.  

Having been called upon to fill the position of the Trustee and guardian of Sri Hayagriva Samsthanam, he had naturally been devoting all his energies, all his physical and mental resources to Seva with unflinching resolve like ‘Sri Varada Ramanuja Swami of Sri Rangam and Kanchi’—from whom as Sri Rangacharya, as a grihastha, received the ‘Bharanyasam’, Srimadh Abhinava Ranganatha Swami had won renown all over the country, as ‘Vairagya-Acharya-Vardhi’, in addition to his being regarded as ‘Jnanaamburashi’, like ‘Sri Maha Parakala Swami'(21st Jiyar), whose seat he had occupied. Like him too, Swami was engaged in the ‘Jeernoddhara’ kainkaryam of Sri Vedantha Deshika Sannidhis in various Divya Deshams and also acquiring centres from where the teachings of Sri Ramanuja may be carried forward, in Thirupathi, in distant Rewah (Rajasthan), Azhwar Thirunagari, Thiruvahindrapuram, etc. To facilitate the propaganda of Sri Vedantha Deshikar, he himself started the ‘Vedantha Vihara Sabha’ under the auspices of which annual examinations were conducted on the model of ancient ‘sadas’, prizes awarded and extension lectures delivered in the Mutt.  

Removal of some of the disabilities of the ‘Thirukkulattars’ (Harijans) also received the attention of this Great Sage as was exemplified when the Late Pandit Sri Madana Mohana Malaviya approached the Swami on one occasion during his visit to Mysore for the purpose. After a thorough discussion, for two days in succession, it was agreed that ‘Manthra-Deeksha’ was allowed by Shastras to the Harijans with some restrictions, and Sri Malaviya went back satisfied with the verdict of Swami. On another Occasion (Dec 1927), when an earnest seeker of truth from Marburg, a European Gentleman named Rudolf Otto, sought an interview with Swami, the latter readily accorded him the interview. The scholar went away enlightened and acknowledged his obligation to the Swami in his book, *‘India’s Religion of Grace and Christianity Compared and Contrasted’* (1930).  

His Highness Sri Maharaja Marthanda Singhjee of Rewah visited the Mutt and paid obeisance to the Acharya. Srimadh Abhinava Ranganatha Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakala Swami occupied a high position in the spiritual world of South India, similar to his predecessor Sri Maha Parakala Swamy of the 16th Century. As the ‘Raja-Guru’ to the Royal Family of Mysore, Swami blessed the Wodeyars, Sriman Maharaja Sri Nalvadi Krishnaraja Wodeyar and Sriman Maharaja Sri Jayachamarajendra Wodeyar Bahudur with Sri Vaishnava Dikha and Kalakshepams. Swami attained paramapadam at the age of 82. Swami adorned the Gadi of Sri Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakala Swamy Mutt for 40 years (1925-1966 Asthana Nirvaham) with eternal kainkaryam to Sri Lakshmi Narayana and Sri Lakshmi Hayavadana.

**Swami’s works:**
* Hayashiropakhyana
* Tatvamuktakalapa - Commentaries in 3 volumes
* Gudartha Sangraha (His Magnum Opus on Sri Bhashya)
* Vedartha Sanjivanam
* Thraiyantha Saramrtham
* Hayavadana Rathnamala Sthothram
* Aupanishad Manihara
* Upanishaddhara
* Ramanuja Matha Darsha
* Paduka Sthuthi

## 31. Śri Krishna Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakāla Swāmi

![Śri Krishna Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakāla Swāmi](https://www.parakalamatham.org/wp-content/uploads/3100-Brahmatantra-Swatantra-Jeer-Swami-CLEAN.png)

> श्रीवासब्रह्मतंत्राभिधकलिमथनावप्त चक्राङ्कभष्यं  
> श्रीवासे देशिकेन्द्रे कलिमथनगुरावर्पितात्मीयभारम्  
> रङ्गेन्द्रब्रह्मतन्त्रादिमपदकलिजिद्देशिकेन्द्रात्ततुर्यं  
> श्रीकृष्णब्रह्मतन्त्राग्रि मपदपरकालार्यवर्यं श्रयाम : ।।३१।।

* **Period of Adorning the Peetam**: 1885-1914 (C.E.)  
* **Tirunakshatram**: Jyeshta, Krishna Panchami, Tiruvona

**Biography:**  
This Jîyar was born in the Shaka year 1762 (1839 C.E.) by the grace of Śri Śrinivasa Brahmatantra Swatantra Swāmi. Therefore, this child was given the purvāshrama name of Krishnamacharya. He ascended the Maha Peetam in the year 1885 C.E.  

To the Matam, he brought along the Archāvigraha of Lakshmi Nārāyana, which was being worshipped in his familial lineage. He composed 67 Śri Granthams. The *Parakāla Guru Vijaya Champu* describing the life of the 28th Jîyar is a work that this Swāmi composed in his Purva ashramam.  

Maha Matāshri Śri Maharani Vanivilasam Ammanni (the wife of Chamarajendra Wodeyar) received Bharanyaasam at the feet of this Jîyar. Her son Śri Krishna Raja Wodeyar IV received Vaishnava deeksha and Panchasamskāra from him. This Jîyar completed 30 Chaturmāsya Sankaplams.

## 30. Śri Ranganātha Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakāla Swāmi

> श्रीघंटांशात्तचक्राङ्‌ङ्कनमुदितहृदं प्राप्तवेदान्तयुग्म  
> वेदान्ताचार्यदधीतहितनिगमभरं श्रीनिवसांघ्रियुग्मात् ।  
> श्रीमच्छ्रीवाससध्धेशिकवरपुरुहूतात्ततुर्याश्रमं  
> तम् वन्दे श्रीरङ्गनाथं कलिमथनगुरुं ब्रह्मतन्त्राध्यशब्दं ।।  
> श्रीरामानुजसिद्धान्तस्थापको जयतादयम्।  
> रङ्गनाथब्रह्मतन्त्र परकालयतीश्वरः ।।३०।।

* **Period of Adorning the Peetam**: 1873-1885 (C.E.)  
* **Tirunakshatram**: Vaishāka, Shukla/Poornima, Vishāka

**Biography:**  
This Jîyar was born along the banks of the river Bāhuda, at a village called Vāvillpādu, in Shaka 1735 (1812 C.E.), in the lineage of Vadhula Gotra. He ascended the Maha Peetam in the year 1873 C.E. after obtaining initiation into Sannyāsāshrama from Śri Śrinivasa Desikendra Swāmi.  

He composed *Śri Rahasya Traya Saara Sangraham* in Śloka format, *Tapa Chakrānkhana Pramāna Vritti*, *Sudarshana Vāda*, *Purusha Sukta Bhāshya*, and many other works dealing with the concept of Nyāya. Śri Chamarajendra Wodeyar received Vaishnava deeksha and Panchasamskāra from this Jîyar. He completed 13 Chaturmāsya Sankaplams.

* **Tirunakshatram**: Mārgashirsa, Shukla, Asvini

He performed Bhagavad Vishayam Upanyasams at various places. He composed *Dhatu Vritti Sangraham*, *Alankara Sangraham*, *Sudhi Vilochana Sangraham*, *Purva Prayoga Sangraham*, and other works. He completed 13 Chaturmāsya Sankaplams residing at the Śri Matams in Tirunārāyanapuram, Mahisurapuram, and Śrirangapattanam.

## 28. Śri Śrinivasa Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakāla Swāmi

![Śri Śrinivasa Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakāla Swāmi](https://www.parakalamatham.org/wp-content/uploads/2800-Brahmatantra-Swatantra-Jeer-Swami-CLEAN.png)

> श्रीमद्धंटावतारोत्तमकलिमथनावाप्तसत्संप्रदाय  
> श्रीभाष्यश्रीरहस्यत्रयसृतिमदसीयांन्ध्रि विन्यस्तभारम्।  
> वेदान्तब्रह्मतन्त्रभिधकलिमथनाल्लब्धतुर्याश्रमं श्री  
> श्रीवासब्रह्मतन्त्रदिमपदपरकालाख्यमाचार्यमीडे ।।२८।।

* **Period of Adorning the Peetam**: 1836-1861 (C.E.)  
* **Tirunakshatram**: Ashāda, Shukla/Poornima, Poorāda

**Biography:**  
This Jîyar was born in the lineage of the great Tirukkurukai Pirān Pillān, in the Shaka year 1713 (1790 C.E.), and was named Krishnamacharya. Upon the instruction of Śri Ghantāvatāra Swāmi, he obtained initiation into Sannyāsāshrama from Vêdānta Brahmatantra Swatantra Swami in the year 1836 C.E. In the same year, he ascended the Śri Peetam.  

He performed Ubhaya Vêdānta pravachanam at Tirunārāyanapuram and Mahisurapuram for many years. He taught the Gita Bhāshya and Bhagavad Vishayam to Śri Mummadi Krishnaraja Wodeyar. He undertook a victorious tour all over the country establishing the doctrine of Rāmānuja, which is well recorded in the work *Parakāla Guru Vijaya Champu*. He completed 26 Chaturmāsya Sankaplams.

He completed 7 Chaturmāsya Sankaplams residing at Tirunārāyanapuram and Mahisurapuram. He ascended to the supreme realm while Śri Ghantāvatāra Swāmi was still adorning the Śri Peetam.

## 26. Śri Ghantāvatāra Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakāla Swami

![Śri Ghantāvatāra Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakāla Swami](https://www.parakalamatham.org/wp-content/uploads/2600-Brahmatantra-Swatantra-Jeer-Swami-CLEAN.png)

> रामानुजादिपरकालयतीन्द्रलब्ध ।  
> वेदान्तयुग्मचरमाश्रमसंप्रदायम्।  
> श्रीशिङ्गरार्यगुरुवीक्षितमात्मवन्तं ।  
> घण्टावतारपरकालगुरुं श्रयामः ।।  
> वेदान्तदेशिकेन्द्रस्य सिधान्तस्थापनोत्सुकः।  
> जयतु श्रीशघंतशं परकालगुरूत्तमः ।।२६।।

* **Period of Adorning the Peetam**: 1810-1837 (C.E.)  
* **Tirunakshatram**: —

**Biography:**  
Born at Nallur along the banks of the river Pinākini in Shaka 1679 (1756 C.E.) in the lineage of the famed Śrivathsa Gotra, this Jîyar was named Krishnamacharya. He was initiated into Sannyāsāshrama by Śri Rāmānuja Brahmatantra Swatantra Swami in the year 1810 C.E. and ascended the Śri Peetam of the Matam in the same year. In virtue, this Jîyar was like the reincarnation of Vêdānta Deśika, hence the title ‘Ghantāvatāra’ was bestowed upon him.  

Under his leadership, the Śri Matam moved to Tirunārāyanapuram. He undertook a Divya Desha Yatra establishing the glory of the Śri Matam throughout the southern kingdoms, and then resided in Tirunārāyanapuram for many years. Thooppul, Tirumala, Śrirangapattanam, Tirunārāyanapuram, and Mahisurapuram became important branches of the Śri Matam.

## 25. Śri Rāmānuja Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakāla Swāmi

![Śri Rāmānuja Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakāla Swāmi](https://www.parakalamatham.org/wp-content/uploads/2500-Brahmatantra-Swatantra-Jeer-Swami-CLEAN.png)

> वन्देय श्रीनिलयपरकालात्तवेदान्तयुग्मं  
> तत्पादाब्जे विनिहितभरं ब्रह्मतन्त्रात्ततुर्यम् ।  
> श्रीकृष्णार्यादधिगतरथांगाब्जमब्जाक्षह्द्यं  
> श्रीमद्रामावरजपरकालाभिधं देशिकेन्द्रम्।।२५।।

* **Period of Adorning the Peetam**: 1772-1810 (C.E.)  
* **Tirunakshatram**: Mesha, Rohini

**Biography:**  
Born in the village of Bagepalli, Shaka year 1632 (1709 C.E.), in the famed Koundinya Gotra, as the son of Tirumalācharya, this Jîyar obtained Panchasamskāra and Matropadesha from Śri Tirumalai Krishnamācharya, a direct disciple of Śri Periya Parakāla Swāmi. He remained a Brahmacharin for the first 24 years of his life and then directly entered Sannyāsāshrama by the grace of Śri Śrinivasa Brahmatantra Swatantra Swāmi. He ascended the Maha Peetam of the Śri Matam after 38 Chaturmāsya Sankaplams at the age of 62 years.  

Maha Matrashree Lakshmammani, the Maharani of the Mysore kingdom, received Vaishnava deeksha and Panchasamskāra from this Jîyar. Due to regional conflicts, the Śri Matam was briefly shifted to Tirupathi. After the fall of Śrirangapattanam in 1799 C.E., this Jîyar returned to crown the young Śri Mummadi Krishnaraja Wodeyar as the Maharaja of Mahisurapura (Mysore). He advised Diwan Purnaiah to build the temple of Swami Sveta Varaha at Mysore, which became the future capital of the Mysore kingdom. He adorned the Śri Peetam to complete another 39 Chaturmāsya Sankaplams and lived for 102 years.

This Swāmi completed sixty Chaturmāsya Sankalpams. Establishing the authority of the Grantha *Śri Rahasya Traya Sāra*, this Swāmi showed the efficacy of Artha Prapatti.

![Śri Perarulāla Jîyar Brahmatantra Swatantra Swāmi](https://www.parakalamatham.org/wp-content/uploads/0100-First-Sri-Brahmatantra-Swatantra-Jeer-Swami-CLEAN.png)

* **Tirunakshatram**: Purattasi, Sravanam

Moola-Âchārya for the Sri Brahmatantra Swatantra Parakala Swami Matham. Śri Bhāshya, Gita Bhāshya, and Mantra Rahasya with Rahasya Traya Sāra came to this Jîyar directly from Vêdānta Deśika. The Bhagavad Vishayam came to this Jîyar from Vêdānta Deśika through Kumāra Varadāchārya.

This Jîyar composed the *Divya Sūri Stuti* and other works, and accepted the position of Śrikārya Dhurandhara of Tiruvênkatamudayān at Periya Perumāl Koil, Tirumala.

## Nigamānta Mahā Desikan

**Meaning:** May Śrīmān Veṅkaṭanātha — the lion among poets and logicians, and the illustrious ācārya who adorns the firmament of Vedānta — ever dwell in my heart.

### Śrī Vedānta Deśika – The Divine Incarnation of Compassion and Wisdom

Śrīmat Vedānta Mahā Deśika incarnated as the *aṁśa* (divine manifestation) of the sacred bell (*ghaṇṭā*) adorning the hand of Lord Śrī Veṅkaṭa Śrīnivāsa — a *nitya sūri* of eternal radiance. He took birth as the illustrious son of Śrī Ananta Sūri, belonging to the Viśvāmitra *gōtra*.

No mortal words can adequately convey the boundless greatness and divine glory of Swāmi Deśika. What follows is a brief and humble account of his life and works.

Swāmi Deśika mastered all the *Śāstras* at the divine lotus feet of his revered *Āchārya*, Śrīmat Appuḷḷār, who was also his maternal uncle. He performed *japa* of the sacred Śrīmat Garuda Mantra at Tiruvahīndrapuram on Oushadhādri, where he was blessed with the divine vision (*sākṣātkāra*) of Śrīmat Garuḍāzhvār, who initiated him into the sacred Śrīmat Hayagrīva Mantra.

Through profound *anushtāna* and unwavering *bhakti*, Swāmi Deśika performed the *japa* of the Śrīmat Hayagrīva Mantra and was thereafter blessed with the divine vision of Śrī Lakṣmī Hayagrīva Perumāḷ, receiving His complete grace and blessings.

In recognition of his unparalleled scholarship, devotion, and service, Swāmi Deśika was divinely conferred with the exalted titles:
* **“Vedāntācārya”** – by Periya Perumāḷ, Lord Śrī Raṅganātha Himself
* **“Sarva-tantra-svatantra”** – by Periya Pirāṭṭi, Śrī Raṅganāyaki Thāyār

He was also celebrated by the great scholars of Śrīraṅgam as **Kavi-tārkika-Siṁha** (the lion among poets and logicians) and **Samasya-Sahasrī** (the master of resolving a thousand challenges).

Swāmi Deśika propagated the *sampradāya* through his *pravachanams* in the sacred *divya desams* of Śrīraṅgam, Tiruvahīndrapuram, Śrī Kanchipuram, and Tiru Nārāyaṇapuram, among others. He composed more than **120 divine Śrī Sūktis**, thereby safeguarding and enriching the *Bhagavad Rāmānuja Darśanam* in every respect. He remains the only *Āchārya* who has composed *Śrī Sūktis* covering both the **Śārīraka Śāstra** (Vedānta) and the **Rahasya-traya-Viṣaya Śāstras**.

## Bhagavad Rāmānuja

![Bhagavad Rāmānuja](https://www.parakalamatham.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/F08-Bhagavad-Ramanuja-04-300x300.png)

> यो नित्यमच्युत पदाम्बुजयुग्म रुक्म-  
> व्यामोहतस्तदितराणि तृणाय मेने ।  
> अस्मद्गुरोर्भगवतोऽस्य दयैकसिन्धोः  
> रामानुजस्य चरणौ शरणं प्रपद्ये ॥

**Meaning:** I surrender at the lotus-feet of Śrī Rāmānujā, whose every moment remains lost in the radiant pair of the Lord’s lotus-feet, who thinks all else like blades of grass, who is the ocean of compassion by virtue of his divine preceptor.

### Śrī Bhagavad Rāmānujāchārya – The Beacon of Compassion and the Crest Jewel of the Āchārya Paramparā

Śrī Rāmānujāchārya stands as the radiant central pendant in the resplendent garland of the *Āchārya paramparā*. Among the most illustrious and venerated ācāryas of the *Śrī Vaiṣṇava sampradāya*, he firmly established the doctrine of *Śrī Viśiṣṭādvaita Vedānta*, fulfilling the divine intent of his predecessor, *Śrī Āḷavandār (Yāmunāchārya)*.

#### Early Life and Divine Grace
Śrī Rāmānuja was born in the year 1017 CE (Panguni Tiruvādirai star) in Sriperumbudur to Śrī Keśava Somayāji and Śrī Kāntimati, both devout followers of Lord Nārāyaṇa, belonging to the Harita *gotra*. From birth, he radiated divine brilliance, and even as a young boy, his compassion and sharp intellect were evident to all.

In his youth, he studied in Kāñchīpuram under the Advaitin scholar *Śrī Yādava Prakāśa*. His cousin, *Śrī Govinda Bhaṭṭar*, joined him in these studies. Endowed with extraordinary insight, Rāmānuja often offered interpretations of the *Upaniṣads* that reflected the supremacy and personal nature of the Lord, differing from his teacher’s interpretations.

The famous incident of the word *“Kapyāsam”* from the Chāndogya Upaniṣad exemplified this difference. Śrī Rāmānuja explained it as “the lotus-like eyes of the Supreme Lord,” while Yādava Prakāśa interpreted it in a less reverential manner. This divine exposition caused jealousy in the teacher, who plotted to harm his disciple.

Feigning a pilgrimage to the Gaṅgā, Yādava Prakāśa planned to kill Rāmānuja during the journey. However, by divine intervention, Śrī Govinda Bhaṭṭar discovered the plot and warned him. As Rāmānuja fled, *Śrī Varadarāja Perumāḷ* and *Śrī Perundevi Thāyār* appeared as a hunter couple and personally guided him back to Kāñchīpuram.

#### The Divine Mission Begins
Around this time, *Śrī Āḷavandār* visited Kāñchīpuram, beheld the youthful radiance of Rāmānuja, and prayed to *Śrī Varadarāja Perumāḷ* that this extraordinary soul should carry forward the torch of *Śrī Vaiṣṇava siddhānta*.

Soon thereafter, a miraculous incident demonstrated Rāmānuja’s divine power. The king’s daughter, possessed by a *brahma-rākṣasa*, was cured instantly when Rāmānuja commanded the spirit to leave her body.

At the Lord’s behest, conveyed through *Śrī Thirukacchi Nambi*, Rāmānuja began performing *tīrtha kainkaryam* (fetching holy water for temple rituals) at *Śrī Varadarāja Perumāḷ Koil*. Meanwhile, *Śrī Periya Nambi*, a direct disciple of *Śrī Āḷavandār*, was sent to Kāñchīpuram to bring Rāmānuja to Śrīraṅgam.

When they arrived, *Śrī Āḷavandār* had just attained *paramapadam*. Upon seeing his body with three fingers folded, Rāmānuja vowed to fulfill three tasks:
* To write a commentary on the *Brahma Sūtras* (*Śrī Bhāṣya*),
* To write a commentary on the *Vishnu Purāṇa*, and
* To write an exposition on *Śrī Nammāzhvār’s Tiruvāymoḻi*.

As soon as he made these vows, the three fingers of *Śrī Āḷavandār* miraculously unfolded — confirming divine acceptance.

#### Receiving Divine Guidance
After returning to Kāñchīpuram, Rāmānuja sought guidance from *Śrī Varadarāja Perumāḷ* through *Śrī Thirukacchi Nambi*. The Lord graciously conveyed six divine instructions (*ārū vākyāni*), forming the foundation of *Śrī Viśiṣṭādvaita Vedānta* and Rāmānuja’s *Śrī Bhāṣya*. Among them was the command to seek initiation (*pañca-saṁskāra*) from *Śrī Periya Nambi*.

Accordingly, Rāmānuja received *pañca-saṁskāra* at the *Śrī Kodanda Rāma Temple* in *Madurantakam* — the formal initiation marking his entry into the *Śrī Vaiṣṇava sampradāya*.

Later, realizing that spiritual progress requires absolute humility and service-mindedness, Rāmānuja entered *sannyāsa āśrama*. Lord Varadarāja Himself conferred upon him the title **“Yatirāja”**, meaning *King among Ascetics*.

#### Udayavar and the Establishment of the Sampradāya
When Rāmānuja visited Śrīraṅgam, *Periya Perumāḷ Śrī Raṅganātha* personally blessed him and entrusted him with the management of temple affairs, bestowing upon him the title **“Udayavar”**.

Later, he approached *Śrī Thirukkōṣṭiyūr Nambi* to learn the meanings of the three *rahasya mantras* — the *Aṣṭākṣara (Mūla Mantra)*, *Dvaya Mantra*, and *Charama Śloka*. To test his devotion and humility, *Śrī Nambi* turned him away seventeen times before finally instructing him.

Having received this sacred knowledge, Rāmānuja, moved by boundless compassion, ascended the temple tower and proclaimed the mantras openly to all, declaring that if by his act others could attain liberation, he would gladly bear the consequences himself. Seeing this supreme compassion, *Śrī Thirukkōṣṭiyūr Nambi* embraced him, calling him **“Emperumānār”** — “the one who embodies the Lord’s own mercy.”

#### Spreading the Light of Śrī Vaiṣṇavism
Rāmānuja’s fame spread across Bhārata-varṣa. Kings, scholars, and devotees came to surrender at his feet. He traveled extensively, re-establishing ancient temples and revitalizing the *sampradāya*.

At *Melukote (Tirunarayanapuram)* in Karnataka, he rediscovered the ancient deity of *Śrī Sampathkumāra (Chellappillai)* and re-established worship there. He also initiated countless devotees, transcending barriers of caste, birth, and background — demonstrating that true worth lies in *śaraṇāgati* (surrender) and *bhakti* to the Lord.

Rāmānuja authored several monumental works, including:
* **Śrī Bhāṣya** – commentary on the *Brahma Sūtras*,
* **Vedānta Sāra** and **Vedānta Dīpa**,
* **Gītā Bhāṣya** (commentary on the *Bhagavad Gītā*),
* **Nitya Grantha** (manual for daily worship), and
* **Gadya Traya** (Śaraṇāgati, Śrīraṅga, and Vaikuṇṭha Gadyams), among others.

#### Establishment of the 74 Simhāsanādhipatis
To ensure the preservation and propagation of the *Śrī Vaiṣṇava siddhānta*, Śrī Rāmānuja established **seventy-four Simhāsanādhipatis** — distinguished disciples entrusted with teaching, guiding, and sustaining the *sampradāya* across regions. Each Simhāsanādhipati was empowered to initiate disciples through *pañca-saṁskāra*, thus making the *siddhānta* accessible to all earnest seekers.

#### Final Years and Attainment of Paramapadam
In his later years, Śrī Rāmānuja continued to guide his disciples from Śrīraṅgam. Having completed his mission of re-establishing the Lord’s supremacy and restoring the path of *prapatti* (total surrender), he attained *paramapadam* at the age of 120.

Before ascending to the Lord’s abode, he assured his disciples that his divine presence would remain eternally at Śrīraṅgam for the upliftment of devotees. To this day, his *thirumeni* (divine body) is preserved and worshipped at *Śrīraṅgam*, radiating spiritual power and grace.

**Legacy:**  
Śrī Bhagavad Rāmānujāchārya stands eternally as the embodiment of compassion, humility, and divine wisdom. He is rightly hailed as:  
**_“Yatirājaḥ Śaraṇam Mama” — Śrī Rāmānujāchārya, the King of Ascetics, is my sole refuge._**
