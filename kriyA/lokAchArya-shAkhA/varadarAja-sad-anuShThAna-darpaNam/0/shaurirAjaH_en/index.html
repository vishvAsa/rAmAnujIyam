PREFACE

This Work is an exposition of the validity of Sadanushthana being a Collection and Collation of Vedic and Scriptural authorities for Shrivaishnava Ethics and Discipline, by an eminent and revered Acharya, written about a Century ago.+++(5)+++

The publication of this valuable work, which seeks to provide the the Vedic, Shastraic and Sampradaya basis of validity and authenticity to the various aspects of Rites, Rituals, Conduct and religious practices of Shrivaishnavas, has been undertaken as a devout offering by a Sishya at the Holy Feet of his Acharya. The work is of particular interest to Shrivaishnavas (Ramanujeeyas) generally and to those of the Southern Denomination (Thennacharya) specially.

The Vedas which are the timeless repository of all valid and worthwhile knowledge, made available to mankind by Eswara in His boundless grace and benevolence, are the ultimate fountain-head, a source book of reference for correct practices, Rites, Conduct and discipline, a compendium of Do’s and Don’ts for one and all. The practices, Ethics and Code of Conduct of all Astika Sampradayas derive their validity and authenticity only from the Vedas. The Shastras, the Smritis, and large volumes of Sampradaya works only seek to elucidate and explicate what the Vedas expound and ordain, and the Vedas are acknowledged as the first and the foremost Shastra.

Says the Gitacharya, the Preceptor-Supreme in the 16th Chapter of Bhagavadgita:

तस्मात् शास्त्रं प्रमाणं ते
कार्याकार्यव्यवस्थितौ ।
ज्ञात्वाशास्त्रविधानेन
कर्म कर्तुमिहार्हसि ॥”

We have also the well-known aphorism of Apasthambha:—

“ धर्मज्ञसमयः प्रमाणं वेदाश्च "" [[ii]]

It is from the vast and limitless expanse of the Ocean of Vedic knowledge the various classes of people (Categorised as सात्त्विक, राजस and तामस on the basis of the predominance of the three gunas, स्वार्थप्रपन्न and परार्थप्रपन्न according to their aptitudes, and levels of mental, moral and spiritual maturity attained by them) derive the appropriate Directives and prescriptions applicable to them on the basis of which the Code of Discipline and Daily Religious Practices, Conduct, Routine governing their lives from womb to tomb is formulated and followed.

The Acharya (or Guru) occupies a very important and essential place in all Sampradayas and his role is to guide and direct the disciples on the right god-bound path to salvation. It is the Acharya, who, by both Example and Precept, guides the seeker of Salvation by dispelling his doubts and indicating the right approach, bringing out the inner, esoteric meaning and significance of the Shastras. Shriman Narayana is the first and foremost Acharya in the Guruparampara line of all denominations. It is therefore imperative that any one seeking salvation, should first seek and select an Acharya, who by his intellectual attainments, spiritual discipline and personal Conduct, is qualified and competent to help him in his spiritual pursuit to attain the goal of reaching the ineffable Lotus Feet of Shriman Narayana.

The role of Acharya has been set-out by Shri Vedantacharya in his inimitable style in the following verse which lists the qualities and qualifications of an Acharya :- [[iii]]

“सिद्धH सत्संप्रदाये स्थिरधियमनघं श्रोत्रियं ब्रह्मनिष्ठं
सत्त्वस्थ सत्यवाचं समयनियतया साधुवृत्त्या समेतम् ।
डंभासूयादिमुक्तं जितविषयगणं दीर्घवन्धुं दयालुं
स्खालित्ये शासितारं स्वपरहितपरं देशिकं भूष्णुरीसेत् ॥

It To Shrivaishnavas Shri Parankusha or Nammazh war, who comes at the summit of the hierarchy of Prapannas, is the supreme example and model of perfection for god- consciousness, practices, right conduct and discipline. was He, who, in his Infinite Grace and Humanity revealed and communicated to Shriman Nathamunigal (in his Yogic transcendence) both the knowledge practices and code of conduct essential and appropriate for Shri- vaishnavas, in the form of Mantra Ratna and the Shri- sooktis of Azhwars as also Shrivaishnava Sadanushthanam. This invaluable Treasure of knowledge and conduct ( and gra) has been zealously preserved, promoted and propagated, and passed on to us by successive generations of Acharyas from Shriman Nathamuni through Yamuna, Shri Ramanuja, Shri Vedantacharya, Shri Varavaramuni, Shri Koil Annan, and so on down to Shri Shuddha Sattwam Swami in unbroken line of Acharya Parampara. in the in- Of Course, there have been certain deviations variations in the scheme of Anushthanams, tervening period. His Holiness Shri Embar Jeeyar Swami, in the direct line of Shri Vaishnava Acharyas, was reputed for his matchless scholarship and erudition in the Shastras and the Sanskrit and the Dravidian Vedantic lore also the essence of the Bhagavadvishaya. An exemplar by the qualities of his head and heart, and selfless spiritual dedication to Divine Service, he was acknowledged as iv as a re-incarnation of Shri Ramanuja himself. At Shriperumbudur, (the birth place of Shri Ramanuja) he attracted streams of scholars and Sishyas from far and near to sit at his feet and quench their thirst for Spiritual knowledge from the elevating and enlightening fountain of his knowledge and personal conduct. Shri Embar Jeeyar Swami and several of his devoted disciples wrote a number of works on the various aspects of the teachings and philosophy of Shri Ramanuja and of the proper Shrivaishnava Code of Ethics and Discipline. The distinguished author of the present work Sadanushthana Darpanam comes in the direct line of and had the good Sishyas of Embar Jeeyar Swami, fortune to imbibe the rich esoteric tradition from his Acharyas and to bequeath to posterity a number of works.

A word about the author’s lineage will be apposite at this stage: He belonged to the illustrious Shuddha Sattwam tradition, which had had the proud privilege of Physical personal link with none other than Shri Ramanuja himself, and had also the rare benefit of his personal initiation; having been referred to as Elaya Villi; and earned the rare good fortune of being the recipient of the blessing of Shri Manavalamamuni, as the foremost among the five dazzling jewels (Pancha Ratnas) deputed by him.

Sadanushthana Darpanam is a work of this great Acharya, a product of his competence and devotion, which in turn, bears the stamp of the ancient, authentic tradition, flowing from no less a spiritual preceptor than Shri Parankusha.

The contents of this valuable work reflect the multifarious aspects of Shri Vaishnava Ethics and Discipline as enunciated and exemplified by Shri Nammazhwar.

The work was compiled and composed in the Chittrai month of Vishu Year, a hundred years ago; it was in palm- leaf manuscript, referred to at Kalakshepams.+++(4)+++ It enunciates and elucidates the authentic Vedic and Scriptural basis of Shri Vaishnava Anushthanams like Oordhwapundra Dharanam, the requisite qualifications for eligibility for initiation to Ashtakshara Mantra, Shripada Theertham, the observance of various Holy Jayantis, Ekadasi Vratham and the recitation of Azhwars’ Divya Prabandhams.

The Publication of this unique work has been undertaken by me at the instance of well-meaning elders, and the first volume (instalment) (f) was brought out in Tamil and Sanskrit in the year Sadharana (12 years ago). The next volume containing chapters 1 to 7 in Sanskrit came out in Pingala (1977).

The current instalment, the Third volume, containing 23 chapters (except chapters 8 and 10) are being published in Sanskrit. These chapters cover very interesting themes relating to Shri Vaishnava Religion and Ethics, which are explained with apt and appropriate references to the Vedas, the Smritis, the Puranas, Bhagavad Vishayam, the Rahasyas and Purva Meemamsa. Particular mention may be made of the fact that illuminating excerpts from the works of Shri Vedantacharya are presented to indicate the validity of several Shri Vaishnava Practices and observances.

Please permit me to indicate briefly that these Chapters advert to among other things: vi

  1. the Southern Tradition of Single Pranamam for which authority is adduced from Shri Vedantacharya himself;
  2. the validity of initiation of only the eight syllabled (Ashtakshara) Mantra (including the Pranava in it) as authenticated by Scriptures;
  3. the prohibition of Shri Vaishnavas performing पितृश्राध on Ekadasi Vrata Day, as supported by citations from हरिदिन तिलक and तत्त्वमुक्ताकलाप of Shri Vedanta Desika;
  4. the Tenkalai Practice of serving rice along with vegetables and other dishes (already offered to God) before Parishechanam and Pranahuti; (as sanctified by references in Rukmini Kalyanam Chapter of Shri Bhagavata Purana);
  5. the validity of Pongal Paranai at Sun Rise during Dhanurmasam;
  6. the Rule regarding Paranai during Shri Jayanti Vratam; and
  7. the propriety of acceptance of Shri Pada Theertham by one and all in Goshtis; as authorised by Poorva Meemamsa.

The author of this work has earned fame for his doubts, as clarity of thought and capacity to dispel doubts, evidenced by his panegyric

“वन्दे समस्तशास्त्रार्थ-
सन्देहच्छेदिनं शुभम् ।
वरदं वावदूकानां
भयदं गुरुपुंगवम् ॥”

His disciples among whom may be mentioned Shri Sadhu Ramanujacharya Swami who later became [[vii]] the Senior Pontiff of the Shri Ramanuja Math at Tirumalai, have been praiseworthy exemplars of correct Shrivaishnava Anushthanam and this should further enhance the value of this work.

Any defects or errors which might have crept in the publication are to be ascribed only to the oversight and over-enthusiasm of the publisher.

The Publisher humbly appeals to Shrivaishnavas to benefit by this work and qualify themselves to be recipients of Shriman Narayana’s Infinite Grace.

One last word: The publisher proposes to edit and bring out the remaining two chapters, and also a Tamil rendering of the entire work, and appeals to Astikas for liberal financial assistance.
