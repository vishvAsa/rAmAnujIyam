विशिष्टाद्वैत सिद्धात हागु श्रीवैष्णव संप्रदयद उत्तम विद्वान् हागु श्रीमन्नाथमुनिगळ पौत्रराद(मॊम्मगराद) आळवंदार् प्राप्य (होंदबेकाद विषय/ गुरि) हागु प्रापकगळ (अदर उपाय/साधन) स्वरूपवन्नु द्वयमहामंत्रद विस्तर विवरणॆय रूपदल्लि तम्म स्तोत्र रत्नदल्लि प्रकाशिसिद्दारॆ. नमगॆ लभ्यवाद पूर्वाचार्यर संस्कृत स्तोत्र ग्रंथगळल्लि इदु पुरातनतमवादद्दु.

पॆरिय नंबिगळु इळैयाऴ्वारन्नु(श्री रामानुजरन्नु) आळवंदारिन शिष्यरन्नागि माडिसलु कांचीपुरक्कॆ हॊरटिद्दरु. तिरुक्कच्चि नंबिगळ उपदेशदंतॆ देवप् पेरुमाळिन कैंकर्यक्कागि साळैक्किणऱिंद(ऒंदु बावि) तीर्थवन्नु तरुतिद्दरु. पॆरिय नंबिगळ स्तोत्र रत्नदिंद पठनवु इळैयाऴ्वारन्नु तुंबा अकर्शिसितु हागु अवरन्नु नम्म संप्रदायक्कॆ तंदितु. ऎम्पॆरुमानारॆंदु प्रसिद्धराद इळैयाऴ्वारिगॆ ई स्तोत्रवु तुंबा प्रियवागित्तु हागु इदर हलवारु भागगळन्नु तम्म श्रीवैकुंठगद्यदल्लु आकरिसिद्दारॆ

पॆरियवाच्चान् पिळ्ळै ई दिव्यवाद प्रबंधक्कॆ विस्तरवाद व्याख्यानवन्नु रचिसिद्दारॆ. दुर्ग्राह्यवाद स्तोत्रगळिगॆ सुव्यक्तवागि अर्थगळन्नु तोरिद्दारॆ. श्लोकगळिगॆ ई व्याख्यानवन्नु अनुसरि सरळ अर्थवन्नु नोडुतिद्देवॆ.

[स्तोत्र रत्न](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/index.php/2021/08/sthothra-rathnam-kannada-simple/)

***स्वादयन्निह सर्वेषाम् त्रय्यन्तार्तम् सुदुर्ग्रहम् ।  
स्तोत्र यामास योगीन्द्रः तम् वन्दे यामुनाह्वयम् ॥***

अडियेन् अरियलु कठिनवाद वेदान्त तत्त्वगळन्नु ऎल्लरिंदलू सुग्राह्यवागि(ग्रहिसलु सुलभवाद रीतियल्लि) स्तोत्र रूपदल्लि प्रकाशिसिद योगिगळल्लि उत्तमराद आळवंदारन्नु प्रार्थिसुत्तेनॆ.

***नमो नमो यामुनाय यामुनाय नमो नमः ।  
नमो नमो यामुनाय यामुनाय नमो नमः ॥***

अडियेन् आळवंदारन्नु पुनःपुनः प्रार्थिसुत्तेनॆ, अवरिगॆ नामस्करिसलागदिरलु अडियेनिंद साद्यविल्ल.


## स्तोत्र रत्न – सरळ विवरणॆ – श्लोक 1-10
*श्री- श्रीमते शठकोपाय नम- श्रीमते रामानुजाय नम- श्रीमद् वरवरमुनये नम-*

[स्तोत्र रत्न](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/index.php/2021/08/sthothra-rathnam-kannada-simple/)

***श्लोक 1*** – मॊदल श्लोकदल्लि आळवंदार् यतार्थवाद धनवाद ज्ञान-वैराग्यगळल्लि नाथमुनिगळ उत्कर्षवन्नु नमस्करिसुत्तारॆ.  
***नमोचिन्त्याद्भुदाक्लिष्ट ज्ञान वैराग्यराशये ।  
नाथाय मुनयेगाधभगवद् भक्तिसिन्दवे ॥***  
अडियेन्, (मनस्सिन) आलोचनॆगॆ मीरिद , अद्भुतवाद ज्ञान हागु वैराग्यवन्नु सुलभवागि (भगवदनुग्रहदिंद) होंदिद्दवरु हागु भगवंतनन्ने ध्यानिसुववरु, हागु भगवद्भक्तिय आर्णववागिरुव नाथमुनिगळिगॆ नमस्करिसुत्तेनॆ.

***श्लोक 2 –*** ई श्लोकदल्लि भगवदवतारद बग्गॆ नाथमुनिगळ ज्ञानद वैभवतमत्ववन्नु विवरिसल्पडुत्तिदॆ अथवा “(हिंदिन श्लोकदल्लि विवरिसल्पट्ट) अवर ज्ञानादिगळु अवरल्लि निल्लदल्लदॆ तम्मवरॆगु (आळवंदार्) बरुत्तिदॆ” ऎंब (अर्थवन्नु) विवरिसुत्तिदॆ.  
तस्मै नमो मदुजिदङ्घ्रि सरोज तत्व  
ज्ञानानुराग महिमातिशयान्तसीम्ने ।  
नाथाय नाथमुनये***त्र परत्र चापि  
नित्यम् यदीय चरणौ शरणम् मदीयम् ॥***  
मधुवन्नु संहरिसिद ऎम्पॆरुमानिन पादकममलगळल्लि यथार्थ ज्ञान हागु भक्तिय महिमॆय माहात्म्यॆय अंतवादवराद श्रीमन्नाथमुनिगळ दिव्य चरणगळे ननगॆ ई लोकदल्लु हागु बेरियदाद (श्रीवैकुंठदल्लि) ननगॆ ऎंदिगू शरणु.

***श्लोक 3*** – दाहदिंड्गिरुववनु हॆच्चिन गात्रदल्लि नीरन्नु कुडियुत्तिद्दरू तीरदंतह दाहशण्तॆ आळवंदार्, “पुनःपुनः नानु अवर दासनु” ऎंदु हेळुतिद्दारॆ.  
***भूयो नमोपरिमिताच्युत भक्ति तत्व  
ज्ञानामृताब्दि परिवाह शुबैर् वचोपि- ।  
लोकेवतीर्ण परमार्थ समग्र भक्ति  
योगाय नाथमुनये यमिनाम् वराय ॥***  
अपरिमितवाद भगवद्भक्तियु हागु तत्वज्ञानद सागरदिंद प्रवहसुत्तिरुव मंगळ श्रीसूक्तिगळ(वचनगळ) रूपदल्लि अवतरिसिदवरु, परमप्रप्यरु, पूर्णरु, भक्तियोगवन्नु होंदिरुववरु हागु योगिगळल्लि शेष्टराद नाथमुनिगळिगॆ पुनः नमस्करिसुत्तेनॆ.

***श्लोक 4*** – श्रीविष्णु पुराणवन्नु (कॊडुगॆयागि अनुग्रहिसिदरिंद) श्री पराशर भगवानन्नु नमस्करिसुत्तारॆ.  
***तत्वेन यश्चिदचीदीश्वर तत् स्वभाव  
भोगापवर्ग तदुपायगतीरुदारः ।  
सन्दर्शयन् निरमिमीत पुराणरत्नम्  
तस्मै नमो मुनिवराय पराशराय ॥***  
विशदवागि (स्पष्टवागि) चित्(चेतन/ज्ञानवन्नु होंदिरुव द्रव्य), अचित्(अचेतन/ ज्ञान रहितवाद द्रव्य) हागु ईश्वरनन्नु (भगवान्), अवुगळ स्वरूपवन्नु(याथात्म्यवन्नु), (ऐहलोकिक) भोगगळन्नु, मोक्षवन्नु, ऐहलोकिक भोगगळिगॆ हागु मोक्षक्कॆ साधनॆगळन्नु जीवात्मनिंद प्राप्यवन्नु यथार्थवागि (सत्यवागि) विवरिसुवंतः पुराणगळल्लि रत्नवाद (उत्तमवादंतः) श्रीविष्णु पुराणवन्नु अनुग्रहिसिद उदारराद ऋषिश्रेष्टराद पराशर ऋषिगॆ नन्न नम्सकारगळु.

श्रीमते रामानुजाय नमः

**श्लोक-5** – आळवंदार् नम्माऴ्वारिन दिव्य पादगळिगॆ बिद्दु अवरल्लि शरणागुत्तारॆ.  
***माता पिता युवतयस् तनया विभूतिः  
सर्वम् यदेव नियमेन मदन्वयानाम् ।  
आद्यस्य नः कुलपतेर् वगुळाभिरामम्  
श्रीमत् तदङ्घ्रियुगळम् प्रणमामि मूर्द्ना ॥***  
तायि ,तंदॆ , युवति(पत्नि) पुत्र, संपत्तु हागु (उल्लिखितवल्लद) मत्तॆल्लवु (ननगू) नन्न संततिगू नम्माऴ्वारिन दिव्य पादगळे. नम्म (श्रीवैष्णव) कुलद नाथरु श्रीवैष्णवश्री(कैंकर्यश्री)युक्तरु वकुळपुष्पगळिंद अलंकृतराद आऴ्वारिन दिव्यपादगळिगॆ शिरसा नमस्करिसुत्तेनॆ.

***श्लोक-6*** – “वै*गुन्तनागप् पुगऴ*” (7.9.7.तिरुवाय्मॊऴियल्लि ऎम्पॆरुमान् आऴ्वारन्नु श्रीवैकुंठनाथनागि स्तुतिसुवंतॆ माडुत्तिद्दानॆ) हेळिदंतॆ, तनगु(ईश्वरनिगॆ) स्तवगळु प्रियवादरिंद हागु आचार्यरिगू भगवद्-स्तुति प्रियवादरिंदलु, उपाय-उपेयगळन्नु विवरिसुत्ता स्तुतिसुव इच्छॆयिंद अरंबिसुत्तारॆ.

***यन्मूर्द्नि मे श्रुतिशिरस्सु च भाति यस्मिन्  
अस्मन् मनोरतपदस् सकलस्समेति ।  
स्तोश्यामि नः कुलदनम् कुलदैवतम् तत्  
पादारविन्दम् अरविन्दविलोचनस्य ॥***  
वेदांतदल्लू नन्न शिरस्सिनल्लु प्राकाशिसुत्तिरुव नम्म कुलधनवु (अलंबिसल्तक्कदाद) कुलदेवतॆयाद पुण्डरीकाक्षन याव दिव्य पादपंकजगळल्लि नम्म प्रवहसुत्तिरुव भक्तियु सुंदरवागि सेरुवुदो अंतः श्रीपादगळन्नु नानु स्तुतिसुवॆ.

***श्लोक-7*** अर्जुननु “विस्रुज्य सशरम्  
चापम्” (धनुशन्नु बाणगळॊंदिगॆ कॆळगॆ बिट्टनु) श्री भगवद्गीते 1.47रल्लि हेळिदंतॆ हॊरट युध्दवन्नु त्यजिसिदंतॆ आळवंदार् (स्तुतिसुव) विवरिसुत्तारॆ

***तत्वेन यस्य महिमार्णवशीकराणु-  
शक्यो न मातुमपि शर्वपितामहाद्यैः ।  
कर्तुम् तदीयमहिमस्तुतिमुद्यताय  
मह्यम् नमो£स्तु कवये निरपत्रपाय ॥***  
भगवद्वैभवद आर्णवदिंद शिव ब्रह्मादिगळिंद अणुमात्रवू अरियलु असाध्यवाद ऎम्पॆरुमानिन महिमॆयन्नु निर्लज्जनागि कवियॆंदु करॆदुकॊंडु स्तुतिसलु हॊरट नन्ननु नाने ( ई परिहास्य कार्यक्कॆ) नमस्करिसुत्तेनॆ.

***श्लोक 8 –*** अर्जुननन्नु ईश्वरनु “करिश्ये वचनम् तव”(नीनु हेळिदंतॆ युध्दवन्नु माडुत्तेनॆ) श्री भगवद्गीते 18.73 ऎंदु हेळुवंतॆ माडिदंतॆ, वाक्किन(बायिन) प्रयोजनवु तन्नन्नु स्तुतिसुवुदे ऎंदू , श्रीविष्णुसहस्रनामदल्लि “स्तव्यः स्तवप्रियः” (स्तुतिसलु तक्कवनु हागु स्तुतियिंद प्रियनागुववनु) ऎंदु हेळिदंतॆ स्तुतियु ननगॆ प्रियवादद्दु ऎंदु प्रॆरिसलु, आळवंदार् स्तुतिसलु निश्चयिसुत्तारॆ.  
***यद्वा श्रमावदि यतामति वाप्यशक्तः  
स्तौम्येवमेव कलु ते£पि सदा स्तुवंतः ।  
वेदाश्चतुर्मुख मुखाश्च महार्णवान्त-  
को मज्जतोरणुकुलाचलयोर्विशेष- ॥***  
भगवद्वैभवद आर्णवदिंद शिव ब्रह्मादिगळिंद अणुमात्रवू अरियलु असाध्यवाद ऎम्पॆरुमानिन महिमॆयन्नु निर्लज्जनागि कवियॆंदु करॆदुकॊंडु स्तुतिसलु हॊरट नन्ननु नानॆ ( ई परिहास्य कार्यक्कॆ) नमस्करिसुत्तेनॆ

श्लोक 9 – ईग आळवंदार् ब्रह्मादिगळिगिंतलु स्तुतिसलु अर्हरॆंदु हेळुतिद्दारॆ.  
***किञ्चैश शक्त्यतिशयेन न तेध्नुकम्प्यः  
स्तोतापि तु स्तुति कृतेन परिश्रमेण ।  
तत्र श्रमस्तु सुलभो मम मन्दबुद्देः  
इत्युद्यमोयमुचितो मम चाप्जनेत्र ॥***  
इदल्लदॆ, नानु निन्नन्नु स्तुतिसलु शक्तनॆंब वैभवदिंद निन्न अनुकंपवन्नु वर्षिसलु अन्हर्हनु, स्तुतिसुव कृत्यदल्लि परिश्रमदिंद निन्न अनुकंपावर्षक्कॆ तक्कवनु, इदरल्लि नन्न मंदबुध्दियिंद सुलभवागि श्रमगोळ्ळुव ननगॆ (स्तुतिरूपदल्लिऎउव) ई प्रयत्नवु (ब्रह्मादिगळन्नु होलिसिदरॆ) उचितवे (प्राप्तवे) आगिदॆ.

***श्लोक 10 –*** भगवद्स्तुतियिंद हिंजेरि, स्तुतिसलु निर्णयिसि, मुंदिन ऐदुश्लोकगळल्लि शरण्यनागिमाडुव परत्ववन्नु विवरिसुतिद्दारॆ. इदन्नु तम्म कृपॆयिंद कारणवाक्यगळिंद भगवद्परत्ववन्नु विवरिसुतिद्दारॆ.  
***नावेक्शसे यदि ततो भुवनान्यमूनि  
नालम् प्रभो भवितुमेव कुतः प्रवृउत्तिः ।  
एवम् निसर्ग सुहृउदि त्वयि सर्वजन्तोः  
स्वामिन् न चित्रम् इदम् आश्रित वत्सलत्वम् ॥***

***श्लोक- 21 –*** आळवंदार् शरण्यनाद भगवद्वैभववन्नु ध्यानिसुतिद्दारॆ. हीगल्लदॆ हिंदॆ प्राप्य स्वरूपवन्नु विवरिसिदरु, इल्लि अदन्नु अर्थिसुत्तिरुववन स्वरूप विवरिसल्पडुत्तिदॆ, हीगल्लदॆ हिंदॆ भगवद् स्वरूपवु विवरिसिदमेलॆ, इल्लि नंतर विवरिसल्पडुव शरणागतिय स्वरूपवन्नु तोरुतिद्दारॆ ऎंदु विवक्षित वागिदॆ.  
***नमो नमो वाङ्ग्मनसातिभूमये  
नमो नमो वाङ्ग्मनसैकभूमये ।  
नमो नमोऽनन्तमहाविभूतये  
नमो नमोऽनन्तदयैकसिन्दवे ॥***  
(स्वप्रयत्नदिंद निन्नन्नु तिळियलु प्रयत्निसुववर) मनस्सु हागु वाक्किगॆ अतीतनाद निनगॆ नमस्कारगळु. (निन्न अनुग्रहदिंद निन्नन्नु तिळिदवरिगॆ) वाक् हागु मनस्सिनिंदलॆ अनंतवाद महदैश्वर्ययुक्तनाद निनगॆ नमस्कारगळु; दयॆय सागरनाद निनगॆ नमस्कारगळु.

  
***श्लोक- 22*** – आळवंदार् तम्म अधिकारवन्नु घोषिसि (हिंदिन श्लोकदल्लि बंद) प्रपत्तियन्नु आचरिसुतिद्दारॆ. हिंदॆ विवरिसल्पट्ट उपेयक्कॆ तक्क उपयद आचरणॆयॆंदू, ई श्लोकवन्नु विवरिसबहुदु.  
***न दर्मनिश्ठोऽस्मि न चात्मवेदी  
न भक्तिमान् त्वचरणारविंदे ।  
आकिंचन्योन्यगति- शरण्य!  
त्वत्पादमूलम् शरणम् प्रपद्ये ॥***  
शरण्यनाद भगवंतने कर्मयोगदल्लि नानु स्थिरनल्ल, आत्मज्ञानवु नन्नल्लि इल्ल, निन्न चरणारविंदगळल्लि भक्तियू इल्लदवनाद नानु, बेरोंदु उपायविल्लदॆ हागलि बेरॊंदु शरणवु(गति) इल्लदॆ निन्न पादपंकजगळन्नॆ उपायवागि स्विकरिसुत्तेनॆ.

  
***श्लोक 23*** – भगवान् , “नन्न विषयदल्लि याव आनुकूल्यविल्लदिद्दरु , निम्मल्लि अर्जित आनुकूल्यवन्नु इल्लदिद्दरु , नानु निम्मल्लि गुणगळन्नु तंदु उपेयवन्नू नीडुवॆनु.” ऎंदु हेळिदरु. आळवंदार्, “नानु पूर्णवागि दोशगळिंदलॆ भरितनागिद्देनॆ.  
***न निन्दितम् कर्म तदस्ति लोके  
सहस्रशो यन्न मया व्यदायि ।  
सोऽहम् विपाकावसरे मुकुन्द!  
क्रन्दामि सम्प्रत्यगतिस् तवाग्रे ॥***  
मोक्षवन्नु प्रसादिसुव भगवंतने ! साविरबारि निन्निंद माडद निशिद्ध कर्मगळु शास्त्रशल्लु इल्ल \[(शास्त्रवन्नु) लोक(ऎंदिद्दु) लोकवन्नु निर्वहुसुवुदरिंद\] , ईग (आ कर्मगळु) विपाक( पक्व) दशॆयल्लिरुवाग बेरोंदु गतियिल्लदॆ निम्म मुंदॆ अळुतिद्दॆनॆ.

**श्लोक- 24** – आळवंदार् ,”निन्न कृपॆये ननगॆ शरणु” ऎंदु ऎंबॆरुमानन्नु कुरितु कूगुतिद्दारॆ. आळवंदार्, “निन्नन्नु हॊंदुवुदु ननगॆ हेगॆ महाभाग्यवो हागॆये निन्न कृपॆगु नानु सूक्त पात्रनु.  
***निमज्जतोऽनन्तभवार्णवान्तः  
चिराय मे कूलमिवाशि लब्दः ।  
त्वयापि लब्दम् भवगवन्निदानीम्  
अनुत्तमम् पात्रमिदम् दयायाः ॥***  
अनंतनॆ! ( देशदिंदागलि कालदिंदागलि वस्तुविंदागलि परिच्छॆद रहितनु) दीर्घ कालदिंद संसारार्णवदल्लि सम्मग्ननाद (चॆन्नागि मुळुगिद) ननगॆ तीरवागि बंदिद्दीय. ओ भगवान्! निन्न कृपॆगू सूक्त पात्रनागिद्देनॆ.

***श्लोक- 25 –*** “नन्नल्लि निम्म भारवॆल्लवू समर्पिसिदमेलॆ श्री रामायणद सुंदर खंडदल्लि ‘तत् तस्य सदृशम् भवेत्’ (9.30 तन्न स्वरूपक्कॆ प्राप्तवादद्दु- ताने लंकॆयन्नु नशिसि नन्ननु रक्षिसुवुदु – आदरिंद अवन आगमदवरॆगु कायुवॆनु)’ ऎंदतॆ इरुवुदल्लदॆ ईगले रक्षिसलु एकॆ निर्बंदिसुतिद्दीर”, ऎंदु भगवान् केळिदाग. आळवंदार्, “नानु नन्न धुःखवन्नु दूरमाडलु हेळुतिल्ल; निन्न भक्तरु ई लोकदल्लि धुःखिसुवुदु निन्न महिमॆगे कळॆ.  
***अभूतपूर्वम् मम भावि किम् वा  
सर्वम् सहे मे सहजम् हि धुःखम् ।  
किन्तु त्वदग्रे शरणागतानाम्  
पराभवो नाथ! न ते\$नुरूप- ॥***  
हे नाथ! पूर्ववुंटागद याव होस धुःखवु ननगॆ संभविसदु; ई धुःखगळॆल्लवन्नु सहिसुतिद्देनॆ, इवु(धुःखगळु) ननगॆ सहजवागि (नन्नोंदिगे हुट्टिदंतहदु) बंदंतहदु, आदरॆ निन्न मुंदॆ निन्न शरणागतर तिरस्कारवु निनगॆ अनुरूपवादद्दल्ल

***श्लोक- 26*** – आळवंदार्, “निन्न महिमॆगॆ कळंकवॆंदु नन्ननु निराकरिसिदरु निन्नन्नु नानु बिडनु.” ऎंदु हागु तम्म अगतित्वदिंद बंदिरुव महा विश्वासवन्नु व्यक्तपडिसुत्तारॆ.  
***निरासकस्यापि न तावदुत्सहे  
महेश! हातुम् तव पादपंकजं*** ***।  
रुशा निरस्तोऽपि शिसुः स्थनंधयो  
न जातु मातुश्चरणौ जिहासति ॥***  
ओ सर्वेश्वरनॆ! ननु (निन्निंद) तळ्ळल्पट्टरु, निन्न पदारविंदगळन्नु ऎंदू बिडनु ; स्थन्यपान माडुव मगुवु (तन्न तायियिंद) कोपदिंद तळ्ळल्पट्टरु, अदु तन्न तायिय पादगळन्नु ऎंदू बिडदु.

***श्लोक- 27*** – आळवंदार्, “निन्नन्नु बिडदॆ इरलु कारणवु केवल अनन्यगितित्ववे ? निन्न भोग्यतॆयल्लि मुळुगिरुव नन्न मनस्सु बेरॊंदन्नु अर्थिसदु.  
***तवामृतस्यन्दिनि पादपङ्कजे  
निवेशितात्मा कथमन्यदिच्छति? ।  
स्थित्तेऽरविन्दे मकरंदनिर्भरे  
मदुव्रतो नेक्षुरकं हि वीक्शते ॥***  
अम्रृतवन्नु स्रविसुत्तिरुव निन्न पादारविंदगळल्लि समर्पिसल्पट्ट नन्न मनस्सु हेगॆ इतर विषयगळन्नु इच्छिसुवुदु ? मधुविनिंद भरितवाद कॆंदावरॆ इदल्लि ऒंदु जेनु कोळवळिकॆयन्नु (ऒंदु क्षुद्र पुष्पवु) नोडुवुदो.

***श्लोक- 28*** – आळवंदार्, “ननगॆ उपकरिसलु अंजलियोंदॆ पर्याप्तवल्लवॆ ?”, ऎंदु केळुतिद्दारॆ.  
***त्वदंग्रिंमुद्दिश्य कदाऽपि केनचित्  
यथा तथा वाऽपि सकृत्कृतोऽञ्जलि ।  
तदैव मुष्णात्यशुभान्यशेषतः  
शुभानि पुष्णाति न जातु हीयते ॥***  
यारिंदादरु यावादाददरोंदु कालदल्लि यावुदादरोंदु रीतियल्लि माडिद अंजलि ऒडनॆ पापगळन्नु अशेषवागि दूरमाडि, शुभगळन्नु वृद्धिसुत्तदॆ (इंतह मंगळवु) ऎंदु क्षयिसुवुदु.

***श्लोक-29*** – ई श्लोकदल्लि मानस प्रपत्तियन्नु तोरुत्तदॆ ,अथवा श्लोक 28 “त्वदंग्रिंमुद्दिश्य”,दल्लि हागु श्लोक 29 “उदीर्ण”दल्लि माडिद शरणागति जनित परभक्तियन्नु तॊरुत्तदॆ ऎंदू हेळबहुदु.  
***उदीर्णसंसारदवाशुशुक्षणिम्

प्रयच्छति त्वचारणारुणंबुज  
द्वयानुरागामृतसिन्दुशीकरः ॥***  
निन्न कॆंपाद दिव्य पादद्वदल्लिन भक्तिय अमृतसागरदिंद ऒंदु हनियु प्रज्वलिसुत्तिरुव  
संसारवॆंब काड्गिच्चन्नु ऒंदु क्षणदल्लि आरिसि श्रेष्ठवाद आनंदवन्नु कोडुत्तदॆ.

***श्लोक-30* –** आळवंदार् यावुदे विळंभविल्लदॆ फलवन्नु प्रसादिसुव सिद्धोपायवन्नु( भगवंतनन्नु) स्वीकरिसि, फलप्राप्तिगॆ कायलागदॆ “कूविक् कॊळ्ळुम् कालम् इन्नम् कुऱुगादो”( तिरुवाय्मॊऴि 6.9.9- निन्नन्नु प्रापिसुव दिनवु इन्नु वेगवागदो)यल्लि हेळिदंतॆ हेळुतिद्दारॆ. हीगल्लदॆ इन्नोंदु निर्वाहवु, “कनैकऴल् काण्बदॆन्ऱुकॊल् कण्गळ्(तिरुवाय्मॊऴि 6.3.10- ऎंपॆरुमानिन पादगळन्नु नन्न कण्गळु ऎंदु कानुवुदु ?)” हागु ″काणक् करुदुम् ऎन् कण्णे(तिरुवाइमॊऴि 9.5.1 नन्न कण्गळु नोडलु इच्छिसुत्तवॆ)” यंतॆ प्रपत्तियींद लभिसिद पर भक्तियकारणदिंद हेळुतिद्दारॆ.  
***विलासविक्रांतपरावरालयम्  
नमस्यदार्तिक्षपणे कृतक्षणम् ।  
धनम् मदीयम् तव पादपंकजं  
कदा नु साक्षात्करवाणि चक्षुषा ॥***  
परराद उत्कृष्टराद देवतॆगळ हागु मानुष लोकगळन्नु अनायसवागि क्रीडॆयंतॆ अळॆदंतह, हागु अदन्नु आरादिसिदवर धुःखगळन्नु नशिसुवंतह, हागु ननगॆ धनदंतिरुव निन्न पादपंकजगळन्नु ऎंदु काणुवॆ?

***श्लोक-31*** – ई श्लोकदल्लि आळंदार् “***पडिक्कळवाग निमिर्त्त निन् पादपन्गयमे तलैक्कणियाय्***” (तिरुवाय्मॊऴि 9.2.2 जगत्तिन परिमाणदवरॆगु एरिद निन्न पादपंकजवु नन्न शिरस्सन्नु अलंकरिसलि ) हागु तिरुवाय्मॊऴि 4.3.6 “***कोलमाम् ऎन् सॆन्निक्कु उन् कमलम् अन्न कुरै कऴले***” ( तिरुवाय्मॊऴि 4.3.6 निन्न तवॆरॆगॆ सदृशवाद दिव्य पादगळु नन्न शिरस्सिगॆ आलंकारवागिदॆ) यल्लि हेळिदंतॆ, ” निन्न दिव्य पादगळ्ळन्नु नोडुवुदोंदॆ सालदु ,निन्न दिव्य पाधगळिंद निन्न शिरस्सन्नु अलंकरिसबेकु” ऎंदु हेळुतिधारॆ.  
***कदा पुनः शंख रथान्ग कल्पक  
ध्वजारविंद अंकुश वज्र लांचनम्।  
त्रिविक्रम! त्वच्चरणाअंबुजद्वयम्  
मदीयमूर्दानमलंकरिष्यति ॥***

***श्लोक- 32*** – ई श्लोकदिंद श्लोक-46 “भवन्तम्”रवरॆगु आळवंदार् ऎम्पॆरुमानिन सुंदर तोळ्गळ, आभरण, आयुद, दिव्य महिशि, दिव्य परिकर हागु ऐश्वर्यद योगवन्नु (भगवन्नु इवन्नु होंदिरुवुदन्नु) सवियुतिद्दारॆ, हागु ई अनुभवजनित (अनुभवदिंद हुट्टिद) कैंकर्यदल्लि ऎंदु तॊडगुवॆ ऎंदु केळुतिद्दारॆ. भगवानन्नु भक्तरोंदिगॆ परमपददल्लि सेविसुव तम्म प्राप्यवन्नु विवरिसुतिद्दारॆ.

***श्लोक- 33*** – आळवंदार् ऎम्पॆरुमानिन शौर्य धैर्यादिगळन्नु हागु अवन दिव्य भुजगळ सौंदर्यवन्नु औभविसुतिद्दारॆ.

***श्लोक-38 –*** ई श्लोकदल्लि आळवंदार् पिराट्टिय(श्रीमहालक्ष्मिय) भोग्यतातिशयवन्नु हागु “***उनक्केऱ्कुम् कोल मलर्प् पावै***” (तिरुवाय्मॊऴि 10.10.6 सुंदरवाद कमलदल्लि वासिसुववळु हागु निनगॆ तक्कवळाद श्रीमहालक्ष्मी)यल्लि हेळिदंतॆ ऎम्पॆरुमानिगॆ अनन्यार्हत्ववन्नु (ऎम्पॆरुमानिगागिये इरुवुदु) अनुभविसुतिद्दारॆ.

## Introduction
SrI: SrImathE SatakOpAya nama: SrImathE rAmAnujAya nama: SrImath varavaramunayE nama:

[![paramapadhanathan](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/paramapadhanathan.jpg)](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/paramapadhanathan.jpg)

[SrIman nArAyaNan](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/) with [SrI dhEvi (SrI mahAlakshmi)](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/), bhUdhEvi, nILA dhEvi and nithyasUris in paramapadham

[SrIman nArAyaNan](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/) is the supreme God who is filled with unlimited and wonderful auspicious qualities. Out of his divine and unconditional grace, he blessed selected [jIvAthmA](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/06/thathva-thrayam-chith-who-am-i/)s to become [AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/) (great saints who preached pure and exclusive devotion towards [SrIman nArAyaNan](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/)). Though He was the supremely independent master of the nithyasUris (eternally free souls) and mukthAthmAs (liberated souls), he wanted to rid himself of the one agony he had.

That agony is him suffering for the suffering of the bound souls in the material world. Him being the supreme father of every one, he was unable to bear the suffering of his children who are caught in the cycle of birth and death in the material world. One may question, if the supreme God will have any agony/sorrow – him being sathya kAma (one who can fulfill all his desires) and sathya sankalpa (one who can accomplish anything). Our [AchAryas](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/introduction-contd/) have explained that him feeling the pain for the suffering souls is also his auspicious quality. Just like an all-capable father who is happily living with his one child will still worry for his other child who is staying away from him on his own and struggling, [bhagavAn](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/12/thathva-thrayam-iswara-who-is-god/), though being all-capable, he is feeling the pain looking at the plight of the [jIvAthmA](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/06/thathva-thrayam-chith-who-am-i/)s in samsAram (material world) who are suffering since time-immemorial due to being covered by ignorance and lacking of true knowledge.

To facilitate the upliftment of such suffering [jIvAthmA](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/06/thathva-thrayam-chith-who-am-i/)s, he gives body and senses for the [jIvAthmA](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/06/thathva-thrayam-chith-who-am-i/)s during srushti (creation), reveals the sAsthram (scriptures), himself descends in to the material world as various incarnations like SrI rAma, krishNa, etc. Even after all of these, the [jIvAthmA](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/06/thathva-thrayam-chith-who-am-i/)s are not really willing to learn the true knowledge and accept his supremacy. Just like a hunter will catch a deer using another trained deer, He decides to uplift [jIvAthmA](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/06/thathva-thrayam-chith-who-am-i/)s through other [jIvAthmA](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/06/thathva-thrayam-chith-who-am-i/)s who are already in the material world. So he chooses a select few of them, blesses them with divine knowledge and makes them [AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/). AzhwAr means one who is immersed in bhagavath vishayam (spiritual matters). These [AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/) appeared in various holy places in the southern part of bhAratha dhEsam as foretold by vEdha vyAsa in SrI bhAgavatham. Totally, [AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/) are said to be 10 in number. [poigai AzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/10/22/mudhalazhwargal/) (sarO yOgi),[bhUthaththAzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/10/22/mudhalazhwargal/) (bhUtha yOgi), [pEyAzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/10/22/mudhalazhwargal/) (mahathAhvaya), [thirumazisai AzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/01/16/thirumazhisai-azhwar/) (bhakthi sAra), [nammAzhwAr (satakOpa/parAnkusa)](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/18/nammazhwar-english/), [kulasEkara AzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/01/18/kulasekara-azhwar/),
[periyAzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/01/20/periyazhwar/) (vishNuchiththa),[thoNdaradippodi AzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/01/08/thondaradippodi-azhwar/) (bhakthAngri rENu),
[thiruppANAzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/01/21/thiruppanazhwar/) (muni vAhana yOgi) and[thirumangai AzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/01/23/thirumangai-azhwar/) (parakAla). They are also said to be 12 in number if [madhurakavi AzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/01/17/madhurakavi-azhwar/) and [ANdAL](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/12/16/andal/) (gOdhA – an incarnation of bhUdhEvi nAchiyAr, one of the divine consorts of [bhagavAn](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/12/thathva-thrayam-iswara-who-is-god/)) are included. While the 10 [AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/) are said to be exclusively devoted to [bhagavAn](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/12/thathva-thrayam-iswara-who-is-god/), [madhurakavi AzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/01/17/madhurakavi-azhwar/) and [ANdAL](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/12/16/andal/) were totally devoted to their [AchAryas](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/introduction-contd/), [nammAzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/18/nammazhwar-english/) and [periyAzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/01/20/periyazhwar/) respectively.

[![Azhwars](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Azhwars-1024x235.jpg)](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Azhwars.jpg)

[AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/)

[AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/) sung many pAsurams (verses) in glorification of [SrIman nArAyaNan](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/). These pAsurams total up to 4000 verses approximately and are thus called 4000 dhivya prabandham. dhivya means divine and prabandham means literature (that which captures [bhagavAn](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/12/thathva-thrayam-iswara-who-is-god/) himself). They brought out the essence of vEdham/vEdhAntham in simple and chaste thamizh. The whole purpose of these dhivya prabandhams is to uplift the [jIvAthmA](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/06/thathva-thrayam-chith-who-am-i/)s by transmitting true knowledge. Several hundreds of years after the time of [AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/), many [AchAryas](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/introduction-contd/) starting with [nAthamunigaL](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/22/nathamunigal/), centered with [SrI rAmAnuja](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/06/emperumanar/) and ending with [mAmunigaL](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/23/azhagiya-manavala-mamunigal/) appeared and preached the divine message of [AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/). While less intelligent people considered[AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/) pAsurams as simple thamizh songs, the most intelligent [AchAryas](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/introduction-contd/) established that these pAsurams bring out the ultimate philosophy of [SrIman nArAyaNan](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/) being the means (to be uplifted from this material world) and the goal (to be situated in ones true nature of eternal service to [SrIman nArAyaNan](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/) in paramapadham – spiritual world). Our [pUrvAchAryas](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/introduction-contd/) fully relished the dhivya prabandhams and centered their life around learning them, teaching them and living by those pAsurams.

[![azhwar-madhurakavi-nathamuni](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/azhwar-madhurakavi-nathamuni.jpg)](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/azhwar-madhurakavi-nathamuni.jpg)[nammAzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/18/nammazhwar-english/) with[madhurakavi AzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/01/17/madhurakavi-azhwar/) and [nAthamunigaL](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/22/nathamunigal/) in either side

There was a dark period in the history where the dhivya prabandhams were lost in time after the time of [AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/). Eventually, it was [nAthamunigaL](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/22/nathamunigal/) who took lots of hardships to locate AzhwAr thirunagari which is the birth place of [nammAzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/18/nammazhwar-english/) and he learnt all the 4000 dhivya prabandham with their meanings by the divine grace of [nammAzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/18/nammazhwar-english/). [nAthamunigaL](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/22/nathamunigal/) classified the 4000 dhivya prabandhams into 4 parts as we see today, taught them to his sishyas and brought out the importance of these dhivya prabandhams. He added kaNNinuN chiRu thAmbu into 4000 dhivya prabandhams in honour of[madhurakavi AzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/01/17/madhurakavi-azhwar/) who showed unflinching devotion towards [nammAzhwAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/18/nammazhwar-english/).

[![Ramanuja_Sriperumbudur](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Ramanuja_Sriperumbudur.jpg)](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Ramanuja_Sriperumbudur.jpg)

[SrI rAmAnuja](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/06/emperumanar/)

[SrI rAmAnuja (emperumAnAr)](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/06/emperumanar/) who is considered as an incarnation of Adhi sEsha and who appeared a century later received the same through the guru paramparA by studying the same under various [AchAryas](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/introduction-contd/) per the desire of [yAmunAchArya](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/01/alavandhar/). He then spread the glories of [AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/) and their works to all sections of the society and greatly facilitated the propagation of this SrIvaishNava sampradhAyam. It is because of his great contribution, this sampradhAyam was declared to be known as [SrI rAmAnuja](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/06/emperumanar/) dharsanam by [SrI ranganAtha](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/) himself. Also, rAmAnusa nURRanthAdhi which was sung in glorification of [SrI rAmAnuja](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/06/emperumanar/) by [thiruvarangaththu amudhanAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/03/26/thiruvarangathu-amudhanar/) was added into 4000 dhivya prabandham by the [AchAryas](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/introduction-contd/) due to his greatness. This rAmAnusa nURRanthAdhi is glorified as “prapanna gAyathri” – that which is to be recited compulsorily by all prapannas (ones who are surrendered) every day, just like how gAyathri manthram is to be recited by brAhmaNas everyday.

[![nampillai-goshti1](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/nampillai-goshti1.jpg)](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/nampillai-goshti1.jpg)[nampiLLai](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/16/nampillai-english/) kAlakshEpa (lecture) gOshti (assembly)

[nampiLLai](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/16/nampillai-english/) is an important AchArya (coming in the parampArA after [emperumAnAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/06/emperumanar/), [embAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/07/embar/), [bhattar](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/11/parasara-bhattar/) and [nanjIyar](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/14/nanjiyar-english/)) who should be mentioned at this juncture. He was living in SrIrangam and was the leader of SrIvaishNava sampradhAyam during his times. It was during his period in SrIrangam, the meanings of 4000 dhivya prabandham were given great importance. He was engaged in giving lectures everyday close to the sanctum sanctorium of [periya perumAL](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/) – [SrI ranganAtha](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/). It is explained that even [periya perumAL](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/) would stand up and try to peek through the window to hear his lectures. [nampiLLai](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/16/nampillai-english/)‘s sishyas also greatly contributed to propagate the meanings of these dhivya prabandhams. [periyavAchchAn piLLai](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/10/05/periyavachan-pillai-english/) who is a sishya of[nampiLLai](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/16/nampillai-english/) and also known as vyAkyAna chakkaravarthy (emperor among commentators) wrote commentaries for all dhivya prabandhams and his contribution is greatly hailed by our [AchAryas](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/introduction-contd/). [vadakku thiruvIdhi piLLai](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/17/vadakku-thiruveedhi-pillai-english/), who is another sishya of [nampiLLai](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/16/nampillai-english/), documented the lectures given by [nampiLLai](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/16/nampillai-english/) on thiruvAimozhi and this document became to be known as eedu 36000 padi (the most cherished vyAkyAnam of thiruvAimozhi).

[![pillailokacharya-goshti](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/pillailokacharya-goshti.jpeg)](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/pillailokacharya-goshti.jpeg)piLLai lOkAchAryar kAlakshEpa gOshti

After [nampiLLai](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/16/nampillai-english/)‘s time, [piLLai lOkAchAryar](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/18/pillai-lokacharyar/) leads the sath sampradhAyam and documents all the most confidential matters of dhivya prabandham in his rahasya granthams. These confidential matters were explained by various [AchAryas](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/introduction-contd/) at various times in various granthams.[piLLai lOkAchAryar](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/18/pillai-lokacharyar/) consolidated those and documented them in his 18 primary works. But towards the end of his life, tragedy struck SrIrangam in the form of mughal invaders who destroyed everything there.[piLLai lOkAchAryar](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/18/pillai-lokacharyar/) arranged for the safe journey of namperumAL (SrI ranganAtha – uthsavar) and escaped from the invaders from there along with namperumAL. Shortly in to the journey, he ascended to paramapadham due to the difficult journey through forests at such advanced age. Then came a lengthy period of suffering for the whole of SrIvaishNava community. After few decades, when peace prevailed and invaders were driven out, namperumAL eventually returned to SrIrangam.

[![srisailesa-thanian-small](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/srisailesa-thanian-small.jpg)](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/srisailesa-thanian-small.jpg)[mAmunigaL](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/23/azhagiya-manavala-mamunigal/) kAlakshEpa gOshti – “srI sailEsa dhayApAthram” thanian submission

Finally, [maNavALa mAmunigaL](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/23/azhagiya-manavala-mamunigal/) who is considered as an incarnation of [SrI rAmAnuja](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/06/emperumanar/) himself, appeared in [AzhwAr thirunagari](http://azhwarthirunagari.koyil.org/). He became a sishya of[thiruvAimozhi piLLai](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/19/thiruvaimozhi-pillai/) and learned all the esoteric meanings of vEdham, vEdhAntham and dhivya prabandhams from his own father and [thiruvAimozhi piLLai](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/19/thiruvaimozhi-pillai/). Under the orders of[thiruvAimozhi piLLai](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/19/thiruvaimozhi-pillai/) (his AchArya), he reached SrIrangam and spent the rest of his life in rejuvenating the sath sampradhAyam to its past glories. He personally searched for all the lost literature, studied them, recorded them in palm leaves with great difficulty and preserved them for future generations to come. Fittingly, in appreciating his great qualities and efforts, SrI ranganAtha himself listens to thiruvAimozhi kAlakshEpam from [mAmunigaL](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/23/azhagiya-manavala-mamunigal/) for a whole year and at the end of the lecture series, he himself appears as a small boy and accepts [mAmunigaL](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/23/azhagiya-manavala-mamunigal/) as his AchArya and submits him the most glorious “SrISailESa dhayApAthram…” thaniyan. Subsequently, many AchAryas who came in various lineages taught and lived by these dhivya prabandhams.

Thus, [AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/)‘ dhivya prabandhams which were focussed on the upliftment of [jIvAthmA](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/06/thathva-thrayam-chith-who-am-i/)s which is to fulfill the desire of [bhagavAn](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/12/thathva-thrayam-iswara-who-is-god/) have been preserved by our [pUrvAchAryas](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/introduction-contd/) through the history. It is emphasised greatly that all SrIvaishNavas should learn the dhivya prabandhams, understand the meanings properly and live by them. Keeping this in mind, with the aim of translating all of 4000 dhivya prabandhams, we are starting humbly in this great endeavour. We have a great desire to complete this translation kainkaryam by 2017 May, when we will commemorate the 1000th year appearance day of [SrI rAmAnuja](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/09/06/emperumanar/). Initially we are starting with English translation but we would try to arrange for translation of these dhivya prabandhams in other languages as well.

It would be very useful to understand the validity and glories of AzhwArs and their dhivya prabhandhams. Please read the following articles which go through them in detail:

With full dependence and faith on [SrIman nArAyaNan](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/), [AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/) and [AchAryas](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/introduction-contd/), we are planning to translate the dhivya prabandhams into simple English (and other languages too). We will provide word-by-word meaning for each pAsuram with specific highlights and important points from the vyAkyAnams. The website will be well organized and easily searchable. We would also attempt to add pictures wherever possible/applicable. With the blessings of [bhagavAn](https://granthams.koyil.org/2013/03/12/thathva-thrayam-iswara-who-is-god/), bhAgavathas and [AchAryas](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/introduction-contd/), we plan to do a faithful kainkaryam of translating these dhivya prabandhams which will be useful for many generations to come.

Though dhivya prabandham only indicates the thamizh pAsurams composed by [AzhwArs](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/azhwars/) (and rAmAnusa nURRanthAdhi of [thiruvarangaththu amudhanAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/03/26/thiruvarangathu-amudhanar/)), in this website/initiative, we would also cover many of the thamizh/samskritham prabandhams which are composed by our [pUrvAchAryas](http://guruparamparai.koyil.org) in the “[Other Prabandhams](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/index.php/others/)” section. We revere our AchAryas SrIsUkthis (divine compositions) greatly too and will also provide translation for the same as much as possible.

#### Introduction

In the previous pAsuram, she was holding veeNA in hand, and thinking about the veeNA itself as [emperumAn](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/) and being in union with Him, said words of victory that highlighted her victory and His losing, but she did not hear His reply of words of His victory and her loss – because she thought veeNA itself as Him. Only due to this she lost the state she was in, in the previous pAsuram, and losing her pride also, she started calling Him thinking about her and the veeNA in its original form itself. As was seen in the previous pAsuram, the SlOkam “BharThAramiva samprApthA jAnakee mudhithASBhavath”, becoming very happy thinking of His ring (kaNaiyAzhi) itself as rAmA, and after realizing that perumAL was not present there, (laments) seethA pirAtti (who is threatened by the demons in ashOkavanam) – “hA rAma hA lakShmaNa hA sumithrE hA rAmamAtha: saha mE jananya: । EshA vipaDhyAmyaham alpaBhAgyA mahArNavE nauriva mUDavAthA ॥ \[SrI rAmAyaNAm – sundhara kANdam – 28-8\] (Oh rAma! lakShmaNa! SumithrA! Mother of rAma! My mothers! I who do not have good deeds in me, has been affected by sorrows, like a boat caught in the sea storm); like how She lamented, parakAla nAyaki also laments in this pAsuram. seethAp pirAtti thought about her dear perumAL and said ‘Oh rAmA!’, and thinking about iLaiya perumAL who is enjoying the presence of perumAL with him and said “Oh lakShmaNa!”, and thinking about the mother of that iLaiya perumAL and called out “Oh sumithrE!”. Likewise, she is also calling perumAL, who does not look at either the greatness or lowliness of anyone for protecting them, and is saddened in this pAsuram (due to separation).

viripozhil sUzh thirunaRaiyUr ninRAy enRum

thuNai mulai mEl thuLi sOrach chOrginRALE 16*

## Word by word meaning

inidhu ugandha – and became very happy,

kALAy enRum – and having the individualism, and

en kaniyE – Oh my fruit

kaNapuraththu – (that became ripe in) thirukkaNNapuram that is

kadi pozhil sUzh – surrounded by fragrant gardens! And,

magizhndhAy enRum – Oh who became happy

manRu amarak kUththAdi – by dancing with pots in the middle of the junction of roads! And,

vada thiruvEngadam mEya maindhA enRum – Oh the proud one who resides firmly in vada thiruvEngadam! And,

vEndhE – Oh the king who

venRu – won and

kaLaindha – destroyed

asurar kulam – the clan of asuras! And

ninRAy enRum – having your divine presence

thirunaRaiyUr – in thirunaRaiyUr

viri pozhil sUzh – that is surrounded by the gardens spread out expanding, and

thunRu kuzhal kaRu niRaththu en thuNaiyE enRum – Oh one having dense hair plaits, dark divine body, and being my companion, saying all these,

sOrginRAL – she becomes sad/faint that the

thuLi sOra – drops of tears flow down

thuNai mulai mEl – the bosoms that match each other.

#### vyAkyAnam

*kanRu mEyththu* – First she says how [emperumAn](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/) protects the little cows without considering any status.

*kanRu mEyththu inidhu ugandha kALAy enRum* – She is asking – Have you abandoned the nature of saving all entities including the calves? Here the word mEyththu (herding the calves) is present in the sense of protecting them. It would be apt to stay relaxed if He protects only the nithyasUris. It would be apt to stay relaxed if He protects the munis like sanakar sometimes. Having been incarnated as balarAma and kaNNan and protecting only the cowherds who are so innocent that they can’t distinguish between their left and right hands, even then one can stay relaxed. But when you are herding the calves who are the epitome of lack of wisdom, how can I stay relaxed while separated from you (because you are not including me in protecting)?

*kanRu mEyththu inidhugandha kALAy* – By kALai (bull) it says His dignity – can mean to imply a king or a youth. When it means king – being in the state of protecting the nithyasUris is like (the king) being in a jail, and only after herding the calves did He become a chakravarthy of all the worlds – is the thought. Only after protecting everyone did He completely gain the nobility of being known as one who protects everyone.

*inidhugandha* – Like said in “*thivaththilum pasu niRai mEyppu uvaththi, sengani vAy engaL Ayar dhEvE* \[[thiruvAimozhi 10.3.10](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/index.php/2019/11/thiruvaimozhi-10-3-10-avaththangal-vilaiyum/)\]”, he is joyful due to herding the group of cows. Due to herding the calves He becomes extremely (inidhu), happy. Cows would have the knowledge that He is protecting. These calves don’t even have that knowledge. So, when He protects the calves He becomes very very happy – is the idea. Oh You who protected the calves that are at the upper limit of ignorance, are not protecting me who understood You and cannot survive life without You! – says she. Oh! leaving the state of being the beloved lover, and being knowledgeable, and helpless, could I not be one among the calves of no intelligence!

kALAy enRum – Here ‘kALai’ can be considered to say the age of youth. Even though she is overcome by sadness, she is saying this due to losing to His youth.

As said in “tharuNau rUpasampannau sukumarau mahAbalau । puNdareeka viSAlAkShau cheerakRushNAjinAmbarau ॥ \[SrI rAmAyaNam – AraNya kANdam – 19-14\]” ((rAma and lakShmaNa are) being young, full of beauty, soft in nature, having great ability, having wide eyes like lotus, having tree barks and deer skin for dress (so describes sUrpaNakA about them to rAvaNa)), when their youth would attract even a vulture like sUrpaNakA, will it spare parakAla nAyaki? It could be that she wishes to further polish her youth by union with Him. Or, after herding the calves, kaNNan’s youth became even better than earlier, like those who ate the kAyakalpa medicine. Humans have the body that grows by food, so it is the food that is the reason for their growth – likewise, for Him the protector, when the act of protecting increases, His youth also gets better.

kadkpozhil sUzh kaNapuraththen kaniyE enRum – It is only to protect those who are present later than the respective vibhava avathAras, that He has come to thirukkaNNapuram, and stood there sweetly – this is the type of explanation of AchAryas who were present before Bhattar. Bhattar considered the phrase “kadipozhil sUzh kaNApuram” to give another explanation. That is, “kaNNapirAn who went herding the calves, went in a different path to surround the calves that moved away from his hands – at that time He saw there a very beautiful garden that would remove all tiredness. That was thirukkaNNapuram. Since it is a garden that mesmerizes, like [nammAzhvAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/18/nammazhwar-english/) also said “*mayal migu pozhil sUzh* \[[thiruvAimozhi – 2.10.3](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/index.php/2016/06/thiruvaimozhi-2-10-3-payanalla-seydhu/)\]”, kaNNan did not have the heart to leave that place and so stayed there itself” – divined Bhattar. It is to be noted that Bhattar divined so because he thinks of viBhava avathAr and archA avathAr as one and the same.

*kadipozhil sUzh kaNApuram* – the garden is such that one would say it is made of fragrance itself – such a thirukkaNNapuram that is surrounded by fragrant gardens. As said in, “sarvaganDha: sarvarasa: \[chAndhogyOpanishath – 3-14-2\]” ((paramapurusha, in His divine body) has got all the fragrances, all the good tastes), this garden’s fragrance is such that He who possesses all the fragrances is engaged in it.

*en kaniyE enRum* – [emperumAn](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/) is like a ripe fruit of that garden. It means He is sweet for me like a ripe fruit. “en kaniyE” – she is showing that she is only a prapanna who has surrendered to Him, consider only Him as the means to attain Him, and that she is not an upAsaka who would go for other means such as Bhakthi. For those who are using Bhakthi etc., as the means, only after completion of such means would it give them the experience of BhagavAn. So, before such completion, He would be tasteless to them like an unripe fruit. For prapannas, from the very time they surrendered to Him as their means He would be sweet to them, so He would be of good taste (enjoyable) to them like a ripe fruit.

*en kaniyE enRum* – Since she thinks as “kaNNapuram onRu udaiyAnukku adiyEn oruvarkku uriyEnO \[periya thirumozhi – 8.9.3\]”, she saying en kaniyE.

*kaniyE enRum* – A ripe fruit would rot and go waste if it is not eaten and enjoyed by someone. One who eats also would not bear if he does not enjoy that fruit. Since You did not give yourself for me to enjoy, are You trying to destroy us both? – says parakAla nAyaki.

*manRamarak kUththAdi maghizhndhAy enRum* – In the previous line, she referred to Him as the fruit that is sweet (only) for the one who enjoys it. Further she says You showed Yourself fully to everyone (by dancing with pots in the middle of the road), like one opening a bag full of aval (rice flakes) in a junction of roads.

*manRamarak kUththAdi* – Oh You who danced such that the theatre stage that is the junction square was completely well set for the dance! That is, even after finishing the dance, that arena would be having the smell of dance. That is, in that dancing arena, there would be an image of dance being performed still. Bhattar gives an example for this – “In thiruvarangam, even after namperumAL has crossed a street during procession, there would be the fragrance of kasthUri which is the fragrance of divine body of thiruvaranganAthan. Likewise, in the arena of thiruvAyppAdi, the fragrance of dance would be filled even after He finished dancing”. The word manRu denotes both a junction and a court/arena. It is the courtyard of assembly of the cowherds. Or, taking manRu to mean those present in the arena/the court, it could mean – all the cowherds of the arena, amara – he danced such that they all would become His belonging. That is, without the mind going after any other matter but fully engaged in the dance, making them think “that, that dance!”.

*maghizndhAy enRum* – It is not important that those who saw the dance were happy. It is He who was happy – such was the dance. That is, it is He who was happy by winning them over with His dance.

*kUththAdi maghizhndhAy enRum* – He who has to be happy seeing thumburu, nAradha, et al dancing after experiencing His sweetness, is being the dancer and the one who is happy too, says parakAla nAyaki. Is it apt for a eeSvaran to do pot dance? It is not so when in the state of parathvam, but for kANNan who is born as a cowherd, this dance gives excellence. Like how when there is more wealth, brahmins would perform vaidhika karmas like yAgam, like how when there is more wealth the samsAris that are engaged in wordly pleasures would marry again and again, the outlet of pride of increased wealth for cowherds is the dance with pots. He the sarvESvaran considered Himself as one of the cowherds only, He danced so – is the idea. The matter that was available for everyone to enjoy in the middle of the junction is the one who is being hard for me to get – says she. The item that is available in the market is being hard for me to get – says she.

*vada thiruvEngadam mEya myndhA enRum* – It is not only about standing in a junction in the place that is thiruvAyppAdi and giving Himself fully to them, it also talks about Him giving Himself fully by standing in thirumalai that is in the middle of both the worlds. “kaNNAvAn enRum, maNNOr viNNOrkku \[[thiruvAimozhi 1.8.3](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/index.php/2015/09/thiruvaimozhi-1-8-3-kannavan/)\]”, divined [nammAzhvAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/18/nammazhwar-english/) too, that the place where people of this world and that world would assemble is thirumalai.

maindhA enRum – It is His youth that He gave everyone, standing in thirumalai. myndhan – young person. As said in “vAnavar vAnavar kOnodum namanRezhum thiruvEngadam \[[thiruvAzimozhi 3.3.7](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/index.php/2016/08/thiruvaimozhi-3-3-7-sumandhu-mamalar/)\]”, “kAnamum vAnaramum vEdumudai vEngadam \[[nAnumugan thiruvandhAdhi – 47](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/index.php/2018/08/nanmugan-thiruvandhadhi-47/)\]”, those who proudly think of themselves as eeSvarans, the vAnaras that were born as animals, and hunters of lower births, all together stole His divine body, but which is being hard for me to get, says she. It could also be considered to mean – In eighth pAsuram, in the vyAkhAyanam of the word “kaLvA”, as per jithanthE 1-5 taken there as a pramANam, the divine body that is common to all the devotees, is belonging to everyone except me!

*venRasurar kulam kaLaindha vEndhE enRum* – The thought here is, “If He is a dear one only, but if it is in someone else’s hands to remove hurdles to reach Him, then I might lose Him. It is not so in His case who destroys very big hurdles such as the clans of asuras”. By this phrase, she is calling – O chakravarthy thirumagan, who killed Kharan, thUshanan in the forest of janasthAnam, who went to lankA, who killed everyone associated to rAvaNa and made him be alone, who uprooted everyone who were having characteristics of asura, and who destroyed their leader rAvaNAn also! She is saying ‘asurar kulam kaLaindha’, referring to rAkshasas as asuras because they are different from the dhEvas who are warriors.

*vEndhE enRum* – As said in, “rAmO rAmO rAma ithi prajAnAnAm aBhavan kaThA: । rAmaBhUtham jagath aBhUth rAmE rAjyam praSAsathi ॥ \[SrI rAmAyaNam – yudhDha kANdam – 128-102\]” (rAmA, rAmA, rAmA is what the people were talking. When rAma ruled the country, the whole world became rAmA), One who is there for the dear ones to rumble about Him, and for enemies to fall, is not showing Himself to me. This AzhvAr is divining the two attributes of perumAL as divined by [periyAzhvAr](http://ponnadi.blogspot.in/2012/10/archavathara-anubhavam-periyazhwar.html) in “thAyar magizha onnAr thaLara \[periyAzhvar thirumozhi 1.7.11\]”, and in “thiN koL asuraraith thEya vaLarkinRan \[periyAzhvAr thirumozhi 1.2.16\]”.

*viripozhil sUzh thirunaRaiyUr ninRAy enRum* – (thirunaRaiyUr being) the place where chakravarthy thirumagan comes and stands for those who are present after His incarnation of rAma. Unlike other dhivya dhESams where [emperumAn](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/) is present with the two divine consorts, in thirunaRaiyUr He is present with only vanjuLavallith thAyAR, and thus appears as rAma who cannot think of anyone other than seethA, is being present there. Meaning of this phrase is “Oh who stood in thirunaRaiyUr that is surrounded by vast garden”. Since the garden is using the loving glance of nambi and vanjuLavallith thAyar as the required water for growth, it is growing by leaps everyday. Since the loving glance of them would not change, the expansion of garden would not change either.

*ninRAy enRum* – He roamed around in search of [pirAtti](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/). After seeing Her in that place (thirunaRaiyUr), He could sustain Himself and then stood there. If that state has to last long for You, then don’t You have to show Yourself to me who is a servant of Her? When You don’t show Your grace on me, will You get Her company? – thinks she.

*thunRu kuzhal karu niRaththu en thuNaiyE enRum* – In this line too parakAla nAyaki is talking about nambi’s divine hair plait, and beauty of His divine body. It means ‘my companion having very dense black hair plait, and black divine body like a dark rainy cloud!’. Like how [nammAzhvAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/18/nammazhwar-english/) is also considering that dark cloud as his help, divined “thOLum nAngudaich churikuzhal kamalakkaN kari vAyk kALa mEghaththai anRi maRRonRilam gathiyE \[[thiruvAimozhi 10.1.1](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/index.php/2019/10/thiruvaimozhi-10-1-1-thala-thamarai/)\]”. The word “thuNai” has got the meaning of “protector”. Or, dear husband could also be a meaning. She is indicating that His divine hair plait and beauty of His divine body by themselves got her interested in Him. During the time of searching for [pirAtti](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/) during His stay in the forest, due to not maintaining it His hair was down with locks. Since after seeing Her here (thirunaRaiyUr), it is then the hair that was properly combed and reformed by Her, it is saying ‘thunRu kuzhal’ (dense hair plait).

*en thuNaiyE* – “praSastha snighDha neela kutila kunthala: \[SrI vishNu sahasranAma BhAshyam – kESava \[23\] nAma Bhattar BhAshya SrIsUkthi\] (One having slippery, black, curly divine hair plait) – having such hair plait that is praised, and with divine body having nature of removing tiredness – showing these You made me Yours – is the meaning. During the days when I was not inclined to be involved in You, You cared for my being and not getting destroyed, and so showed your beauty of form and made me Yours and joined me – all of these You did so that You could leave me now? – says she. The word “thuNai” implies both the person who made her interested in Him, and also the one who protected her. In the pAsuram “ilai thuNai maRRu en nenjE \[[nAnumugan thiruvandhAdhi – 8](https://divyaprabandham.koyil.org/index.php/2018/06/nanmugan-thiruvandhadhi-8/)\], [thirumazhisai AzhvAr](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2013/01/16/thirumazhisai-azhwar/) showed both these meanings for the word “thuNai”. Oh who is my dear destiny, and who is the means of protection as well – is the thought.

*enRum thuNai mulai mEl thuLisOrach chOrginRALE* – Mother is saying that as my daughter was calling Him out like this, she fainted in the floor.

*thuNai mulai* – When He and she enjoy in union, her bosoms would be a companion instrument for both of their enjoyment. In the state of separation from Him too, the bosom of His touch would be a companion for her. So she is saying ‘thuNai mulai’.

*thuLi sORach chOrginRALE* – Due to her tears pouring out like fire and falling on her bosoms and changing its colour, she also fainted, says the mother.

Considering the “*en thuNaiyE*” in previous line together with “sOrginRALE” of this line, the meaning of this word is as follows – Like how those who are falling down would say “ammA” while falling down, even when suffering due to Him not showing up, she is telling His name only while fainting and falling down. As a help to her as a mother, while I was standing looking at her thinking “would she look at my face (for help?)”, she, even without considering that He whom she considered as help had left her, is thinking about His showing up only, and fainted; may be this is how the state would be of those who are having connection with [emperumAn](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/). It is the nature of such people to ignore the other matters/things that shout out “take me, take me”, and not being able to sustain oneself without Him even when He has ignored. Even though the mother had considered herself as a protector, the daughter had ignored her (and considered only [emperumAn](https://acharyas.koyil.org/index.php/2012/08/17/divya-dhampathi/) as her protector).

#### pAsuram

#### **Simple translation**

pUthanA came to cheat krishNa, the sarvaSEshi, disguising in the form of \[krishNa’s\] mother, cried out and fell on earth, after he made her life leave her by mercifully consuming it through her poison-filled bosom; he is death for demons; to be seen with amazement and be praised by vidhyAdharas, chAraNAs, sidhdhas, emperumAn assumed a feminine form, and gave beautiful, tasty nectar to dhEvathAs when indhra had lost his wealth; I saw such emperumAn in thiruvallikkENi.

#### **Highlights from vyAkyAnam (Commentary)**

*vanjanai …* – emperumAn who consumed the life of pUthanA through her poison filled bosom, where the deceitful one assumed a feminine form to cheat him, and caused her to cry out and fall on earth.

*nAdhanai …* – One who saved and gave himself when he killed pUthanA who came in disguise of a contradictory form, to kill self who is the life of the universe. harivamSam “*sthanyam thath vishasammiSram rasyamAsIth jagathgurO:*” (her breast-milk which was poisoned, was very sweet for him).

*dhAnavar kURRai* … – One who is death personified for demons. One who fed the dhEvathAs with a feminine form that does not match purushOththama (best among men) and remained to be praised by vidhyAdharas, chAraNas, sidhdhas et al in amazement.
