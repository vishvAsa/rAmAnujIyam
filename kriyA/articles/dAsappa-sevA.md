The Dāsayyas are hereditary religious servants associated with the Vaiṣṇava tradition of Karnataka. Their role combines devotional singing, ritual service, household blessings, alms collection, and the preservation of oral recitations and kanda padya-s.

Entry into the tradition traditionally requires dīkṣā. One form is the Śaṅkha-Cakra mudrā-dhāraṇa, i.e. the conch and discus are branded onto the shoulders, marking dedication to the service of Nārāyaṇa – and then they proceed to wander, singing the glories of the lord. 

Dāsayyas often carry a items - Jōlige - the sacred alms bag , Jagate - a brass gong, a bāṇike, shakha among other things like the Rāma bāña. They are also called to village fairs where they offer animal bali to Kenchanna (red) & Kariyanna (black) - who they say are Jaya & Vijaya
