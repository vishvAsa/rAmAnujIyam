श्रीः

|| कैवल्यविचारः ||

(मुमुक्षु-प्पडि-व्याख्यायां श्री-बालसरस्वती-कृतायां तात्पर्य-दीपिकायां दृश्यमाना अधो-निर्दिष्टा [[एतद्वादसम्बन्धिन्यः|एतद्-वाद-सम्बन्धिन्यः]] कारिकास् तद्-ग्रन्थानुवादेन प्रदर्श्यन्ते । यदि तत्रायम् शब्दानुवादस् स्यात् तर्हि श्रीमद्-वेान्ताद्-देशिक-साम्बुदाचार्य-सूक्तित्वेन भाव्यताम् । यद्यर्थानुवादः, तथाप्येषां आचार्याणां कैवल्य-विषये ऽभिमतस् सार आविष्कृतो भवति । तथा च तत्रानुपूर्वी—

Translation: The following verses (kārikās) pertaining to this philosophical doctrine, which are seen in the Tātparyadīpikā (a commentary on the Mumukṣuppaḍi) composed by Śrī Bālasarasvatī, are presented here by translating/reproducing that text. If this is a verbatim reproduction of words, then it should be regarded as the auspicious statements of Śrīmad Vādihansāmbudācārya. If it is a translation of the meaning (a paraphrase), even then, the essence of the revered Ācārya's intended view on the subject of Kaivalya is revealed here. The sequence is as follows:

> " वादिहंसाम्बुवाहास् तु—
>
> संसारे दुःखितो जन्तुर् आनन्दं परमात्मनि ।  
> तत्राप्त्युपायं तद्-भक्तिं लब्ध्वा तत्रैव मज्ज्ति ॥
>
> ‘जन्मान्तर-सहस्रेषु [[तपोध्यान्समाधिभिः|तपो-ध्यान-समाधिभिः]] ।  
> नराणां क्षीण-पापानां कृष्णे भक्तिः प्रजायते ’ ॥

Translation: Vādihansāmbuvāha, however, states: A soul, deeply distressed in this cycle of worldly existence (Saṃsāra), having found true bliss in the Supreme Lord (Paramātman) and having obtained devotion (Bhakti) which is the means to attain Him, becomes completely immersed right there. "Only after thousands of past births, through austerities (Tapas), meditation (Dhyāna), and deep absorption (Samādhi), does devotion to Lord Krishna arise in those human beings whose sins have been entirely eradicated."

> सर्वत्र चास्ति तत्-प्रेम यद्-वत् तन्-निबन्धनम् ।  
> अनिष्टान्तर-हेतुत्वे ऽप्य् आत्मनां क्वचिद् एव हि ॥
>
> ‘या प्रीतिर् अविवेकानां विषयेष्व् अनपायिनी ’ ।  
> सा यस्य देव-देवे स्यात् तस्य [[भक्तावधिकारिया|भक्तावधिकारिता]] ॥

Translation: Love for Him, which is firmly rooted in His nature as the supreme bestower, exists everywhere. Even though the individual souls might be the cause of other undesirable things, this true love is indeed found only in a few. "That unfailing love which the non-discriminating (ignorant) possess for worldly sense-objects — he who harbors that exact same intensity of love for the God of gods is fully qualified for Bhakti (Devotion)."

> यस्य प्रीतिर् असञ्जाता स पुनस् तत्-प्रसिद्धये ।  
> आत्मावलोकने यत्नं कुरुते तत्र कारणम् ॥

Translation: He in whom such natural love has not arisen, in order to establish it, makes an effort towards self-realization (Ātmāvalokana). The cause (method) for that is as follows:

[[P196]] न्यायकुलिशे

ज्ञानयोगः कर्मयोगः इति द्वितयम् ईरितम् ।  
यः पुण्य-परिपाकेन पूर्वम् एव जितेन्द्रियः ॥

ज्ञानयोगो ऽधिकुरुते स्व-शास्त्रेणावगम्य सः ।  
विवेकात्म-चिन्ता या तु निरन्तरा ॥

Translation: A twofold path is declared: Jñāna Yoga (the path of knowledge) and Karma Yoga (the path of action). He who has already conquered his senses beforehand through the ripening of his meritorious deeds, becomes qualified for Jñāna Yoga, having understood it through his respective scriptures. That constant contemplation on the self, which consists of discriminating the soul from primordial nature (Prakṛti) and material elements — some men of exceeding merit engage in that path first.

केचिद् आदौ प्रवर्तन्ते तत्र पुण्याधिका नराः ।  
अन्ये तु कर्मयोगेन पाप-कर्म-क्षये सति ॥

सत्त्वोद्रेकाद् वशी-कृत्य करणान्य् अधिकुर्वते ।  
अथवान्तर्गत-ज्ञाने कर्मयोगे ऽधिकुर्वते ॥

Translation: Others, however, through Karma Yoga, upon the exhaustion of their sinful actions, become qualified by subduing their sensory organs due to the rising of Sattva (the mode of goodness and purity). Alternatively, they become qualified for a Karma Yoga that implicitly includes knowledge (Jñāna) within it.

लोक-सङ्ग्रह-सौकर्य-पर्यालोचनतस् ततः ।  
नियमोपेत-योगेन ज्ञानयोगं विनैव तु ॥

पश्यन्त्य् आत्मानम् इत्य् एवम् अधिकार-व्यवस्थितिः ।  
दृष्ट्वानुकूलम् आत्मानम् अन्यत्र विगत-स्पृहः ॥

Translation: From that, by considering the ease of maintaining the world order (Loka-saṅgraha)... even without explicitly practicing Jñāna Yoga, they behold the Self through Yoga endowed with strict disciplines (Niyama). Thus is the organization of eligibilities (Adhikāra). One who has perceived his own self as agreeable, completely free from desires elsewhere,

भोग्य-भूत-परात्मानम् उपायम् अनुसन्दधत् ।  
तेनैव चापराधेन क्षये सत्य् अपि कर्मणाम् ॥

Translation: while contemplating the Supreme Lord (who is the ultimate object of enjoyment) merely as a means (Upāya). By that very offense, even when his karmas are destroyed,

तिरोहितेशानुकूल्यस् स्वात्मानं भोग्यम् अश्नुते ।  
तद् य इत्थं विदुर् इति केवलस्याचिरादिका ॥

Translation: his natural favorable disposition towards the Lord becomes concealed, and he experiences only his own self as the object of enjoyment. Thus, for the 'Kevalin' (the seeker of pure self-isolation) who is mentioned in the Shruti as 'those who know it thus', the path beginning with light (Arcirādi-gati) is heard.

गति-श्रुतानुभूतिस् तु ब्रह्मणः प्राप्तिर् ईरिता ।  
ब्रह्मानन्दानुभवं केवलस्य यदीष्यते ॥

‘योगिनामपि सर्वेषां मद्-गतेनान्तरात्मना ’ ।  
‘चतुर्-विधा भजन्ते मां जनास् सुकृतिनो ऽर्जुन ’ ॥

[[P197]] कैवल्यविचारः

अव्यक्त्तो ऽक्षर इत्य् उक्तस् तम् आहुः परमां गतिम् ।  
यं प्राप्य न निवर्तन्ते तद्-धाम परमं मम ॥

इत्यादिषु स्मर्यमाणस् सत्य-प्राण-विदोस् तथा ।  
श्रुत-छन्दोग-शाखायां फल-भेदो ऽसमञ्जसः ॥

Translation: However, the true experience is declared to be the attainment of Brahman. If the experience of the bliss of Brahman is accepted for the Kevalin as well, then the distinction of fruits mentioned in Smriti texts like "And of all yogins, he who with his inner self merged in Me..." (Gita 6.47), "Four kinds of virtuous men worship Me, O Arjuna..." (Gita 7.16), and "The unmanifest is called the Imperishable; they call that the highest goal. Attaining which they do not return, that is My supreme abode" (Gita 8.21), and similarly the distinction heard in the Chandogya Upanishad for the knowers of Truth and Prāṇa, would become completely inconsistent.

तद् एवं भगवत्-प्राप्तिः केवल्याद् अतिरिच्यते ।  
अथवा सर्वथा मोक्षः कैवल्यं कमला-पतेः ॥

प्रत्यगात्म-स्वरूपं हि तच्-शेषत्वैक-लक्षणम् ।  
तथा शास्त्रेण विज्ञातं तथा चैवावलोकितम् ॥

Translation: Therefore, the attainment of the Supreme Lord is utterly distinct from (and superior to) Kaivalya. Alternatively, Kaivalya is entirely the ultimate liberation of reaching the Lord of Kamalā (Lakshmi). For the true nature of the inner self (Pratyagātman) is characterized solely by being a subsidiary/servant (Śeṣatva) to Him. It is known as such through the scriptures, and it is realized exactly as such.

तथैव चाभिलक्ष्येत प्राप्येत च तथैव च ।  
विशेषणानुकूल्यञ्च न विशिष्टानुकूलताम् ॥

विहन्ति प्रत्युतैतस्या उपचायकम् एव तत् ।  
आजान-सिद्ध-विज्ञान-प्रसरे चानिवारिते ॥

Translation: It should be aimed for in exactly that way, and attained in exactly that way. And the agreeableness of the attribute (the individual soul) does not destroy the agreeableness of the Qualified (the Supreme Lord). On the contrary, it only serves to enhance it. When the expansion of one's innate, natural knowledge is unobstructed (in the liberated state),

आनुकूल्य-तिरोधानम् अशेषेष्व् अप्रमाणकम् ।  
कथञ्चापार-करुणा-निधिर् आत्म-प्रदो हरिः ॥

भक्त्यर्थं यतमानं तु विस्मारयति चेतनम् ।  
उभयोर् अविशेषेण ब्रह्म-प्राप्ति-श्रुताव् अपि ॥

Translation: the idea that its favorable disposition (towards God) is concealed in all respects is completely devoid of authoritative proof. And how could Lord Hari, who is a boundless ocean of mercy and the bestower of Himself, ever cause a conscious being who is actively striving for devotion to forget Him? Even though the attainment of Brahman is declared in the Shruti without distinction for both,

आकार-भेद-वाच्यत्व-कृत्स्न-वैरूप्य-दूषिता ।  
कान्ते कमल-वासिन्याः केवलोपायता मतिः ॥

आत्मावलोकनाद् अर्वाङ् न तदात्मानुकूल्य-धीः ।  
यदा च सा तदा प्रीतिर् जायते भगवत्य् अपि ॥

Translation: postulating that they are expressed through a difference in forms is tainted by inconsistency. The understanding that the Beloved of the Lotus-dweller (Narayana) is merely the sole means—before the realization of the self, there is no cognition of its favorableness to Him. And when that (self-realization) occurs, then love is naturally born for the Supreme Lord as well.

[[P198]] न्यायकुलिशे

केवलोपायता-बुद्धिर् न तत्रोपपद्यते ।  
यावद् भोग्यत्व-सम्बन्धः परस्मिन् नोपलभ्यते ॥

तावज् जीवात्म-निष्ठायाः पर-विद्याङ्गता-स्थितिः ।  
यदा परस्मिन् भोग्यत्वं प्रकर्षेणानुपश्यति ॥

तदा तत्-किङ्कर-स्वात्म-विद्यैव पर-वेदनम् ।  
अतो भेदोपचारेण ब्रह्म-प्राप्ति-गति-श्रुतिः ॥

Translation: Therefore, the notion of Him being merely a means does not hold true there. As long as the relationship of Him being the supreme object of enjoyment is not perceived in the Supreme Lord, until then, being fixed on the individual soul remains merely a subsidiary part (Aṅga) of the Supreme Knowledge (Para-Vidyā). When one excellently perceives that the Supreme Lord is the ultimate object of enjoyment, then the knowledge of one's own self as His servant is itself the knowledge of the Supreme. Therefore, the Shruti regarding the path and the attainment of Brahman (for the Kevalin) uses a metaphorical designation of difference.

भक्ति-विज्ञान-निष्ठाभ्यां चिन्तनीयं पृथग्-बलम् ।  
अधिकारि-व्यवस्थ-अर्थम् इदानीन्तनम् ईरितम् ॥

यथा फलैकता-शब्दो न्यायश् चानन्यथा-स्थितः ।  
न तथा फल-भेदार्थ-शब्दा न्यायाश् च सन्ति नः ॥

Translation: The distinct strengths of steadfastness in devotion (Bhakti) and knowledge (Vijñāna) must be contemplated. What has been stated just now is for the purpose of establishing the respective eligibilities (of different seekers). Just as the scriptural statement declaring the unity of the fruit and its underlying logic remain irrefutable, there are no such statements or logic for us that prove a difference in the ultimate fruit.

अयम् एव पक्षस् समीचीन इति भगवद्-भाष्यकाराभिप्राय इत्य् आहुः ॥

Translation: They declare that this view alone is correct and appropriate, and this is the true intention of the venerable Commentator (Bhagavad Rāmānujācārya).
