**॥ सज्जन-वैभवम् ॥**

रामानुज-दया-पात्रं ज्ञान-वैराग्य-भूषणम् ।  
श्रीमद्-वेङ्कटनाथार्यं वन्दे वेदान्त-देशिकम् ॥

## प्रतिज्ञा
सर्वाधीश्वर-लक्ष्मीश-  
सपर्या-निरतात्मनाम् ।  
सतां वैखानसाख्यानां  
सम्भवो ऽत्र निरूपयते ॥

इह खलु जगति  
कमलारमण-चरणारविन्द-द्वन्द्व- परिचर्या-परायणां,  
परित्यक्त-विधि-शर्वाद्य्-आर्वाचीन-देवतानां,  
निखिल-नैगमिक-धर्म-निर्वहण-प्रवणानाम्  
अखिल-भूसुर-परिषद्-भूषणायमानानां श्री-वैखानसानां  
प्रत्यक्ष-जन्मानि प्रयुक्तम् अपरिमितं वैभवं  
श्रुति-स्मृतीतिहास-पुराणादिषु बहुशो जोघुष्यते ।  
तद् एतद् अनभिज्ञ-जन-प्रबोधनाय संक्षेपेण संदृश्यते । 

<details><summary>English</summary>

In this world, the immense glory of the Vaikhanasa community—those who are birthright devotees dedicated to the service of the lotus feet of Lord Vishnu (Kamalaramana) and who distance themselves from the worship of lesser deities—is loudly proclaimed throughout the Vedas, Smritis, Itihasas, and Puranas. These individuals are the very ornaments of the scholarly assembly, committed to upholding the entirety of Vedic dharma. To enlighten those who may be unaware of this greatness, a brief overview is presented here. 
</details>


## श्रुतिः
तत्र श्रुतिस् तावत् - तैत्तिरीयके समाम्नायते - 

> **“आपो॒ वा इ॒दम् आ॑सन्थ् सलि॒लम् ए॒व । स प्र॒जाप॑तिर् एकः॑ पुष्कर-प॒र्णे सम॑भवत् । तस्यान्त॒र् मन॑सि काम॒स् सम॑वर्तत । इदँ सृ॑जेय॒म् इति॑ ।” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यक १.२३.१)** 

इत्य् आरभ्य 

> “स तपो ऽतप्यत । स तप॑स् त॒प्त्वा । शरी॑रम् अधूनुत । तस्य॒ यन् मां॒॒सम् आसीत् । ततो॑ ऽरु॒णाः के॒तवो॒ वात॑रश॒ना ऋष॑य॒ उद॑तिष्ठन्न् । ये नखाः । ते वै॑खान॒साः । ये वालाः । ते वा॑लखि॒ल्याः ।” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यके १.२३.२-३) 

इत्यादि ।

<details><summary>English</summary>

Evidence from the Vedas - The Taittiriya Aranyaka (1.23.1) describes the origins of creation: "In the beginning, all this was only water. Prajapati alone appeared upon a lotus leaf. Within his mind, a desire arose: 'Let me create this universe.'" The text continues to explain that Prajapati performed intense penance. Upon completing this penance, he shook his physical form. From his flesh arose the Rishis known as the Arunas, Ketus, and Vatarashanas. The specific origin of the Vaikhanasas is then revealed: "From his nails (nakha) emerged the Vaikhanasas; from his hair emerged the Valakhilyas." (Taittiriya Aranyaka 1.23.2-3).
</details>




अत्र निखिल-जगद्-एक-कारण-भूत-- भगवन्-नारायण-नाभी-कमल-सम्भूतस्य प्रजापतेर् मानसाद्  
वैखानसादीनां सृष्टिर् अभिधीयते । 

नारायण एव निखिल-जगद्-एक-कारणम् इत्य् अयम् अर्थः  
कथं निर्णीयते 
इति चेत् इत्थं, कारण-वाक्येषु छान्दोग्ये तावत्, **“सद् एव सोम्येदमग्र आसीद् एकम् एवाद्वितीयं” (छान्दोग्य उपनिषद् ६.२.१)** इति जगत्-कारणं सच्-शब्देन प्रतिपाद्यते । सच्-शब्दो हि सामान्य-रूपं ब्रह्मणः । बृहद्-रूप-सत्ता-योगि (निर्दिशति) सर्वदा स्वार्थ-साधारण्येन तस्य लोक-वेदयोः प्रयोगात् । तेन [[नाथं|नार्थ-]]विशेषोपस्थापनं कर्तुं प्रभवति । अतः तदर्थ-विशेष-जिज्ञासायां बृहदारण्यके ब्रह्म-वाक्ये, **“इदम् एकम् एवासीद्” (बृहदारण्यक उपनिषद् १.४.३)** इति जगत्-कारणं ब्रह्म-शब्देन निर्दिश्यते । ब्रह्म-शब्दो हि ब्रह्मत्त्व-गुण-योगि-वस्तु-वाचित्वेन सच्-शब्दापेक्षया विशेष-रूपः, अतः सच्-शब्दो ब्रह्म-शब्दार्थे पर्यवस्यति । सर्व-शाखा-प्रत्यय-न्यायेन सर्वेषां कारण-वाक्यानां [[ऐकाथ्येस्यावश्याभ्युपगन्तव्यत्वात्|ऐकार्थ्यस्यावश्याभ्युपगन्तव्यत्वात्]], नात्रापि पश्वधिकरण-न्यायेन सामान्य-शब्दानां समान-प्रकरणोदित-विशेष-पर्यवसानम् [[वश्यं भावाच्चा|अवश्यंभावाच्च]] । पश्वधिकरणे हि, **“पशुना यजेत” (कृष्ण-यजुर्वेद ६.१.११)**, इत्य् अत्र पशु-सामान्य-वाचि-पशु-शब्दः, **“छागस्य वपाया मेधसो” (तैत्तिरीय-ब्राह्मणम् ३.६.८)** इत्य् उक्ते छाग-रूप-पशु-विशेषे पर्यवस्यति । तन् न [[यश्रछाशो|यच्छागो]] वा मन्त्र-वर्णाद् इति । एवं सच्-शब्दो ऽपि ब्रह्म-शब्दार्थे पर्यवस्यति । किञ्च **“अन्नं ब्रह्मेति व्यजानात् । प्राणो ब्रह्मेति व्यजानात् । मनो ब्रह्मेति व्यजानात् । विज्ञानं ब्रह्मेति व्यजानात् ।” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यक ५.१५.२-५)** इत्यादि-ब्रह्म-शब्दस्यापि चेतनाचेतन-वस्तुषु [[प्रयोशदर्शनात्|प्रयोग-दर्शनात्]] सामान्य-रूपस्य विशेषाकाङ्क्षायाम् **“आत्मा वा इदम् एक एवाग्र आसीत्” (ऐतरेय उपनिषत् १.१)** इतीदं कारण-वाक्यं चैतन्य-गुण-योगिनो वस्तुनः कारणत्वं प्रतिपादयति । तस्माद् अस्मिन्न् आत्म-शब्दे ब्रह्म-शब्दः पर्यवस्यति । तस्यापि सर्व-चेतन-वाचकत्व-साधारण्यात् सामान्यत्वम् अस्ति तस्य विशेषाकाङ्क्षायां **“एको ह वै नारायण आसीत् न ब्रह्मा, नेशानो, नेमे द्यावा-पृथिवी, न नक्षत्राणि, नापो, नाग्निर् न सोमो, न सूर्यः स एकाकी न रमेत” (महोपनिषद्)** इतीदं कारण-वाक्यं [[अनन्यशामित्वे|अनन्य-शेषित्वे]] नात्यन्तासाधारण्यात्, सामान्य-शङ्का-रहितेन नारायण-शब्देन कारणं प्रतिपादयति, तस्माद् आत्म-शब्दो ऽपि नारायण-शब्दे पर्यवस्यति । किञ्च न ब्रह्मेत्यादिना कारणत्वेन शङ्कितानां ब्रह्मादीनां तदानीम् अविद्यमानत्व-प्रतिपादनेनात्रानुपात्तान्य् अपि कारण-वाक्यानि नारायण-शब्दार्थ एव पर्यवस्यन्तीति [[शम्यते|गम्यते]] । अस्मिन् काले ब्रह्मादीनाम् अविद्यमानत्वं नारायण-शब्द-वाच्यस्यैकस्य वस्तुनो विद्यमानत्वं चोपबृंहण-वाक्यम् अपि व्यक्तं वदति । **“ब्रह्मादिषु प्रलीनेषु नष्टे स्थावर-जङ्गमे । एकस् तिष्ठति विश्वात्मा स तु नारायणः प्रभुः”** इति ।


Here, the process of creation from the mind of Prajapati (Brahmā), who arose from the lotus in the navel of Lord Nārāyaṇa—the one cause of the entire universe—is described. The question of how it is determined that Nārāyaṇa alone is the one cause of the entire universe is explained as follows: First, in the causal statements of the Chāndogya Upaniṣad, the cause of the world is indicated by the word "sat" (existence): "sadeva somyedamagra āsīdekamevādvitīyam" (Chāndogya Upaniṣad 6.2.1). The word "sat" denotes Brahman in its general aspect, as it signifies pure existence, which is common to everything. Its use in both worldly and Vedic contexts points to its universal meaning. Therefore, it can convey the general nature but not the specific aspect of the Supreme Lord. Hence, to inquire into that specific meaning, in the causal statement of the Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad, the cause of the world is denoted by the word "Brahman": "idamekamevāsīt" (Bṛhadāraṇyaka Upaniṣad 1.4.3). The word "Brahman" is more specific than "sat," as it denotes an entity endowed with the quality of greatness. Thus, the word "sat" culminates in the meaning of the word "Brahman." This is necessarily accepted because all causal statements must have a unified meaning, following the principle of coherence across all scriptural branches. Moreover, similar to the principle seen in the "paśvadhikaraṇa" (topic of the sacrificial animal), specific words used in a general sense resolve into a specific meaning indicated within the same context. For example, in the "paśunā yajeta" (Kṛṣṇa Yajurveda 6.1.11), the general word "paśu" (animal) culminates in the specific meaning of a goat, as indicated by a complementary text such as "chāgasya vapāyā medhaso" (Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa 3.6.8). Similarly, the word "sat" culminates in the meaning of "Brahman." Furthermore, since the word "Brahman" is also seen used for both sentient and insentient objects (as in statements like "annaṃ brahmeti vyajānāt," "prāṇo brahmeti vyajānāt," etc. from Taittirīya Āraṇyaka 5.15.2-5), it still retains a general nature. When there is a desire for further specificity regarding this general form, the causal statement "ātmā vā idam eka evāgra āsīt" (Aitareya Upaniṣad 1.1) establishes that the cause is an entity endowed with consciousness. Therefore, in this context, the word "Brahman" culminates in the meaning of the word "Ātman" (the Self). Since the word "Ātman" also has the commonality of referring to all individual selves, it too is general. When there is a desire for its specific aspect, the causal statement "eko ha vai nārāyaṇa āsīt na brahmā neśāno neme dyāvāpṛthivī na nakṣatrāṇi nāpo 'gnirna somo na sūryaḥ sa ekākī na ramet" (Mahopaniṣad) establishes the cause with the word "Nārāyaṇa," which is free from any doubt of commonality due to its unique supremacy. Thus, the word "Ātman" also culminates in the meaning of the word "Nārāyaṇa." Moreover, by stating "na brahmā" (not Brahmā), etc., this text shows that Brahmā and other beings, who could be suspected of being the cause, did not exist at that time. Therefore, all other causal statements, even those not explicitly cited here, culminate in the meaning of the word "Nārāyaṇa." This is established. The supplemental verse also clearly declares that when Brahmā and others are dissolved and all movable and immovable beings are destroyed, the one Universal Self who remains is the Lord Nārāyaṇa: "brahmādiṣu pralīneṣu naṣṭe sthāvarajaṅgame | ekastiṣṭhati viśvātmā sa tu nārāyaṇaḥ prabhuḥ ||"


किञ्च नारायणानुवाके - **“ना॒राय॒ण-प॑रं ब्र॒ह्म॒ त॒त्त्वं ना॑राय॒णः प॑रः । ना॒राय॒ण-प॑रो ज्यो॒ति॒र् आ॒त्मा ना॑राय॒णः प॑रः ।” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यक ६.११.१)** इति कारण-वस्तु-वाचकयोः ब्रह्मात्म-शब्दयोः नारायण-शब्देन सामानाधिकरण्याद् आभ्याम् उपलक्षण-भूताभ्यां सर्वाणि कारण-वाक्यानि नारायण-शब्दम् एव पर्यवस्यन्तीति ज्ञायते । तस्मान् नारायण एव (सर्व-) जगत्-कारणम् । अस्मिन्न् अनुवाके नारायणस्य **“यच् च॑ कि॒ञ्चिज् ज॑गत्य॒स्मि॒न् दृ॒श्यते श्रूय॒ते ऽपि॑ वा । अन्त॑र् ब॒हिश्र् च॑ तत् स॒र्वं॒ व्याँ॒प्य ना॑राय॒णस् स्थि॑तः ।” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यक ६.११.१)** इति सर्व-व्याप्तिम् उक्त्वा **“प॒द्म॒-को॒श-प्र॑तीका॒श॒ँ॒ हृ॒दय॑ञ् चाप्य॒धो-मु॑खम् ।” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यक ६.११.२)** इत्य् आरभ्य **“तस्याश् शिखा॒या म॑ध्ये प॒रमात्मा व्य॒वस्थि॑तः” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यक ६.११.२)** इत्य् अन्तेन सर्वान्तर्यामित्वं प्रतिपाद्य - **“स ब्रह्मा॒ स शिव॒स् सेन्द्र॒स् सो ऽक्ष॑रः पर॒मस् स्व॒राट्” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यक ६.११.२)** इति ब्रह्मादि-शब्दानां तच्-शब्द-परामृष्ट-नारायण-शब्देन सामानाधिकरण्य-प्रयोगात् तेषां सर्वेषां तत्-तच्-शब्द-वाच्यानां नारायणो ऽन्तर्यामीति विज्ञायते । तस्मात् सर्वान्तर्यामी नारायण एव जगत्-कारणम् । मनुर् अपि नारायणस्यैव जगत्-कारणत्वम् अवदत् । **“आसीद् इदं तमो-भूतम् अप्रज्ञातम् अलक्षणम् । अप्रतर्क्यम् अविज्ञेयं प्रसुप्तम् इव सर्वतः ॥” (मनुस्मृति १.५)**


Furthermore, in the Nārāyaṇānuvāka (the hymn to Nārāyaṇa) it is stated: “nārāyaṇa-paraṃ brahma tattvaṃ nārāyaṇaḥ paraḥ । nārāyaṇa-paro jyotir-ātmā nārāyaṇaḥ paraḥ ॥” (Taittirīya Āraṇyaka 6.11.1) [Translation: "Brahman is subordinate to Nārāyaṇa; the Reality is Nārāyaṇa, the Supreme. The supreme light is Nārāyaṇa; the Self is Nārāyaṇa, the Supreme."] From this, since the words "Brahman" and "Ātman," which denote the causal reality, are used in apposition with the word "Nārāyaṇa," it is understood that all causal statements, represented by these two terms (Brahman and Ātman), culminate in the word "Nārāyaṇa" alone. Therefore, Nārāyaṇa alone is the cause of the entire universe. In this same hymn, after stating the all-pervasiveness of Nārāyaṇa: “yacca kiñcij jagaty asmin dṛśyate śrūyate 'pi vā । antar bahiś ca tat sarvam vyāpya nārāyaṇaḥ sthitaḥ ॥” (Taittirīya Āraṇyaka 6.11.1) [Translation: "Whatever is seen or heard in this universe, within and without—pervading all that, Nārāyaṇa abides."] And beginning with “padmakośa-pratīkāśaṃ hṛdayaṃ cāpy adhomukham” (Taittirīya Āraṇyaka 6.11.2) ["(He who is) facing downwards in the heart, resembling the bud of a lotus..."] up to “tasyāś śikhāyā madhye paramātmā vyavasthitaḥ” (Taittirīya Āraṇyaka 6.11.2) ["In the middle of its flame, the Supreme Self is established"], the status of being the Inner Ruler of all is established. Then it is said: “sa brahmā sa śivaḥ sendraḥ so 'kṣaraḥ paramaḥ svarāṭ” (Taittirīya Āraṇyaka 6.11.2) [Translation: "He is Brahmā, He is Śiva, He is Indra; He is the Imperishable, the Supreme, the Self-luminous."] Here, since the words "Brahmā," etc., are used in apposition with the pronoun "saḥ" ("He"), which refers back to the word "Nārāyaṇa," it is understood that Nārāyaṇa is the Inner Ruler of all those entities denoted by those respective terms (Brahmā, Śiva, etc.). Therefore, Nārāyaṇa, the Inner Ruler of all, is alone the cause of the universe. Manu also declared that Nārāyaṇa alone is the cause of the world: “āsīd idaṃ tamo-bhūtam aprajñātam alakṣaṇam । apratarkyam avijñeyaṃ prasuptam iva sarvataḥ ॥”


इति प्रळयावस्थाम् उक्त्वा - **“ततस् स्वयम्भूर् भगवान् अव्यक्तो व्यञ्जयन्न् इदम् । महा-भूतादि-वृत्तौजाः प्रादुर् आसीत् तमो-नुदः ॥ यो ऽसाव् अतीन्द्रिय-ग्राह्यः सूक्ष्मो ऽव्यक्तस् सनातनः । सर्व-भूत-मयो ऽचिन्त्यः य एष स्स्वयम् उद्बभौ ॥ सो ऽभिध्याय शरीरात् स्वात् सिसृक्षुर् विविधाः प्रजाः । अप एव ससर्जादौ तासु वीर्यम् अपासृजत् ॥ तद् अण्डम् अभवद् धैमं सहस्रांशु-सम-प्रभम् । तस्मिन् जज्ञे स्वयं ब्रह्मा सर्व-लोक-पितामहः । आपो नारा इति प्रोक्ता आपो वै नर-सूनवः ॥ ता यद् अस्यायनं पूर्वं तेन नारायणः स्मृतः । यत् तत् कारणम् अव्यक्तं नित्यं सदसदात्मकम् ॥ तद्-विसृष्टस् स पुरुषो लोके ब्रह्मेति कीर्त्यते ॥” (मनुस्मृति १.६-१.११)** इति मनुना नारायणस्य जगत्-कारणत्वम् उक्तम् । श्री-विष्णु-पुराणे - **“यज्ञियं च जगद् ब्रह्मन् यतश् चैतच् चराचरम् । लीनम् आसीद् यथा यत्र लयम् एष्यति यत्र च ॥” (विष्णु पुराणः १.१.५)** इति मैत्रेयेण पृष्टो भगवान् पराशरः – **“विष्णोः सकाशाद् उद्भूतं जगत् तत्रैव च स्थितम् । स्थिति-संयम-कर्तासौ जगतो ऽस्य जगच् च सः ॥” (विष्णुपुराणम् १.१.३१)** इति सङ्ग्रहेण प्रश्नस्योत्तरं वदन् सृष्टेर् आदिना रमादौ विष्णु-शब्देनोक्त्वा तदनन्तरं महदादि-पञ्च-भूत-पर्यन्त-समष्टि-सृष्टिम् उक्त्वा व्यष्टि-सृष्टि-विवक्षुः - **“नारायणाख्यो भगवान् ब्रह्मा लोक-पितामहः ॥ उत्पन्नः प्रोच्यते विद्वन् नित्यम् एवोपचारतः ।” (विष्णुपुराणम् १.३.३-४)** इति भगवान् नारायण एव ब्रह्मा समभूद् इत्य् अवदत् । अत्र - **“तद्-विसृष्टः स पुरुषो लोके ब्रह्मेति कीर्त्यते” (मनुस्मृति १.११)** । **“नारायणाख्यो भगवान् ब्रह्मा लोक-पितामहः ॥ उत्पन्नः प्रोच्यते ब्रह्मन् नित्य एवोपचारतः” (विष्णुपुराणम् १.३.३-४)** इति ।


Having thus described the state of dissolution, Manu states the creation, beginning from Narayana:"tataḥ svayambhūr bhagavān avyakto vyañjayan idam । mahābhūtādi-vṛttaujāḥ prādurāsīt tamo-nudaḥ ॥ yaḥ asāv atīndriya-grāhyaḥ sūkṣmo 'vyaktaḥ sanātanaḥ । sarva-bhūta-mayo 'cintyaḥ ya eṣaḥ svayam udbabhau ॥ so 'bhidhyāya śarīrāt svāt sisṛkṣur vividhāḥ prajāḥ । apa eva sasarjādau tāsu vīryam apāsṛjat ॥ tad aṇḍam abhavad dhaimaṃ sahasrāṃśu-samaprabham । tasmin jajñe svayaṃ brahmā sarva-loka-pitāmahaḥ ॥ āpo nārā iti proktā āpo vai nara-sūnavaḥ ॥ tā yad asyāyanaṃ pūrvaṃ tena nārāyaṇaḥ smṛtaḥ ॥ yat tat kāraṇam avyaktaṃ nityaṃ sad-asad-ātmakam ॥ tād vā sṛṣṭaḥ sa puruṣo loke brahmeti kīrtyate ॥" (Manusmṛti 1.6-1.11) [Translation: "Then, the self-existent Lord, the unmanifest, making this (universe) manifest, the one whose power consists of the great elements etc., appeared, dispelling the darkness. He who is beyond the grasp of the senses, subtle, unmanifest, eternal, consisting of all beings, inconceivable, He Himself shone forth. Desiring to create various beings from His own body, He first created the waters alone and placed His seed in them. That (seed) became a golden egg, radiant like a thousand suns. In that (egg) was born Brahmā Himself, the grandfather of all worlds. The waters are called nārāḥ, and the waters are indeed the progeny of Nara (the Primordial Man). Since He had His resting place (ayana) in them first, therefore He is remembered as Nārāyaṇa. That which is the cause, the unmanifest, eternal, having the nature of both the manifest and unmanifest—from that, verily, was produced that Person who is celebrated in the world as Brahmā."] Thus, Manu declared Nārāyaṇa to be the cause of the world. In the Śrī Viṣṇu Purāṇa, Maitreya asked Parāśara: "yajñiyañ ca jagad brahman yataś caitac carācaram । līnam āsīd yathā yatra layam eṣyati yatra ca ॥" (Viṣṇu Purāṇa 1.1.5) [Translation: "O Brahmin, from what did this universe, consisting of the moving and non-moving, arise? In what was it dissolved before? And in what will it be dissolved again?"] In reply, the venerable Parāśara, summarizing the answer to the question, first stated with the word 'Viṣṇu' as the source of creation: "viṣṇoḥ sakāśād udbhūtaṃ jagat tatraiva ca sthitam । sthiti-saṃyama-kartāsau jagato 'sya jagac ca saḥ ॥" (Viṣṇu Purāṇa 1.1.31) [Translation: "From Viṣṇu has this universe arisen; in Him it exists; He is the agent of its preservation and dissolution; He is the universe itself."] Then, after describing the collective creation from the mahat-tattva (the great principle) down to the five gross elements, and wishing to describe the individual creation, he said: "nārāyaṇākhyo bhagavān brahmā loka-pitāmahaḥ ॥ utpannaḥ procyate vidvann ityam evopacārataḥ." (Viṣṇu Purāṇa 1.3.3-4) [Translation: "The Lord named Nārāyaṇa Himself became Brahmā, the grandfather of the worlds. He is spoken of as 'born', O wise one, merely as a figurative expression, for He is eternal."] Here (the meaning is): "tad vā sṛṣṭaḥ sa puruṣo loke brahmeti kīrtyate" (Manusmṛti 1.11) – "From that (Nārāyaṇa), verily, was produced that Person who is celebrated in the world as Brahmā." "nārāyaṇākhyo bhagavān brahmā loka-pitāmahaḥ ॥ utpannaḥ procyate brahman nitya evopacārataḥ" – "The Lord named Nārāyaṇa Himself became Brahmā... He is spoken of as 'born', O Brahmin, merely as a figurative expression, for He is eternal."


मनु-पराशर-वचनाभ्यां नारायण एव ब्रह्म-शरीरी भगवान् व्यष्टि-सृष्टिम् अप्य् अकरोद् इत्य् अर्थस् सिध्यति । अतो नारायणस् सर्व-कारणम् । अस्मिन्न् अनुवाके नारायण-शब्द-वाच्यो ऽयं, **“विश्व॑म् ए॒वेदं पुरु॑ष॒” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यक ६.११.१)** इति विश्व-शब्द-समानाधिकृतेन पुरुष-शब्देन प्रतिपादितः । श्री-पुरुष-सूक्ते ऽपि **“सहस्र-शीर्षा पुरुषः” (पुरुषसूक्तम् १)** इत्य् आरभ्य पुरुष एवेदं सर्वम् इति जगत्-सामानाधिकरण्येनोक्तम् । **“अद्भ्यस् सम्भूतः” (महानारायणोपनिषत्)** इत्य् अत्रापि **“विश्वकर्मणस् समवर्तताधि” (महानारायणोपनिषत्)**, **“तस्य त्वष्टा विदधद् रूपम् एति” (महानारायणोपनिषत्)** इति कारणत्वम् उक्त्वा **“तत्-पुरुषस्य विश्वम् आजानम् अग्रे” (महानारायणोपनिषत्)** इति तत्-कारणत्वं पुरुषं महान्तम् । आदित्य-वर्णं तमसः परस्ताद् इति पुरुष-सूक्तोक्त-वाक्योक्त्या तद्-ऐककण्ठ्यं दर्शितम् । अत एव सर्व-शाखा-प्रत्यय-न्यायेन सर्वासूपनिषत्सु नारायण एव सकल-जगत्-कारणत्वेन प्रतिपादितः । स एव पुरुष-शब्द-वाच्य इति च प्रसिद्धम् । अस्य **“ह्रीश् च ते लक्ष्मीश् च पत्न्यौ” (महानारायणोपनिषत् उत्तरनारायणानुवाकः)** इति श्रियः-पतित्वम् अप्य् उक्त्वा **“आपो वा इदम् आसन् सलिलम् एव” (नृसिंह-पूर्व-तापिन्य्-उपनिषत् १.१)** । इत्यादाव् अस्मिन्न् अनुवाके तत्-पुरुषस्य पुरुषत्वम् । **“स सहस्र-शीर्षा पुरुषः । सहस्राक्षः सहस्र-पात् ।** **भूत्वोदतिष्ठद्” (पुरुषसूक्तम्)** इति पुरुष-सूक्तोक्तः पुरुषः प्रतिपादितः । तस्माद् अस्मिन्न् अनुवाके उच्यमाना सृष्टिः श्री-पुरुष-सूक्तोक्त-पुरुष-कृता । अत्र तावत् आदौ **“आपो वा इदम् आसन् सलिलम् एव” (नृसिंह-पूर्व-तापिन्य्-उपनिषत् १.१)** इति समष्टि-सृष्टिर् उक्ता । अप्-शब्दो ऽयं महदाद्य्-अण्ड-पर्यन्त-समष्टि-सृष्टेरुपलक्षणार्थः ।


From the statements of Manu and Parāśara, it is established that Nārāyaṇa Himself, the Lord who is the indwelling Self of Brahmā, also performed the individual creation. Therefore, Nārāyaṇa is the cause of everything. In this hymn (the Nārāyaṇānuvāka), this entity denoted by the word Nārāyaṇa is also presented by the word 'Puruṣa' (the Cosmic Person), which is used in apposition with the word 'Viśva' (the universe): "viśvam evedaṃ puruṣaḥ" (Taittirīya Āraṇyaka 6.11.1) [Translation: "This universe is verily the Puruṣa."] In the Śrī Puruṣasūkta as well, beginning with "sahasraśīrṣā puruṣaḥ" (Puruṣasūkta 1), it is stated with the universe in apposition that the Puruṣa alone is all this. Also in the (Mahānārāyaṇa Upaniṣad): "adbhyaḥ sambhūtaḥ" ["Born from the waters"], "viśvakarmaṇaḥ samavartatādhi" ["He arose over the Viśvakarman (the universal architect)"], "tasya tvaṣṭā vidadha drūpameti" ["His fashioner (Tvaṣṭṛ) determined His form"] – thus stating His causal nature, it is said: "tat puruṣasya viśvam ājānam agre" ["From that Puruṣa, the universe was born in the beginning"], meaning that Great Puruṣa is the cause. The agreement between this and the statement from the Puruṣasūkta – "āditya-varṇaṃ tamasaḥ parastāt" ["Having the splendor of the sun, beyond darkness"] – is shown. Therefore, by the principle of the agreement of all scriptural branches (sarvashākhāpratyayanyāya), it is established that in all the Upaniṣads, Nārāyaṇa alone is presented as the cause of the entire universe. And He is well-known as the referent of the word 'Puruṣa'. It is also stated that He is the consort of Śrī: "hrīś ca te lakṣmīś ca patnyau" (Mahānārāyaṇa Upaniṣad, Uttaranārāyaṇānuvākaḥ) ["Modesty (Hrī) and Prosperity (Lakṣmī) are Your two consorts."]. Furthermore, in passages like "āpo vā idam āsan salilam eva" (Nṛsiṃhapūrvatāpinyupaniṣad 1.1) ["In the beginning, this was water alone, fluid alone"], which are part of this hymn (the Nārāyaṇānuvāka), the 'Puruṣahood' of that Puruṣa is established. The Puruṣa described in the Puruṣasūkta – "sahasraśīrṣā puruṣaḥ sahasrākṣaḥ sahasrapāt . bhūtvodatiṣṭhat" ["The Puruṣa with a thousand heads, a thousand eyes, a thousand feet... having manifested, He stood above (the creation)."] – is presented here. Therefore, the process of creation described in this hymn is the one performed by the Puruṣa extolled in the Śrī Puruṣasūkta. Here, first the collective creation is stated: "āpo vā idam āsan salilam eva" (Nṛsiṃhapūrvatāpinyupaniṣad 1.1). This word 'āpaḥ' (waters) here serves as an indicator for the entire collective creation, from the mahat-tattva (the great principle) up to the cosmic egg.


तद् उक्तं मनुना - **“ततस् स्वयम्भूर् भगवान् अव्यक्तो व्यञ्जयन्न् इदम् । महा-भूतादि-वृत्तौजाः प्रादुर् आसीत् तमो-नुदः ॥ असाव् अतीन्द्रिय-ग्राह्यः सूक्ष्मो ऽव्यक्तस् सनातनः । सर्व-भूत-मयो ऽचिन्त्यः स एकः सर्वम् उद्बभौ ॥ सो ऽभिध्याय शरीरात् स्वात् सिसृक्षुर् विविधाः प्रजाः । अप एव ससर्जादौ तासु वीर्यम् अपासृजत् ॥ तद् अण्डम् अभवद् धैमं सहस्रांशु-सम-प्रभम् ॥” (मनुस्मृतिः १.६-१.९)** इति श्री-विष्णु-पुराणे ऽपि - **“प्रधानं पुरुषञ् चैव प्रविश्यात्मेच्छया हरिः । क्षोभयामास सम्प्राप्ते सर्ग-काले व्ययाव्ययौ ॥” (विष्णुपुराणम् १.२.२९)** इत्य् आरभ्य महदादि-पञ्च-भूत-पर्यन्त-समष्टि-सृष्टिम् उक्त्वा - महदादयो विशेषान्ता ह्य् अण्डम् उत्पादयन्ति (ते) - तैर् अण्ड-सृष्टिम् अप्य् उक्त्वाथ व्यष्टि-सृष्टिं चिकीर्षुः - **“जीव-समष्टि-रूपं प्रजापतिं स्व-नाभि-कमले ससर्ज”** । अयम् अर्थ उच्यते **“स प्रजापतिर् एकः पुष्कर-पर्णे समभवत्” (नृसिंह-पूर्व-तापिन्य्-उपनिषत् १.१)** इति । अयं मनुनाप्य् उच्यते **“तस्मिन् जज्ञे स्वयं ब्रह्मा सर्व-लोक-पितामहः । तस्मिन् पूर्वोक्ते ऽण्डे आपो नारा इति प्रोक्ताः ॥ आपो वै नर-सूनवः । ता यद् अस्यायनं पूर्वं तेन नारायणः स्मृतः । यत् तत् कारणम् अव्यक्तं नित्यं सदसदात्मकम् । तद्-विसृष्टस् स पुरुषो लोके ब्रह्मेति कीर्त्यते” (मनुस्मृतिः १.९-१.११)** इति ॥


That is what was stated by Manu: "tataḥ svayambhūr bhagavān avyakto vyañjayan idam । mahābhūtādi-vṛttaujāḥ prādurāsīt tamo-nudaḥ ॥ asāv atīndriya-grāhyaḥ sūkṣmo 'vyaktaḥ sanātanaḥ । sarva-bhūta-mayo 'cintyaḥ sa eka sarvam udbabhau ॥ so 'bhidhyāya śarīrāt svāt siskṣur vividhāḥ prajāḥ । apa eva sasarjādau tāsu vīryam apāsṛjat ॥ tad aṇḍam abhavadd dhamam sahasrāṃśu-samaprabham ॥" (Manusmṛtiḥ 1.6-1.9) [Translation: "Then the self-existent Lord, the unmanifest, making this (universe) manifest, the one whose energy consists of the great elements etc., appeared, dispelling the darkness. He who is beyond the grasp of the senses, subtle, unmanifest, eternal, consisting of all beings, inconceivable — He alone shone forth as everything. Desiring to create various beings from His own body, He first created the waters alone and placed His seed in them. That (seed) became a golden egg, radiant like a thousand suns."] In the Śrī Viṣṇu Purāṇa as well, beginning with: "pradhānaṃ puruṣañ caiva praviśyātmaicchayā hariḥ । kṣobhayāmāsa samprāpte sarga-kāle vyayāvyayau ॥" (Viṣṇu Purāṇam 1.2.29) [Translation: "At the time of creation, Hari, entering Prakṛti (Primordial Nature) and Puruṣa (the Cosmic Person) by His own will, agitated the mutable and immutable principles."] the collective creation from the mahat-tattva down to the five gross elements is described. It is stated that the mahat etc., culminating in the specific elements, produce the cosmic egg. Having described that creation of the egg, and now desiring to describe the individual creation, it says: "jīva-samṣṭi-rūpaṃ prajāpatiṃ sva-nābhi-kamale sasarja" ["He created Prajāpati, the collective form of individual souls, in the lotus of His navel."]. This meaning is expressed in (the Nṛsiṃhapūrvatāpinyupaniṣad): "sa prajāpatir ekaḥ puṣkara-parṇe samabhavat" (1.1) ["That one Prajāpati came into being on a lotus leaf."]. This is also stated by Manu: "tasmin jajñe svayaṃ brahmā sarva-loka-pitāmahaḥ । tasmin pūrvokte 'ṇḍe āpo nārā iti proktāḥ ॥ āpo vai nara-sūnavaḥ । tā yad asyāyanaṃ pūrvaṃ tena nārāyaṇaḥ smṛtaḥ । yat tat kāraṇam avyaktaṃ nityaṃ sad-asad-ātmakam । tad visṛṣṭaḥ sa puruṣo loke brahmeti kīrtyate" (Manusmṛtiḥ 1.9-1.11) [Translation: "In that aforementioned egg, Brahmā Himself, the grandfather of all worlds, was born. The waters are called nārāḥ, and the waters are indeed the progeny of Nara (the Primordial Man). Since He had His resting place (ayana) in them first, therefore He is remembered as Nārāyaṇa. That which is the cause, the unmanifest, eternal, having the nature of both the manifest and unmanifest — from that, verily, was produced that Person who is celebrated in the world as Brahmā."]


ब्रह्माण्डे - श्री-रङ्ग-माहात्म्ये च **आसीद् इदं तमो-भूतम् अप्रज्ञातम् अलक्षणम् । [[अप्रसुप्तमविज्ञेयं|अप्रसुप्तम् अविज्ञेयं]] प्रसुप्तम् इव सर्वतः ॥ एक एवाभवत् तस्मिन् देवो नारायणः प्रभुः । स जगत्-सृष्टि-संहारैर् विकर्तुम् अकरोन् मनः ॥ स्वांशांश-कलया लक्ष्म्या स्वया मूर्त्या जगन्-मयः । स्व-कलानन्त-शयने [[विशिश्ये|विशिश्रिये]] प्रळयोदधिम् ॥ तस्य कामस् समजनि मनसो वीर्यम् आदितः । तदाविष्टस् स्व-देहे तु सो ऽपश्यत् सचराचरम् ॥ तस्य नाभेर् अभून् [[नाकं|नाळं]] नाना-रत्न-मनोहरम् । तस्मिन् हिरण्मयं पद्मं सर्व-गन्ध-मधूत्कटम् ॥ तस्मिन् चतुर्मुखो ब्रह्मा सङ्कल्पात् परमात्मनः । सृष्टिस् तु सर्व-लोकानाम् आसील् लोक-पितामहः ॥ (ब्रह्माण्डे - श्री-रङ्ग-माहात्म्ये)** इति ब्रह्मा-सृष्टिर् उक्ता । श्रीमद्-उत्तर-रामायणे **“पद्मे दिव्ये ऽर्क-सङ्काशे नाभ्याम् उत्पाद्य माम् अपि । प्राजापत्यं त्वया कर्म सर्वं मयि निवेशितम् ॥” (रामायणम् ७.१०४.७)**


In the Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa (Śrī Raṅgamāhātmya): "āsīd idaṃ tamo-bhūtam aprajñātam alakṣaṇam । aprasuptam avijñeyaṃ prasuptam iva sarvataḥ ॥ eka evābhavat tasmin devo nārāyaṇaḥ prabhuḥ । sa jagat-sṛṣṭi-saṃhārair vikartum akarot manaḥ ॥ svāṃśāṃśa-kalayā lakṣmyā svayā mūrtyā jagan-mayaḥ । sva-kalā-nanta-śayane viśiśye praḷayodadhim ॥ tasya kāmaḥ samajani manaso vīryam āditaḥ । tad āviṣṭaḥ sva-dehe tu so 'paśyat sa-carācaram ॥ tasya nābher abhūn nālaṃ nānā-ratna-manoharam । tasmin hiraṇmayaṃ padmaṃ sarva-gandha-madhūtkaṭam ॥ tasmin caturmukho brahmā saṅkalpāt paramātmanaḥ । sṛṣṭis tu sarva-lokānām āsīl loka-pitāmahaḥ ॥" (Brahmāṇḍe - Śrī Raṅgamāhātmye) [Translation: "This universe existed as unmanifest darkness—unperceived, without distinguishing marks, unsleeping yet as if asleep, unknowable in every way. In that (state), the one Lord, the God Nārāyaṇa, alone existed. He resolved within His mind to effect the creation, preservation, and dissolution of the world. By His own will, consisting of His partial manifestations and portions, along with Lakṣmī, by His own form that pervades the universe, He entered the ocean of dissolution, upon the bed of His own infinite power (Śeṣa). From His mind, desire first arose as creative energy. Possessed by that (energy) within His own body, He beheld the entire universe, both moving and non-moving. From His navel arose a stalk, delightful with various gems. On that (stalk) was a golden lotus, fragrant with every perfume. In that (lotus), by the will of the Supreme Self, four-faced Brahmā came into being. The creation of all the worlds proceeded from him, the grandfather of the worlds."] Thus, the creation of Brahmā is described. Also, in the Śrīmad Uttara Rāmāyaṇa: "padme divye 'rka-saṅkāśe nābhyām utpādya mām api । prajāpatyaṃ tvayā karma sarvaṃ mayi niveśitam ॥" [Translation: "Having produced me also in the divine lotus, resplendent as the sun, (arising) from the navel, You have placed in me all the activity of creation (prajāpatya karma)."]


इति प्रजापतिना भगवन्-नाभि-कमलात् स्व-सृष्टिर् उक्ता । व्यष्टि-करणाय प्रजापति-पदे स्वस्य स्थापनञ् चोक्तम् । तस्मात् व्यष्टि-सृष्टिं कर्तुं स्व-सङ्कल्पम् अकरोद् इत्य् उच्यते । **“तस्यान्तर् मनस् कामस् समवर्तत । इदं सृजेयम् इति”** । **(नृसिंह-पूर्व-तापिन्य्-उपनिषत् १.१)** एवं सङ्कल्प-पूर्वं सृष्टिः क्रियत इति । तद्-अर्थम् उपपादयति - **“तस्माद् यत् पुरुषो मनसाभिगच्छति” (नृसिंह-पूर्व-तापिन्य्-उपनिषत् १.१)** इत्यादिना **“य एवं वेदेत्य् अन्तेन वाक्य-जातेनैव कृत-सङ्कल्प-सिसृक्षुस् स प्रजाः,”** यथा मनोरथं – सर्वाः प्रजाः स्रष्टुं शक्याभावात् तल्-लाभाय स्व-कारण-भूतं पुरुषं तपसा समाराधयामासेत्य् उच्यते - **“स तपो ऽतप्यत” (नृसिंह-पूर्व-तापिन्य्-उपनिषत् १.२)** इति । ब्रह्माण्डे **“ओम् इत्य् एकाक्षरं ब्रह्म व्याहरन् संस्मरन् हरिम् । पद्मासन-स्थो भगवान् परमं तप आस्थितः ॥”**


Thus, the creation by Prajāpati (Brahmā) from the lotus in the navel of the Lord is described. The establishment of Himself in the position of Prajāpati for the purpose of individual creation is also stated. Therefore, it is said that He formed the resolve to perform the individual creation. "tasyāntar manas kāmaḥ samavartataḥ । idaṃ sṛjeyam iti" (Nṛsiṃhapūrvatāpinyupaniṣat 1.1) [Translation: "Within Him (the Lord), desire arose in the mind: 'Let Me create this.'"] Thus, it is established that creation is preceded by a resolve (saṅkalpa). To explain the means for that (creation), a series of statements beginning with "tasmād yat puruṣo manasā 'bhigacchati" (Nṛsiṃhapūrvatāpinyupaniṣat 1.1) ["Therefore, whatever a person approaches with the mind..."] and ending with "ya evaṃ veda" (Nṛsiṃhapūrvatāpinyupaniṣat) ["He who knows thus"] describes that the One (Prajāpati) who had formed the resolve and desired to create beings, realizing that all types of beings could not be created merely by wishing for them, performed austerities to propitiate the Puruṣa (the Supreme Person) who is His own source, in order to attain that ability. This is stated as: "sa tapo 'tapyata" (Nṛsiṃhapūrvatāpinyupaniṣat 1.2) [Translation: "He performed austerity."] And in the Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa "om ity ekākṣaraṃ brahma vyāharan saṃsmaran harim । padmāsanastho bhagavān paramaṃ tapa āsthitaḥ ॥" [Translation: "The Lord, seated in the lotus posture, uttering and meditating upon the single-syllabled Brahman 'Om' and upon Hari, engaged in the highest austerity."]


इतीदं तपश्-चरणम् उक्तम्, एवं तपसा भगवन्तम् उपास्य **“स तपस् तप्त्वा । शरीरम् अधूनुत” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यके १.२३.२)** इत्य् उक्त्वा, अत्र शरीरम् अधूनुतेत्य् आरभ्य **“ते वालखिल्या” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यके १.२३.३)** इत्य् अन्तस्य वाक्यस्य केचिद् अर्थम् ऊचुः । प्रजापतिवद् आसिसृक्षुः स्व-शरीर-विधूननम् अकरोत् । अनन्तरं तस्य यन् मांसम् आसीद् इति । विधूताच् शरीरात् यन् मांसं विशीर्णम् अभवत् । **“ततो ऽरुणाः केतवो वातरशना ऋषय उदतिष्ठन्” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यके १.२३.२-३)** इति विशीर्णात् तन्-मांसाद् अरुणादय ऋषयः उदतिष्ठन् । **“ये नखाः । तै वैखानसाः” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यके १.२३.३)** इति विशीर्णेभ्यो नखेभ्यो वैखानसा ऋषयः उदतिष्ठन् । वातरशना ऋषय उदतिष्ठन् इति विशीर्णात् तन्-मांसाद् अरुणादय ऋषय उदतिष्ठन् । **“ये नखाः । ते वैखानसाः” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यके १.२३.३)** इति विशीर्णेभ्यो नखेभ्यो वैखानसा उदतिष्ठन् । **“ये वालाः । ते वालाखिल्याः” । (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यके १.२३.३)** इति विशीर्णेभ्यो वालेभ्यो वालखिल्या उदतिष्ठन् इति । एवम् अर्थ-स्वीकरणे **“तस्य यल् माँसम् आसीद्” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यके १.२३.२)** इति तस्मात्-प्रजापति-शरीरात् पृथग्-भूतं मांसं यच्-छब्द-सहितम् उक्त्वा तत इति पञ्चम्यन्ते मांसः परामृश्यते । **“अरुणाः केतवो वातरशना ऋषय उदतिष्ठन्न् इति” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यके १.२३.३)** । तस्मान् मांसाद् एतेषाम् उत्पत्तिर् उक्ता । एवं **“ये नखा” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यके १.२३.३)** इत्य् अमुकेभ्यो वैखानसा उदतिष्ठन् । **ये वालास् तेभ्यो वालाखिल्या** उदतिष्ठन्न् इति । तस्माद् वैखानसा वालखिल्यास् ताभ्य उदतिष्ठन् इत्य् अनुक्ते तेषां तेभ्यः उत्पत्तिर् वक्तुं न शक्यते । नैव **“बहुलं छन्दसी” (अष्टाध्यायी २.४.३९)** इति न्यायात् तेभ्य इत्य् उभयत्र छान्दसेन विभक्ति-व्यत्ययनात्रेण तयोः पूर्ववद् उपादानत्व-सम्पत्तेर् उक्तार्थ-स्वीकारस् सुकर इति चेन् न । एवम् अर्थ-स्वीकरणे व्यष्टि-कारण-भूतस्य ब्रह्म-शरीरस्य शैथिल्यम् उक्तं स्यात् । अरुणादीनां केवलं मांस-मात्रत्वात् । वैखानसादीनाम् अस्थि-मात्रत्वं, वालखिल्यानां वाल-मात्रत्वञ् च प्रतीयते । किञ्च अरुणादीनां महर्षीणां अमृत-शब्द-वाच्यत्व-कारण-भूत-जितेन्द्रियत्वाभावात् तथैव केवल-चेतनत्वञ् च प्रयुज्यते । किञ्च ब्रह्म-शरीरात् पृथग्-भूत-मांसादि-रूपत्वेनाशुद्धत्वम् अप्य् उद्भावयितुं शक्यते । न तथा वक्तव्यम् (प्रपत्ति) प्रजापति-शरीरान् मांसादीनां पृथग्-भावो न वाच्यः । अपृथक्-सिद्धाभ्यस् त एतेषां ब्रह्माभूद् इति वक्तव्यतास्ति । तदानीं शरीरम् अधूनुत । तस्य यन् माँसम् आसीद् इत्य् उक्तिर् न घटते । विधूताच् शरीराद् उदतिष्ठन्न् इति वाक्यं भवेत् । न तथा किमपि वाक्यं प्रतीयते । किमपि वाक्यं प्रतीयते । किञ्च कारण-वाक्येषु कस्मिंश्चिद् वाक्ये वा स्मृतीतिहास-पुराणेषु वा ब्रह्म-शरीर-मांसादिभ्य एवारुणादयो जायन्त इति किमपि वचनं विद्यते चेद् अमम् अर्थः स्वीक्रिपताम् । तदभावात् समीचीनार्थ-सद्भावाच् चायम् अर्थो न वाच्यः । ननु उपदिष्टात् – मम वै त्वङ्-माँसा । समभूद् इति प्रजापतिनोक्तस्य मांस-शब्दस्यार्थो ऽयम् एव । तस्माद् उभयत्रार्थैक्यं स्वीकार्यम् इति चेन् न ।


Thus, the performance of this austerity has been described. Having thus propitiated the Lord through austerity, as stated in "sa tapas taptvā, śarīram adhūnota" [He, having performed austerity, shook His body], starting from this phrase "śarīram adhūnota" [shook His body] up to the concluding phrase "te vālakhilyāḥ" [they are the Vālakhilyas], some have explained the meaning as follows: Prajāpati, desiring to create, shook His own body. Thereafter, from that (body), whatever flesh there was... From the shaken body, whatever flesh fell off. "tato 'ruṇāḥ ketavo vātaraśanā ṛṣaya udatiṣṭhan" — from that scattered flesh, the sages named Aruṇā, Ketava, and Vātaraśanā arose. "Those which were nails (nakhāḥ), from them the Vaikhānasa sages arose." From the scattered nails, the Vaikhānasa sages arose. "vātaraśanā ṛṣaya udatiṣṭhan" — from that scattered flesh, the sages named Aruṇā, etc., arose. "Those which were hairs (vālāḥ), they are the Vālakhilyas." From the scattered hairs, the Vālakhilyas arose. If this meaning is accepted, then after stating "tasya yan māṃsam āsīt" [whatever flesh there was of His] — i.e., the flesh that became separate from Prajāpati's body — using the word "yat" and then referring back to that flesh in the ablative case with "tataḥ" [from that], it is stated: "'aruṇāḥ ketavo vātaraśanā ṛṣaya udatiṣṭhan" [From that (flesh), the sages Aruṇā, Ketava, and Vātaraśanā arose]. Thus, the origin of these sages from that flesh is stated. Similarly, from those (nails) mentioned as "ye nakhāḥ", the Vaikhānasa sages arose; from those hairs mentioned as "ye vālāḥ", the Vālakhilyas arose. Since it is not explicitly stated that the Vaikhānasas and Vālakhilyas arose from them, their origin from those sources cannot be stated. Moreover, by the maxim "bahulaṃ chandasi" (in Vedic language, there is much flexibility), due to the poetic interchange of cases in both instances, the meaning "from them" can be understood, and thus the previously stated meaning of their source can be accepted easily. If so, then this is not correct. If this meaning is accepted, it would imply a looseness or disintegration of Brahmā's body, which is the source of individual creation. It would also suggest that the Aruṇādi sages were merely lumps of flesh, the Vaikhānasas merely bones, and the Vālakhilyas merely hair. Furthermore, it would ascribe to the great sages like Aruṇā, who are denoted by the word 'amṛta' (immortal) and are characterized by perfect sense-control, a nature of being merely sentient flesh. Moreover, one could even impute impurity to them, as having the form of flesh, etc., separated from Brahmā's body. This should not be stated. (Therefore, the proper understanding is:) The separation of flesh, etc., from Prajāpati's body should not be stated. There is a need to state that these sages (Aruṇādi, etc.) are non-different from Brahmā Himself. At that time, the statement "śarīram adhūnota, tasya yan māṃsam āsīt" [He shook His body; whatever flesh there was of His] would not be appropriate. The statement would have to be "vidhūtāc charīrād udatiṣṭhan" [they arose from the shaken body]. No such statement is perceived. Furthermore, if there were any statement in any causal text (of the Vedas) or in Smṛti, Itihāsa, or Purāṇa, stating that the Aruṇādi sages were born from the flesh, etc., of Brahmā's body, then this meaning could be accepted. In the absence of such a statement, and given the existence of a proper meaning, this (literal flesh-and-bones) meaning should not be proposed. Now, if one argues that since it is taught elsewhere (in the Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa) "mama vaitvaṅ māṃsā, samabhūt" [Verily, my skin and flesh became...] spoken by Prajāpati, the meaning of the word 'māṃsa' (flesh) here is the same, and therefore uniformity of meaning in both places should be accepted — this is also not correct.


तदानीं प्रजापतेर् अज्ञान-सम्भवात् । अज्ञानाद् एव खलु प्रजापति स्वस्यापि स्रष्टारं भगवन्तम् आलोक्य तस्य स्व-मांसाद् उत्पत्तिम् अवोचत् । तत एव खलु – नैवम् अब्रवीत् । पूर्वम् एवाहम् इह आसम् इति । तद्-वाक्यं प्रतिषिद्धम् । अस्याभिप्रायः - त्वद्-उत्पत्तेः पूर्वम् एव अहम् इहासम् । त्वां मनसासृजम् । अतस् त्वं मां न सृष्टवान् । तथापि – मम वै त्वङ्-माँसा । समभूद् इत्य् अज्ञेन त्वयोक्तम् इति । अतो मांसादि-शब्दानाम् एवम् उक्तो ऽर्थस् त्यज्यताम् । (इदानीं सज्जन-गृहीतस् समीचीनानेक-प्रमाण-न्याय-सिद्धः कश्चिद् अर्थः श्रूयताम् । **“सद् एव सोम्येदमग्र आसीद्” (छान्दोग्योपनिषद्‌ ६.२.१)** आदौ सर्व-कारण-वस्तु-प्रतिपादन-वाक्ये तदैक्षत । **“बहु स्यां प्रजायेयेति” (छान्दोग्योपनिषद्‌ ६.२.३)** इत्य् एवं रूपेषु कारण-वाक्येषु मानस-सृष्टिः प्रतिपाद्यते । श्री-रङ्ग-माहात्म्ये च - **“आदि-कर्तादितस् सर्वं मनसा निर्ममे पुरा । ब्रह्माण्डे तस्य कामस् समजनि मनसो वीर्यम् आदितः ॥”** इत्य् एवं विधानि वाक्यानि बहूनि मानस-सृष्टिं प्रतिपादयन्ति ।


At that time, it is possible that Prajāpati spoke out of ignorance. Indeed, due to ignorance, Prajāpati, upon seeing the Lord who was his own creator, stated that he (the Lord) arose from his (Prajāpati's) own flesh. It is for this very reason that the Lord refuted him, saying: "Naiva abravīt. Pūrvam evāham ihāsam iti." ["He did not speak correctly. I existed here even before."] The meaning of this statement is: "I existed here even before your origin. I created you by my mind. Therefore, you did not create me." Yet (Prajāpati, due to ignorance, had said) "Mama vai tvaṅ māṃsā samabhūt" ["Verily, my skin and flesh became..."]. Therefore, let the meaning of the words 'skin', 'flesh', etc., spoken by me (Prajāpati) in that manner be disregarded. (Now, let the proper meaning, upheld by the virtuous and established by many scriptural proofs and principles of reasoning, be heard.) In passages like "sadeva somyedamagra āsīt" (Chāndogya Upaniṣad 6.2.1), which state the causal reality of everything, it is said: "tadaikṣata, bahu syāṃ prajāyeyeti" (Chāndogya Upaniṣad 6.2.3) ["It thought: 'Let me become many, let me be born.'"]. In such causal statements, mental creation (mānasa sṛṣṭi) is taught. And in the Śrī Raṅgamāhātmya- "ādikartāditah sarvaṃ manasā nirmame purā" ["The Prime Creator fashioned everything by mind in the beginning."] And in the Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa: "tasya kāmaḥ samajani manaso vīryam āditaḥ" ["From his mind, desire arose as the primal energy."] There are many such statements that teach mental creation.

यद् आसीत्”** इति च । सङ्कल्पारम्भम् आरभ्य मनसो व्यापार उक्तः । अत्रायम् अर्थो ऽवधार्यताम् । **“आकाशाद् वायुः । वायोर् अग्निः” (तैत्तिरीयोपनिषद् २.१)** इत्य् आरभ्य अन्नात् पुरुष इत्य् अन्तेन कारण-परम्परा-प्रतिपादकेषु श्रुति-वाक्येषु पूर्वस्य कार्यत्वम् उत्तरस्य कारणत्वम् इति दृष्टम् । ऋग्वेदोपनिषदि **“अथातो रेतः सृष्टिः । प्रजापते रेतो देवाः । देवानां रेतो वर्षम् । वर्षस्य रेतो ओषधयः । ओषधीनां रेतो ऽन्नम् । अन्नस्य रेतो रेतः । रेतसो रेतः प्रजाः । प्रजानां रेतो हृदयम् । हृदयस्य रेतो मनः । मनसो रेतो वाक् । वाचो रेतः कर्म”** इत्य् अत्रापि पूर्वस्य कार्यत्वम् उत्तरस्य कारणत्वम् अयम् अर्थो दृष्टः । किञ्चात्र पूर्वस्य कार्यत्वम् उत्तरस्य कारणत्वञ् च यद् भवति तद्-रेतश्-शब्देनोक्तम् । तस्मिन् **“मनसो रेतः प्रथमं यद् आसीत्”** इति । अत्र मनसः कार्यत्वान् न रेतश्-शब्देन यद् उक्तं तद् रेतश्-शब्देनोक्तं भवेत् । अतो मनसो रेत इत्य् उक्तं कार्य-कारण-भूतं, मानसं वीर्यम् उच्यते । तद् उक्तं ब्रह्माण्डे - **“तस्य कामस् समजनि मनसो वीर्यम् आदितः** इति ।


Thus, in this hymn (anuvāka) as well, mental creation (mānasa sṛṣṭi) by the Lord is being described. This is illustrated as follows: After stating the collective creation of primordial nature with "āpo vā idam āsan salilam eva" (Nṛsiṃhapūrvatāpinyupaniṣat 1.1) ["In the beginning, this was water alone, fluid alone"], it is said: "sa prajāpatir ekaḥ puṣkara parṇe samabhavat" (Nṛsiṃhapūrvatāpinyupaniṣat 1.1) ["That one Prajāpati came into being on a lotus leaf."]. This describes the manifestation of Prajāpati, the collective form of individual souls, created by the Lord through His own mind, from the lotus in the Lord's navel. This is corroborated by Prajāpati's own statement: "prajāpatyaṃ tvayā karma sarvaṃ mayi niveśitam" ["All the activity of creation has been placed in me by You."]. The Lord established Himself in the position presided over by Prajāpati, for the purpose of individual creation. It is then said that Prajāpati formed the resolve to perform individual creation: "tasyāntar manasi kāmaḥ samavartata, idaṃ sṛjeyam iti" ["Within him (Prajāpati), desire arose in the mind: 'Let me create this.'"]. Beginning with "tasmād yat puruṣo manasā 'bhigacchati" ["Therefore, whatever a person approaches with the mind..."] and concluding with "kāmas tad agre samavartatādhi" (Ṛgveda 10.129.4) ["In the beginning, desire arose upon That (the One)."], this entire section rationally explains the resolve (saṅkalpa) as the first stage. Then, with the phrase "manaso retaḥ prathamaṃ yad āsīt" (Ṛgveda 10.129.4) ["That which was the first seed of mind."], the creative activity of the mind of Prajāpati is indicated by the word 'retaḥ' (seed). Subsequently, from "sato bandhum asati nir avindan" ["They found the bond of existence in non-existence."] up to "ya evaṃ veda" (Māṇḍūkyopaniṣad 9) ["He who knows thus."], it is established that individual creation was not accomplished by Brahman's mental power alone existing at that time. Therefore, for the purpose of attaining the power to perform individual creation through his own mind, Prajāpati performed austerity and propitiated the Lord. This is the meaning. "sa tapas taptvā" — having performed austerity, i.e., having worshipped the Lord, "śarīram adhūnota" — he shook his body. Due to the overwhelming excellence of the Lord's essential form directly realized during the performance of austerity, and due to the joy and majesty arising from attaining the creative power bestowed by the Lord, he shook his body, his entire body thrilled. Now, the meaning of the statement "tasya yan māṃsam āsīt" is explained. 'Māṃsa' means 'mānasa' (that which is of the mind). By the maxim "bahulaṃ chandasi" (Pāṇini 2.4.39), which states that in Vedic language there is much flexibility regarding case-endings, gender, number, time, verbs, and the like, and by the principle that meanings are determined from context and grammatical indications, and also from the rule of Nirukta (etymology) that one may explain words based on similarity of letters (when meaning is determined by context), the word 'māṃsa' here can be understood as 'mānasa' (of/from the mind). Or, by the popular etymological explanation "mano 'smin sīdatīti" ('that in which the mind settles'), the word 'māṃsa' is understood to mean 'mānasa'. Furthermore, consider the sequence: "tasyāntar manasi kāmaḥ samavartata" ["In Him (the Lord), desire arose in the mind."], "tasmād yat puruṣo manasā 'bhigacchati" ["Therefore, whatever a person approaches with the mind..."], and "manaso retaḥ prathamaṃ yad āsīt" ["That which was the first seed of mind."]. Here, starting from the initial resolve, the activity of the mind is described. This meaning should be understood. In Vedic passages like "ākāśād vāyuḥ, vāyor agniḥ" (Taittirīyopaniṣad 2.1) ["From space arose air, from air fire."] up to "annāt puruṣaḥ" ["From food, the person."], which teach a sequence of cause and effect, it is observed that the former is the effect and the latter is the cause. Similarly, in the Ṛgvedic Upaniṣad: "athāto retaḥ sṛṣṭiḥ, prajāpater eta devāḥ..." ["Now, therefore, the creation of seed. The seed of Prajāpati is the gods..."]. Here too, the meaning is understood: the former is the effect and the latter is the cause. Moreover, in such sequences, whatever is the effect of the former and the cause of the latter is denoted by the word 'retaḥ' (seed). Therefore, in "manaso retaḥ prathamaṃ yad āsīt", since the mind is the effect, that which is denoted by the word 'retaḥ' is its cause. Hence, the phrase "manaso retaḥ" means the cause, which is the seed of the mind; it is called the mental potency (mānasaṃ vīryam). This is stated in the Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa: "tasya kāmaḥ samajani manaso vīryam āditaḥ" ["From his mind, desire arose as the primal energy."].


मानव-शास्त्रे च - **“अप एव ससर्जादौ तासु वीर्यम् अपासृजत् । तद् अण्डम् अभवद् धैमं सहस्त्रांशु-सम-प्रभम्” (मनुस्मृति १.७)** इति ॥ सृष्टि-कारणत्वेन प्रमाणान्तरोक्तं मनो-वीर्यम् एव वीर्यम् उच्यते । तस्मान् मानसमित्यनेनोत्तर-कार्योत्पादक-शक्ति-विशेष-युक्तं मानसम् एवोच्यते । - मानसं वै प्राजापत्यम् - इतीदं वाक्यम् अपि प्राजापत्य-शब्देन तत्रोत्तरस्य मांस-शब्दस्य मानसत्वम् एव विवृणोति । किञ्चात्रोक्तः पवित्र-शब्दः प्रजापति-मानसस्य मांस-शब्द-प्रतिपादनात् शङ्कितम् अशुद्धत्वं परिहरति । किञ्च मानसा ऋषय इतीदं वाक्यम् अपि - ततो ऽरुणाः केतवो वातरशना ऋषय उदतिष्ठन्न् इति । ततः उत्थितानाम् ऋषीणां मनसा जातत्वम् एव प्रकटयति । तस्मात् बहु-प्रमाण-पर्यालोचनया मांसं मानसमिति सिद्धम् । किञ्च बहुषु कारण-वाक्येषु मानस-सृष्टेर् एव प्रतिपाद्यत्वात् मांस-सृष्टेः क्वचिद् अप्य् अदृष्टचरत्वाच् च । अत्रापि मांसम् इति मानसम् एवोच्यते । मनः कस्मिंश्चिद् विषये सज्जतीति मानसम् । तस्मात् तस्य प्रजापतेर् मानसं तपश्-चरण-काले भगवति सक्तम् आसीद् इति यत् तेन कारणेन हि भगवत्-प्रसाद-लब्ध-सृष्टि-शक्त्यतिशयायत्तात् । तस्मान् मानसात् भगवता उत्थापिताः प्रजापति-तपः-फल-भूताः अरुणाः केतवो वातरशना इत्य् एवं नामानः ऋषयः उदातिष्ठन् । उभयथापि मनसा जाता इत्य् अर्थः । अरुणाः केतवो वातरशना इति तेषाम् ऋषीणां नामानीति कश्चिन् निर्वाहः । यद्वा - अरुणा इति तेषां तेजो ऽतिशयः उच्यते । अरुण्या रोहितत्वं - **“यद् अग्ने रोहितं रूपं तेजसः तद् रूपम् इति” (छान्दोग्योपनिषद्‌ ६.४.१)** श्रुतिः । तेजस्विन इत्य् अर्थः । यद्वा आदित्योदयात् पूर्वम् एवारुणाः खलु दृश्यन्ते । तस्मात् प्रथमोदयात् तेजस्वित्वाद् अरुणा इत्य् उच्यन्ते । केतु-शब्दो ध्वज-पर्यायः । गरुड-ध्वज इत्यादिषु ध्वज-शब्दः चिह्न-वाचित्वेनोच्यते । तस्मात् तत्-पर्यायः केतु-शब्दः चिह्न-वाचकः । केतवः सर्वेषाम् ऋषीणाम् अभिजन-विद्या-वृत्तादिषु चिह्न-भूताः लक्षण-भूताः । निदर्शन-भूता इत्य् अर्थः । त एक सर्वेभ्य उच्छ्रिता दृश्यन्त इति यावत् । वातरशना - रशनेति प्राणिनाम् अलङ्कार-भूताः केचन पदार्थ-विशेषाः वात-स्वभावाः प्राणिनां रशनावद् अलङ्कार-भूता इति । वातस्य सर्व-प्राणत्व-सर्व-सन्ताप-हरत्व-सर्वाप्यायकत्व-सर्व-विषय-निस्सङ्गत्वादयो ऽनेक-स्वभावा विद्यन्ते । ऋषीणाम् अपि स्वभावः उदतिष्ठन् - उच्चैर् अतिष्ठन् सर्वेषाम् उपर्य् अतिष्ठन् । यद्वा [[ऊर्ध्वशतयः|ऊर्ध्व-गतयः]] स्थिता इत्य् अर्थः । मुमुक्षव इति यावत् । उत्थान-क्रिया तत्-कर्तृ-पूर्विका, पूर्व-सृष्टानाम् एव बुद्धि-पूर्विका सृष्टिर् घटते, न तु सृष्टानाम् । तस्मात् सर्वेषां भगवता पूर्व-सृष्टिस् सिद्धैव । किञ्च ऋषय इत्य् एषां ऋषि-शब्दाभिधेयं दृष्टम् । ऋषित्वञ् च तेषां पूर्व-सृष्टत्वोपपादकत्वं तस्य चिर-काल-सम्पाद्यत्वात् तदानीं सृष्टत्वे तदभावाच् च । तस्मात् भगवता मनसा सृष्टाः । प्रजापतेः तपः-फलत्वेन तस्मान् मानसाद् आविर्भूता ऋषयः उदतिष्ठन्न् इत्य् अर्थः । एतेष्व् अति-महतां केषाञ्चित् ऋषित्वम् उपपाद्यते । **“ये नखाः । ते वैखानसाः ये वालाः । ते वालखिल्याः” (तैत्तिरीय आरण्यके १.२३.२-३)** इति । ये नखा । इन्द्रिये ऽपि खम् इति (नखरः) चामरः अतः खम् इन्द्रियाख्या न येषां, ते नखाः ।, अतो नेन्द्रिय-विशिष्टाः । नख-शब्द इन्द्रिय-रहितत्वं वदति । यथा नर इत्य् अयं शब्दो ऽपि - रीङ् क्षये (-धातुमात्राद् एफस्य (रीङ् क्षय इति धातोः ड-प्रत्ययेन निष्पन्न र इति शब्दस्य) क्षय-वाचित्वान् नर इत्य् अयं शब्दः क्षय-भोग-राहित्यं वदति । जह्नुर् नारायणो नरः । नराज् जातानि तत्त्वानि इत्यादिषु नर-शब्दस्यायम् एवार्थः । एवं नख-शब्दो ऽपि इन्द्रिय-राहित्यं वदति । इन्द्रियवताम् इन्द्रिय-रहितत्वं नाम इन्द्रिय-वश्यता-रहितत्वम् । नखाः - इन्द्रिय-पराधीना न भवन्तीति भावः । अनखाः - इन्द्रियाधीन-ज्ञानाधीना न भवन्तीति चार्थः । सकलेन्द्रिय-प्रत्याहार-पूर्वक-केवल-भक्ति-रूप-ज्ञानिनो भवन्तीत्य् अर्थः । यद्वा छन्दसि [[स्वख्यत्ययेन|वर्णव्यत्ययेन]] अनखा इति छेदन इन्द्रिय-राहित्य-निषेधेन इन्द्रिय-सहितत्वं भगवद्-भागवत-परिचर्या-करणैक-योग्य-विशिष्टेन्द्रिय-वत्त्वम् एव वदति, यथा नाग-शब्दो गमन-राहित्य-निवर्तितो गमनम् एव वदति तद्वद् अयम् अर्थः । विचित्रा देह-सम्पत्तिर् इत्यादिना करण-कलेबराणां भगवद्-आराधनार्थतया सृष्टत्वोक्तेः । वर्णाश्रमाचारवता पुरुषेण परः पुमान् । विष्णुर् आराध्यते पन्था नान्यस् तत्-तोष-कारकः ॥ इति वर्णाचाराणां भगवद्-आराधन-रूपत्व-प्रतिपादनात् तद्-अनुष्ठानस्य करण-कलेबर-सापेक्षत्वात् तथानुष्ठान-परास् स्वेन्द्रियवन्त एव । किञ्च विष्णु-धर्माद्य्-अनेक-पुराणेषु — **“सा जिह्वा या हरिं स्तौतीत्य् आरभ्य तं न भवेत् करण-लोपम् इति । एवं रूपैर् अनेकैर् वचनैर् भगवत्-समाराधन-रूप-स्व-धर्मानुष्ठानोपयुक्तानाम् एव शरीरिणाम् इन्द्रियवत्त्वं तद्-अनुपयुक्त-शरीराणां शरीरवत्त्वं प्रत्यादि ।”**


In the Manusmṛti also, it is stated: "apa eva sasarjādau tāsu vīryam apāsṛjat। tad aṇḍam abhavad dhaimaṃ sahasrāṃśu samaprabham॥" (Manusmṛti 1.7) ["He first created the waters alone and placed his energy (vīrya) in them. That (seed) became a golden egg, radiant like a thousand suns."]. Here, the 'vīrya' (energy/seed) mentioned as the causal factor for creation, as stated in other authoritative texts, is the mental energy (mano-vīrya). Therefore, the term 'mānasa' (mental) here signifies that mental potency which is endowed with the specific power to produce subsequent effects. The statement "mānaṃ vai prājāpatyam" also clarifies, through the word 'prājāpatya', that the subsequent word 'māṃsa' here means 'mānasa' (of the mind). Furthermore, the word 'pavitra' (pure) used here removes any suspicion of impurity that might arise from the word 'māṃsa' (flesh) being used to denote Prajāpati's mental creation. Also, the phrase "mānasā ṛṣayaḥ" (mental sages) in the statement "tato 'ruṇāḥ ketavo vātaraśanā ṛṣaya udatiṣṭhan" ["Then the sages named Aruṇā, Ketava, and Vātaraśanā arose"] clearly indicates that these sages who arose were born from the mind. Therefore, upon examination through multiple scriptural proofs, it is established that 'māṃsa' here means 'mānasa'. Moreover, since mental creation is described in many causal statements, while a creation from flesh is never heard of anywhere, here too 'māṃsa' must be understood as 'mānasam' (the mental). 'Mānasa' means that which is attached to or fixed in a certain object. Therefore, Prajāpati's mind was fixed upon the Lord during his performance of austerities. Due to this reason, empowered by the extraordinary creative power obtained by the Lord's grace, from that 'mānasa' (the mind), the sages named Aruṇā, Ketava, and Vātaraśanā, who were the fruits of Prajāpati's penance, arose, being raised up by the Lord. In both interpretations, the meaning is that they were born from the mind. Aruṇāḥ, Ketavaḥ, Vātaraśanāḥ — these are the names of those sages, according to one interpretation. Alternatively, Aruṇāḥ indicates their exceptional brilliance (tejas). 'Aruṇatva' means a reddish hue, as per the Śruti: "yad agre arārohitam rūpaṃ tejasas tad rūpam iti" (Chāndogyopaniṣad 6.4.1) ["That reddish form which appears first is the form of tejas (brilliance)."] Meaning: they are brilliant. Or, since 'aruṇāḥ' (the reddish glow) is seen even before sunrise, they are called 'Aruṇāḥ' due to being the first to shine (like the dawn). The word Ketavaḥ is synonymous with 'dhvaja' (banner/flag). The word 'dhvaja' is used to mean a sign or emblem, as in 'Garuḍadhvaja' (He whose banner is Garuḍa). Therefore, its synonym, 'ketu', also means a sign. Thus, 'Ketavaḥ' are those who are emblematic, characteristic, or exemplary among all sages in terms of lineage, knowledge, conduct, etc. They are distinguished and visible above all others, as it were. Vātaraśanāḥ — 'Raśanā' refers to certain objects that serve as ornaments for living beings. 'Vātaraśanā' means those who are by nature like ornaments (raśanā) made of wind (vāta). Wind (vāyu) has many inherent qualities: it is the vital force of all, it moves everywhere, it nourishes all, it is unattached to all objects, etc. These sages, whose inherent nature (like that of wind) arose — meaning they stood high, above all, or stood upright (in a state of spiritual aspiration, mumukṣavaḥ). The act of arising (utthāna kriyā) presupposes an agent (kartṛ). Creation with prior intention (buddhipūrvikā sṛṣṭi) happens for those already created, not for those being created. Therefore, it is established that all (these sages) were first created by the Lord. Furthermore, the fact that they are designated by the word 'Ṛṣi' (seer) is observed. Their status as Ṛṣis also proves they were created earlier, as that status is achieved after a long period (of penance/study); it would be absent if they were just then created. Therefore, they were created by the mind of the Lord, and manifested from the 'mānasa' (mind) as the fruit of Prajāpati's penance. This is the meaning of "te ṛṣaya udatiṣṭhan". For some among these great ones, the status of Ṛṣi is appropriate. "Ye nakhāḥ, te vaikhānasāḥ; ye vālāḥ, te vālakhilyāḥ" ["Those who are 'nakhāḥ', they are the Vaikhānasas; those who are 'vālāḥ', they are the Vālakhilyas."]. 'Ye nakhāḥ' — 'Kha' also means sense organ (as in "indriye 'pi kham"). Therefore, 'nakha' means those for whom 'kha' (sense organs) are 'na' (not). Hence, they are without specialized sense organs. The word 'nakha' indicates being devoid of sense organs. Just as the word 'nara' (from the root ṝ meaning to move, with a negative prefix) indicates freedom from decay or enjoyment, as in "jahnur nārāyaṇo naraḥ" or "narāj jātāni tattvāni", similarly, the word 'nakha' indicates freedom from sense organs. For those possessing sense organs, 'freedom from sense organs' means not being under their control. 'Nakhāḥ' are those who are not subservient to their senses. 'Anakhāḥ' means those whose knowledge is not dependent on the senses. They are those who have knowledge arising from pure devotion, having first withdrawn all senses. Alternatively, by a Vedic etymological derivation (chandasī), 'nakha' negates the absence of sense organs and actually indicates the possession of specific, refined senses fit only for serving the Lord's devotees. Just as the word 'nāga' negates the absence of movement (na + gam) and actually indicates movement itself, so is this meaning. This is supported by statements elsewhere that the complex bodily faculties (karana, kāyādi) are created for the worship of the Lord. Since the duties prescribed for the varṇas and āśramas are meant for the worship of Viṣṇu — "varṇāśramācārataḥ puruṣeṇa paraḥ pumān, viṣṇur ārādhyate panthā nānyas tat-toṣa-kārakaḥ" — and since following those duties requires the possession of bodily faculties (karana, kāyādi), those who follow them indeed possess such faculties. Moreover, in many Purāṇas like the Viṣṇudharmādi, statements beginning with "sā jihvā yā hari stavīti" ["That tongue alone which praises Hari..."] up to "taṃ na bhavet karaṇa-lopam" ["There should be no loss of those faculties for him"] establish through many such declarations that only those embodied beings whose faculties (indriyas) are engaged in the performance of duties, which are forms of worshipping the Lord, possess such faculties (indriyavattva). For other bodies not useful for such worship, their possession of a body is denied.


श्री-भागवते च - **“जिह्वे कीर्तय केशवं”** मुर-रिपुम् इत्यादिना सकलेन्द्रियाणां भगवत्-समाराधने विनियोगः श्री-शुक-ब्रह्मर्षि-[[खादीत्|आदीनाम्]] । एवं भगवद्-आराधनोपयुक्तेन्द्रियवन्तः अनखा इत्य् उच्यन्ते । एवं विधा वैखानसा इत्य् अर्थः । ये वालाः । ते वालखिल्या इत्यस्यार्थ उच्यते । बहुलं छन्दसीति सूत्र-बलात् कालबलस्वर-कर्तृ-यङाञ्चेति सर्व-वर्णानां व्यत्ययोक्तेः । बवयोर् अविशेष इति प्रसिद्धेश्च । बाला इति वाकारो बकारत्वेन व्यत्यस्यते । ये बाला इति चोभयथापि वक्तुं शक्यते । ये वालाः वृद्धाः । अनेन ज्ञान-वृद्धत्वादय उच्यन्ते । श्री-रामायणे - **“शील-वृद्धैर् ज्ञान-वृद्धैर् वयो-वृद्धैश् च”** इति सज्जन-विशेषा उच्यन्ते । अत्रापि तथाविधा वृद्धा अबाला इत्य् उच्यन्ते ये बाल-भाववन्तः ते बालाः । ज्ञानाधिकानां बाल-भावे स्थितिः वेदान्त-वाक्येन विहिता । सहकार्यन्तर-विधिः पक्षेण तृतीयं तद्वतो विध्यादिवद् इत्य् अस्य सूत्रादेर् अधिकरणस्येदं विषय-वाक्यम् उच्यते । तस्मात् ब्रह्मणः पाण्डित्यं निर्विद्य बाल्येन तिष्ठासेत् । बाल्यञ्च पाण्डित्यञ्च निर्विद्याथ मुनिर् इति बाल्येन तिष्ठासेत् बाल-भावेन तिष्ठेद् इति विधीयते । को बाल-भाव इत्याकाङ्क्षायाम् इदं सूत्रम् उच्यते । **“अनाविष्कुर्वन्न् अन्वयात्”** इति । अस्य सूत्रस्यार्थः । विदुषा बाल्यम् उपादेयतया विहितं बाल्यं बाल-भावः, बालस्य कर्म वा बाल्यं, बालस्य वयो ऽवस्थादि बाल्यम् । बालस्य हृदयं वा बाल्यम् । तत्र बालस्य कर्म काम-चारादि । बालस्य वयो नाम वेदाध्यायक-विहित-कर्मानुष्ठान-योग्यावस्थायाः पूर्वावस्था । उभयम् अपि विदुषो ऽनुपादेयम् । तत्र पारिशेष्याद् बालस्य हृदयं बाल्यम् । तद् उपादेयम् इत्य् अर्थः । अनाविष्कुर्वन् – हृदयम् अनाविष्कुर्वन् । बाल-हृदयस्य यत् स्व-प्रभावानाविष्कार-कृद् रूपं तद्-उपादानो वर्तेत विद्वान् कुतः - अन्वयात् । तस्यैवान्वयात् । बाल्येन तिष्ठासेद् इत्य् अस्मिन् विधौ तस्यैव ह्य् अन्वय-सम्भवः । इतरेषां विद्या-विरोधित्व-श्रवणात् ।


In the Śrīmad Bhāgavata also, statements like "jihvā kīrtaya keśavam" ["O tongue, glorify Keśava"] and "murāripum" etc., prescribe the dedication of all sense organs to the worship of the Lord. Therefore, sages like Śuka and Brahmā, whose senses are thus engaged in the worship of the Lord, are described as "anakhāḥ" (not under the control of senses). Such are the Vaikhānasas. Now, the meaning of "Ye vālāḥ, te vālakhilyāḥ" is explained. By the force of the sūtra "bahulaṃ chandasi" and the principle that all letters can interchange, and due to the well-known equivalence of 'va' and 'ba', the form "vālāḥ" can be seen as a transposition where the 'ā' and 'a' sounds switch, yielding "bālāḥ". Thus, it can be interpreted both ways: "ye vālāḥ" (the aged) or "ye bālāḥ" (the childlike). Ye vālāḥ — 'Vālāḥ' means the aged. This indicates those who are mature in knowledge, etc. As in the Śrī Rāmāyaṇa: "śīla-vṛddhair jñāna-vṛddhair vayo-vṛddhaiś ca" ["by those advanced in character, knowledge, and age"], referring to distinguished, venerable persons. Here too, such venerable, aged persons are indicated. They are 'abālāḥ' (not children). Ye bālāḥ — 'Bālāḥ' means those who abide in a childlike state. The Vedānta scriptures prescribe that the wise should abide in a childlike state. This is a supplementary injunction (sahakāri-viśeṣa-vidhi) analogous to other injunctions. Therefore, it is stated: "brahmaṇaḥ pāṇḍityaṃ nirvidya bālyena tiṣṭhāset" ["Having become dispassionate towards scholarship in Brahman, one should abide in childhood."] and "bālyaṃ ca pāṇḍityaṃ ca nirvidyātha munir bhavet" ["Having become dispassionate towards both childhood and scholarship, one becomes a sage."]. The injunction is: one should abide in childhood, in a childlike state. What is this 'childlike state' (bāla-bhāva)? To answer this, the sūtra "anāviṣkurvan anvayāt" is quoted. The meaning of this sūtra is: For the wise person, childhood (bālya) is prescribed as something to be adopted. 'Bālya' means childlike state, or the action/nature of a child, or the age/condition of a child, or the heart of a child. Among these, the actions of a child (like capriciousness) and the age of a child (the stage before eligibility for Vedic study and prescribed duties) are both not to be adopted by the wise. By implication, it is the heart of a child that is to be adopted. That is the meaning. Anāviṣkurvan — not manifesting, i.e., not manifesting (the profundity of) the heart. The wise person should adopt the state of not revealing the inherent profundity of a child's heart. Why? Anvayāt — because of connection. There is a connection possible only with this (the child's heart) in the injunction "bālye na tiṣṭhāset" ["one should abide in childhood"]. Other interpretations (childish actions or age) are heard to be contradictory to knowledge (vidyā).


**“नाविरतो दुश्चरितान् नाशान्तो नासमाहितः । नाशान्तो मानसो विप्रः ज्ञानेनैवम् अवाप्नुयात् ॥”** **“आहार-शुद्धौ सत्त्व-शुद्धिः”** इत्यादिषु । बाल्येन तिष्ठासेत्, बाल्ये हीन्द्रिय-विकारादि-काम-क्रोध-सम्भव-स्वभावाविष्करण-स्व-रक्षण-चिन्तनादि-विरहिते यथा वर्तते, ज्ञानवान् सम्यग्-ज्ञानवत्तया तथा वर्तेत तद्-अधिकरण-विषय-श्रुति-वाक्ये तात्पर्यार्थः । अतो बाला इत्य् अनेन शब्देन तथा विज्ञानवत्त्वादि-काम-क्रोधादि-सर्व-दोष-रहितत्व-स्व-वैभवानाविष्करणादि-मत्त्वं सर्वम् उच्यते तस्माद् एवं वर्तमाना ये बालास् ते वालखिल्या इत्य् अर्थः । एवं जीव-समष्टि-रूपस्य प्रजापतेर् मानसाद् उत्थापिता अरुणाः केतवो वातरशना इति पूर्वम् एवैतन्-नाम-वताम् ऋषीणां जितेन्द्रियत्व-सम्यग्-ज्ञानवत्त्व-तद्-अनुगुण-वैभवादि-प्रकाशनाय **“ये नखाः ये वालाः”** इत्याभ्यां तेषाम् ऋषित्वोपपादक-निरतिशय-शान्तिमत्त्व-सम्यग्-ज्ञानवत्त्वादिकम् उक्त्वा **“ते वैखानसाः ते वालखिल्याः”** इति श्रुतिर् एव नामाकरोत् ।


"nāvirato duścaritān nāśānto nāsamāhitaḥ । nāśānto mānaso vipro jñānenainam avāpnuyāt ॥" ["Not one who has not ceased from bad conduct, nor one who is not calm, nor one who is not composed, nor one whose mind is not tranquil — such a brāhmaṇa attains This (Brahman) through knowledge alone."] And in statements like "āhāra-śuddhau satva-śuddhiḥ" ["From purity of food, comes purity of being."]. The meaning of the scriptural statement pertaining to the injunction "bālyena tiṣṭhāset" ["one should abide in a childlike state"] is this: Just as a child naturally abides, free from manifesting the disturbances of the senses, the arising of desire and anger, and the anxiety of self-preservation, so should a person of perfect knowledge (jñānavān), by virtue of his knowledge, abide. This is the purport of the Vedic text that is the subject of that discussion (adhikaraṇa). Therefore, by the word "bālāḥ" (childlike ones), all this is indicated: possession of such supreme knowledge, freedom from all faults like desire and anger, and the non-manifestation of one's own majesty, etc. Those who thus abide are the Vālakhilyas. Thus, the sages named Aruṇā, Ketava, and Vātaraśanā — who existed with these names even before — were raised from the mind (mānasa) of Prajāpati, the collective form of individual souls. To reveal their qualities — mastery of the senses, possession of perfect knowledge, and the corresponding majestic attributes — the Śruti, after stating through the phrases "ye nakhāḥ" and "ye vālāḥ" their unequalled tranquility, perfect knowledge, etc., which qualify them as Ṛṣis, itself gave them the names "te vaikhānasāḥ" and "te vālakhilyāḥ".


श्रीमद्-रामायणे - तत्र वैखानसा नाम वालखिल्याश् च नामतः । इत्य् उक्तत्वात्, एतयोः शब्दयोः तन्-नामधेयत्वम् उक्तं सिद्धम् । किञ्च श्रीमन्-नाम-सहस्राध्याये - **“वैखानस् साम-गायनः”** इति वैखानस-शब्द-वाच्यत्वम् उक्तम् । तदनन्तरं साम-गायन इत्य् अप्य् उक्तम् । तस्यार्थः - साम्ना गीयत इति साम-गायनः । अयम् अर्थो **“वैखानसं पूर्वे ऽहन् साम भवति”** इत्यस्यां श्रुत्याम् अनुसन्धेयः । शान्ति-पर्वणि - **“यत् तस्मिन्न् उपलभ्यं तु तत् तेजस् तत् त्रयीमयम् । तद् वैघानः परं ब्रह्म इति ब्रह्म-विदो विदुः ॥”** इति । ब्रह्म-शब्द-वाच्यस्य भगवतो वैखानसत्वम् उक्तम् । आत्म-संहितायाम् - **“महा-भूतेन येनेदं व्याप्तं विधनसा जगत् । तम् एव शरणं गच्छ जगत्-कारणम् अच्युतम् ।”** इति । शाण्डिल्येन शतानन्दम् अभिवीक्ष्योक्तम् अतो भगवतो [[विनश्|विखनस्]]-शब्द-वाच्यत्वं च सिद्धम् । [[विघनाघन|विघना]]-शब्देन पापम् उच्यते । विघनः अपहत-पाप्मेत्य् अर्थः । एवं सर्वेश्वरस्य विघन इति नाम विद्यते । एवं नामवतस् सर्वेश्वरात् व्यष्टि-कारणाय समुत्पन्नस्य ब्रह्मणो ऽपि वैखानसत्वम् उक्तम् । शान्ति-पर्वणि - **“सृष्टस् तुष्टस् समुद्युक्तो ब्रह्मा योनि-मयः प्रभुः । खनित्वा चात्मनात्मानं धर्मादि-गुण-संयुतम् ॥ ध्यानम् आविश्य योगेन हृद्य् आसीद् विखनसो मुनिः ॥”** इत्य् आरभ्य - **“तस्माद् वैखानसो नाम स आसीद् अण्डज-प्रियः । इत्य् अन्तेन ग्रन्थेन पञ्च-भूत-सृष्ट्यनन्तरं व्यष्टि-सृष्टि-कारणाय विखनश्-शब्द-वाच्येन नारायणेन सृष्टो ऽण्डजो ब्रह्मा विखनसो जातत्वाद् व्यष्टि-करणत्वाच् च वैखानसो ऽभूद् इत्य् अर्थः ।”**


In the Śrīmad Rāmāyaṇa, it is stated: "tatra vaikhānasā nāma vālakhilyāś ca nāmataḥ" ["There, those known by the names Vaikhānasas and Vālakhilyas..."]. Therefore, it is established that these two words are indeed their names. Furthermore, in the Śrīman Nāmasahasrādhyāya (the Thousand Names of Viṣṇu-Viśṇū sahasranāma): "vaikhānassa-sāmagāyanaḥ" — the name 'Vaikhānasa' is mentioned, followed by 'Sāmagāyana'. Its meaning is: 'Sāmagāyana' means 'one who is sung by Sāman hymns'. This meaning should be contemplated in the Śruti: "vaikhānasaṃ pūrve 'han sāma bhavati" ["On the former day, the Sāman becomes Vaikhānasa."]. In the Śāntiparva (of the Mahābhārata): "yat tasminn upalabhyaṃ tu tat tejas tat trayīmayam। tad vaighānaḥ paraṃ brahma iti brahmavido viduḥ॥" ["That which is obtainable in That (Brahman), that brilliance, that which consists of the three Vedas, that Vaighāna is the Supreme Brahman — so know the knowers of Brahman."] Here, the Lord, denoted by the word 'Brahman', is said to be 'Vaighāna' (or Vaikhānasa). In the Ātmasaṃhitā: "mahābhūtena yenedaṃ vyāptaṃ vidhanasā jagat। tam eva śaraṇaṃ gaccha jagat-kāraṇam acyutam॥" ["Take refuge in that very Imperishable One, the cause of the world, by whom this world is pervaded through the great elements as Vidhanasā."] This was spoken by Śāṇḍilya to Śatānanda. Thus, the Lord being denoted by the word 'Vidhanasā' is established. The word 'vidhana' (or 'vighana') means sin; 'Vidhana' means 'He whose sins are destroyed'. Thus, the Supreme Lord has the name 'Vidhana'. Thus, it is stated that Brahmā, who arose from the Supreme Lord — the possessor of all names — for the purpose of individual creation, also has the quality of being 'Vaighānasa' (related to Vidhana/Vaikhānasa). In the Śāntiparva: "sṛṣṭaḥ tuṣṭaḥ samudyukto brahmā yonimayaḥ prabhuḥ। khanitvācātmanātmānaṃ dharmādi-guṇa-saṃyutam॥ dhyānam āviśya yogena hṛdy āsīd vikhanaso muniḥ॥" ["Brahmā, the lord of creation, satisfied and diligent, of the nature of the source (yoni), having dug out (khanitvā) from himself his own self endowed with qualities like righteousness, etc., and having entered meditation through yoga, became the sage Vikhanas in heart."] Beginning with this and ending with the passage "tasmād vaikhānasa nāma sa āsīd aṇḍaja-priyaḥ" ["Therefore, that beloved of the lotus-born (Brahmā) was named Vaikhānasa."] — this text explains that after the creation of the five elements, for the purpose of individual creation, Brahmā, the lotus-born, created by Nārāyaṇa (denoted by the word 'Vikhanas'), became 'Vaikhānasa' because he originated from 'Vikhanas' and because of his role as the individual creator.


किञ्च श्री-भागवते - **“न खलु गोपिका-नन्दनो भवान् अखिल-देहिनाम् अन्तरात्मा । विघनसार्थितो विश्व-गुप्तये सख उदेयिवान् सात्वतां कुले ।” । (श्रीमद्भागवत १०.३१.४)** अत्र विखनश्-शब्देन ब्रह्मोच्यते । तथा भू-भार-निर्हरणाय प्रार्थितो भगवान् अवतीर्ण इति पुराणेषूच्यते । तस्माद् विखन इति ब्रह्मणो नाम । एवं विखनश्-शब्द-वाच्येन नारायणेन तदपत्येन वैखानस-शब्द-वाच्येन ब्रह्मणापि ये मनसा सृष्टास् ते सर्वे वैखानस-नामान एव । एवं मनसा सृष्टा ऋषयः । एवं सर्व-प्रजा-सृष्टि-कर्तार इति श्रुत्योच्यते । **“मानसा ऋषयः प्रजा असृजन्तेति”** मानस-सृष्टिषु प्रजा-सृष्टि-कर्तॄन् भगवतो ऽयं भगवान् एवावदत् । **महर्षयस् सप्त पूर्वे चत्वारो मनवस् तथा । मद्-भावा मानसा जाता एषां लोक इमाः प्रजाः ॥** अत्र **“मानसा जाता एषां लोक इमाः प्रजा”** इत्य् उक्तत्वात् मानस-सृष्टिष्व् एते प्रजा-सृष्टि-कर्तार इति ज्ञायते । मद्-भावाः - मया आहित-सृष्टि-शक्तयो मदिच्छानुवर्तिनः प्रजा असृजन्तेत्य् अर्थः । तस्माद् एते ऋषयः सर्वासां प्रजानां मूल-भूता इति । तस्माद् एतेषां मूल-भूतास् ते सर्वे वैखानसा एव । तद् उक्तं शान्ति-पर्वणि - **भृग्व्-अङ्गिरो मरीच्य्-अत्रिः पुलहः क्रतुः । तथा वसिष्ठो दक्षश् च नव स्वायम्भुवा द्विजाः ॥ एते वैखानसानां तु ऋषीणां भावितात्मनाम् । वंश-कर्तार उच्यन्ते सात्विकाहार-भोजिनाम् ॥**

अत्र भृग्वादीन् स्वायम्भुवान् इत्य् उक्त्वा एते वैखानसानां तु वंश-कर्तार इत्य् उक्तम् । ननु सर्वेश्वरस्य ब्रह्मणो विघनश्-शब्द-वाच्यत्वात् ताभ्यां मनसा सृष्टानां महर्षिणां तैर् अपि सृष्टानां सर्वेषां च वैखानसत्वात्, त एव सर्वेषां एक-गोत्रत्वात् पापाद् अन्योन्यं विवाहादि-सम्बन्ध-कारण-हेतु-भूतो गोत्र-भेदः कथम् अभूद् इति चेत् - अत्र वंश-कर्तार इति वंश-भेद-कर्तार इत्य् उच्यते गोत्र-भेद-कर्तार इत्य् अर्थः । कथम् एतेषां गोत्र-भेद-कर्तृत्वम् इति चेत्, उच्यते । अत्र गोत्र-भेद-हेतुः योनिजत्वम् । तत् तु भगवता प्रजापतिना सृष्टानां भृग्वादीनां महर्षीणां न विद्यते । एतैस् सृष्टानां ब्राह्मणानां योनिजत्वं विद्यते । तस्मात् तत्-तद्-ऋषीन् आरभ्य गोत्र-भेदो ऽभूत् । एतैस् सृष्टानां योनिजत्वं कथम् इति चेत् । महा-भारते उमा-महेश्वर-संवादे रुद्रेण क्रियमाणे दाक्षायणी-दक्षयोस् संवादानुवादे **“अहं ज्येष्ठा विशिष्टा च तस्मात् त्वं मां सुपूजय”** इति दाक्षायण्युक्तो दक्षः तां प्रत्येवम् उक्तवान् ।


Furthermore, in the Śrīmad Bhāgavata (10.31.4): "na khalu gopikā-nandano bhavān akhila-dehinām antar-ātmā । vighanasārthito viśva-guptaye sakha udeyivān sātvatāṃ kule ॥" ["O friend, You are not merely the darling of the gopīs. You are the inner Self of all embodied beings. Invoked by Brahmā (Vighana) for the protection of the universe, You have arisen in the Yadava dynasty."] Here, the word 'Vighanasā' (or Vighana) refers to Brahmā. It is described in the Purāṇas that the Lord incarnated, being implored (by Brahmā) for the removal of the earth's burden. Therefore, 'Vighana' (or Vikhana) is a name of Brahmā. Thus, those who were mentally created by Brahmā, who is denoted by the word 'Vaikhānasa' (derived from Vikhana) and who is the offspring of Nārāyaṇa denoted by the word 'Vikhanas', are all indeed named 'Vaikhānasa'. Thus, the sages were created by mind. Thus, the Śruti states that they are the creators of all beings: "Mānasā ṛṣayaḥ prajā asṛjanta" ["The mental sages created the beings."]. Among mental creations, the Lord Himself said that He is the creator of beings: "Maharṣayaḥ saṃprāpūrve catvāro manavas tathā । mad-bhāvā mānasā jātā eṣāṃ loka imāḥ prajāḥ ॥" ["The great sages, the four Manus in the beginning, born from my being, mental (creations) — their worlds and these beings (are created)."] Since it is stated here, "mānasā jātā eṣāṃ loka imāḥ prajāḥ" ["mental (creations) are born; their worlds and these beings"], it is understood that among mental creations, these (sages, etc.) are the creators of beings. "Mad-bhāvāḥ" means: they, endowed with the creative power placed by me, following my will, created the beings. Therefore, these sages are the root of all beings. Hence, they, being the root (of all), are all indeed Vaikhānasas. This is stated in the Śāntiparva: "Bhṛgv-aṅgiras-marīcy-atriḥ pulahaḥ kratus tathā । vasistho dakṣaś ca nava svāyambhuvā dvijāḥ ॥ ete vaikhānasānāṃ tu ṛṣīṇāṃ bhāvitātmanām । vaṃśa-kartāra ucyante sāttvikāhāra-bhojinām ॥" ["Bhṛgu, Aṅgiras, Marīci, Atri, Pulaha, Kratu, as well as Vasiṣṭha and Dakṣa — these nine are the twice-born of Svāyambhuva (Manu). They are said to be the founders of the lineages of the Vaikhānasa sages, who are self-realized and eat pure food."]


एवम् उक्तो ऽब्रवीद् वाक्यं दक्षस् संरक्त-लोचनः । न त्वं ज्येष्ठापि मे पूज्या अन्या मन्ये मतास् सुताः ॥ आसां ये चैव भर्तारः ते मे बहु-मतास् सुते । वसिष्ठो ऽत्रिः पुलस्त्यश् चाङ्गिराः पुलहः क्रतुः ॥ मरीचिर् भृगुर् इत्य् एते श्लाघ्या जामातरो मम इति दक्षेणोच्यमाने वाक्ये महर्षीणां जामातृत्व-प्रतिपादनात्, तत्-तत्-सृष्टि-सृष्टानां योनिजत्वम् तत एव तत्-तत्-सृष्टानां त्रि-पूर्वक-गोत्र-भेदश् चाभूद् इति विज्ञायते । एवम् एव योनि-जातानाम् ऋषीणां वंश-कर्तार इत्य् उक्तिर् एव-विध-सृष्टि-कारणात् । आश्वलायन-प्रवरे सर्वे समान-गोत्रास् स्युर् इति शौनकादिर् इत्यादिना सर्वेषां ब्राह्मणानां, समान-गोत्रत्वञ् चोदितम् । शौनके तु गृह-पति-गोत्रान्वय-विशेषास् तस्येत्यादिना गोत्र-भेद-कारणम् उक्तम् । तथाहि लोकेषु ब्राह्मण्यं ब्राह्मणादि-कृत्यं । ने ब्राह्मण इत्यादिषु जाति-भेद-प्रतिपादकेषु वाक्येषु योनिजत्व-विशेष एव जाति-भेद-कारणत्वेनोच्यते । एवं गोत्र-भेद-हेतुश् च योनिजत्वम् एव । तस्मात् कृतोद्वाहानां दक्ष-जामातृ-प्रमुखानां भृग्वादीनाम् ऋषीणां नामधेय-पूर्वकम् एव बोधायनादि-सूत्रेषु प्रवर-गणाः न क्रियन्ते । अत्र तावत् बोधायनः - **“अथातः प्रवरान् व्याख्यास्यामः - सप्तानाम् ऋषीणाम् अगस्त्याष्टमानां पक्षा भवन्ति । त्रयः पक्षा भृगूणाम् । पञ्चार्षेया वत्साः विदा आर्षेण भृगूणाम् आदितो व्याख्यास्यामः ।” (बोधायन-श्रौत-सूत्रम्)** मार्कण्डेया मण्डूक् इत्यादयः । आपस्तम्ब-प्रवरे भृगूणाम् एवाग्रे व्याख्यास्यामो जामदग्न्या वत्सास् तेषां पञ्चार्षेय-प्रवरा भार्गव-च्यवनाप्लवानौर्व-जामदग्न्येत्यादयः ॥ वैखानस-सूत्र-प्रवरे - **“अथातः प्रवरान् व्याख्यास्यामः - विश्वामित्र-जमदग्नि-भरद्वाज-शौतमात्रि-वसिष्ठ-काश्यपानां सप्तर्षीणाम् अगस्त्यादि यद् अपत्यम् इत्यादि । तत्र भार्गव-जामदग्न्य-वत्सानां पञ्चार्षेयस् सप्तार्षेयो भार्गव-च्यवनाप्लवानौर्वश् चेत्यादि ।”** एवं सर्वेषु प्रवरेषु एत एव गोत्र-कारा उच्यन्ते । तस्माद् अत्र कश्चिद् अर्थो ऽवधार्यताम् । विखनश्-शब्द-वाच्यं ब्रह्माणम् आरभ्य जातानां सर्वेषां वैखानसत्वं साधारणम् । एतेषु बहुषु मानसेषु ये कृतोद्वाहाः ते वंश-कर्तारो ऽभवन् । अन्ये ऽपि ये ऋषयः प्रवरेषु गोत्र-मूल-भूतत्वेनोच्यन्ते । सर्वे वंश-कर्तारो भवन्तीति ज्ञेयम् । एवं ब्रह्माणम् आरभ्य प्रवृत्ते वैखानसत्वे सर्वेषां विद्यमाने ऽपि पश्चात् सर्वे स्व-स्व-वंश-कर्तृ-भूतर्षीणां नामधेयैर् भार्गव आत्रेयः काश्यप इत्य् एवं व्यपदिश्यन्ते । किञ्च बोधायनादि-सूत्रिखो ऽपि बोधायन, आपस्तम्ब-सत्याषाढ, आश्वलायन इत्य् एवं सूत्र-कृन्-नामधेयैर् अपि व्यपदिश्यन्ते । एवं बहुषु जातानां ब्राह्मणानां स्व-स्व-वंश-कर्तृ-भूतर्ष्यादि-नामधेयैर् बहु-विधा व्यपदेशा दृश्यन्ते । किञ्च सूत्र-कृद् अपि नैतेषाम् अवान्तरो महर्षिः अपि तु सकल-सूत्र-कृत्-कूटस्थ-ब्रह्मैव ॥


When spoken to in this manner, Dakṣa, with eyes reddened (with anger), said: "na tvaṃ jyeṣṭā 'pi me pūjyā anyā manye matāḥ sutāḥ ॥ āsāṃ ye caiva bhartāraḥ te me bahu-matāḥ sute । vasiṣṭho 'triḥ pulastyaś cāṅgirāḥ pulahaḥ kratuḥ ॥ marīcir bhṛgur ity ete ślāghyā jāmātaro mama" ["You (the Moon) are not the eldest, nor worthy of my worship; my other sons-in-law are esteemed by me. And the husbands of these (daughters) are highly regarded by me, O son. Vasiṣṭha, Atri, Pulastya, Aṅgiras, Pulaha, Kratu, Marīci, and Bhṛgu — these are my praiseworthy sons-in-law."] Since in this statement by Dakṣa, the great sages are proclaimed as sons-in-law, it is understood that those created by their respective creation (the daughters of Dakṣa) were born from wombs (yoni-ja). And from that alone, the distinctions of the gotras (lineages) of those created beings, preceded by the three (pravaras), also arose. Similarly, the statement that the sages born from wombs are "founders of lineages" refers precisely to the cause of creation from wombs. In the Āśvalāyana pravara, it is stated that all (of a certain group) would have the same gotra, like "Śāṇaka, etc." The identity of gotra for all brāhmaṇas is taught. Differences in gotra lineage, as in "Śaunaka, the householder of such-and-such gotra," are also explained. For instance, in statements like "In worldly terms, brahminhood (and) the duties of a brāhmaṇa, etc." or in passages indicating caste distinctions like "Not a brāhmaṇa, etc.," it is the specific condition of being born from a womb that is stated as the cause of caste distinction. Thus, the cause of gotra distinction is also being born from a womb. Therefore, for sages like Bhṛgu, etc., who were the chief sons-in-law of Dakṣa and who married, the pravara groups (in sūtras like those of) Bodhāyana, etc., are not formed merely based on their names. Here, for instance, Bodhāyana states: "athātaḥ pravarān vyākhyāsyāmaḥ — saptānām ṛṣīṇām agastyāṣṭamānāṃ pakṣā bhavanti. trayaḥ pakṣā bhṛgūṇām. pañcārṣayā vatsāḥ vidā ārṣeṇa bhṛgūṇām ādito vyākhyāsyāmaḥ." (Bodhāyana Śrauta Sūtra) [Markaṇḍeya, Maṇḍūk, etc.] In the Āpastamba pravara, it is stated: "We shall first explain those of the Bhṛgus. The Vatsas of Jamadagni; their five-ṛṣi pravara is: Bhārgava, Cyavana, Āplavana, Aurva, Jāmadagnya," etc. In the Vaikhānasa sūtra pravara: "athātaḥ pravarān vyākhyāsyāmaḥ — viśvāmitra jamadagni bharadvāja śaumatāri vasiṣṭha kāśyapānāṃ saptarṣīṇām agastyādi yad apatyam ityādi. tatra bhārgava jāmadagnya vatsānāṃ pañcārṣeyaḥ saptārṣeyo bhārgava cyavanāplavān aurvaś cetyādi." Thus, in all pravara systems, these same sages are stated as the founders of gotras. Therefore, a certain meaning should be ascertained here. Starting from Brahmā, denoted by the word 'Vikhanas', the quality of being 'Vaikhānasa' is common to all who are born. Among these many mental (creations), those who married became the founders of lineages. Other sages too, who are mentioned in the pravara systems as being the root of gotras, should be understood as all being founders of lineages. Thus, although the quality of being 'Vaikhānasa', originating from Brahmā, is present in all, later, all are designated by the names of those sages who were the founders of their respective lineages, such as Bhārgava, Ātreya, Kāśyapa, etc. Furthermore, even the composers of sūtras like Bodhāyana are designated by the names of the sūtra composers: Bodhāyana, Āpastamba, Sātyāṣāḍha, Āśvalāyana, etc. Thus, among the many-born brāhmaṇas, various designations are seen, based on the names of the founding sages of their respective lineages. Moreover, the composers of these sūtras are not some subordinate sages, but the eternal Brahman Itself, the author of all sūtras.


तथाहि - **“(देवानां) (वेदानां) व्यसनाद् यन् तु प्राग् रूपं मिहितं (महत्) तु यत् ॥ ततस् तु वैखानसत्वं तु सकल-वेद-सारतमत्वेन विखनश्-शब्द-वाच्येन ब्रह्मणा प्रोक्ततम् । तथा च भार्गवे वासाधिकारे यावत् सम्मिहितावस्था वेदनां व्यसनात् पुरा । तां तु वैखानसीं शाखां वदन्ति ब्रह्म-पारगाः ।”** । इति, (मरीचि-प्रोक्तायाम् आदि-संहितायां शाखानुक्रमणाध्याये - **“अतः परं प्रवक्ष्यामि शाखानुक्रमणं द्विजाः । बृहच्-छृङ्गश् च सत्सान् पृथुलो वेद-पर्वतः ॥ इत्य् उपक्रम्य - अतो यजुष्-प्रभेदं तु विदुर् एकशतं बुधाः ॥”** - इत्य् अन्तं महा-प्रबन्धम् अभिधाय - वेदानां व्यसनाद् यत् तु प्राग् रूपं मिहितं महत् । तां तु वैखानसीं शाखाम् इति वेद-विदो विदुः इत्य् उक्तम् । अत्र पृथुलो वेद-पर्वत इत्य् अनेन **“तँ ह त्रीन् गिरि-रूपान् अविज्ञाताज्ञिव दर्शयाञ्चकार”** इति श्रुतिर् अनुसन्धीयते । अनयोः श्लोकयोर् अयम् अर्थः । यदा हि भगवान् वेद-व्यासो वेदान् व्यस्तवान् ततः पूर्वस्याम् अवस्थायां विखनश्-शब्द-वाच्येन भगवता धृता यावस्था सा सम्मिकितावस्था तद्-अवस्थानां वेदानां वैखानसी शाखेति आख्याभूद् इति । ततस् तान् वेदान् ब्रह्म-सृष्ट्यनन्तरं भगवान् ब्रह्मण उपदिदेशेति श्रुत्योच्यते - **“यो ब्रह्माणं विदधाति पूर्वं यो वै वेदांश् च प्रहिणोति तस्मै” (श्वेताश्वतरोपनिषद् ६.१८)** इति ।


Indeed, it is so: "(devānāṃ) (vedānāṃ) vyasanād yantu prāg rūpaṃ mihitaṃ (mahat) tu yat ॥ tatas tu vaikhānasatvaṃ tu sakala-veda-sāratamatvena vikhanas-śabda-vācyena brahmaṇā proktatam। tathā ca bhārgave vāsādhikāre yāvat sammihitā 'vasthā vedanāṃ vyasanāt purā। tāṃ tu vaikhānasīṃ śākhāṃ vadanti brahma-pāragāḥ ॥" (Mārīcī-proktāyām Ādisaṃhitāyāṃ Śākhānukramaṇādhyāye) ["(Regarding the gods and) the Vedas, that great, unified form which existed before their division — that state, proclaimed by Brahmā denoted by the word 'Vikhanas' as the most essential essence of all the Vedas, is indeed 'Vaikhānasatva'. And as stated in the Bhārgava section on residence: 'The unified state of the Vedas before their division — that very branch the knowers of Brahman call the Vaikhānasī (branch).'"] Thus, in the chapter on the order of branches (Śākhānukramaṇa) in the Ādisaṃhitā spoken by Marīci, after beginning with "ataḥ paraṃ praśakṣyāmi śākhānukramaṇaṃ dvijāḥ। brahmacchṛṅgaś ca satsān pṛthulo vedaparvataḥ॥" ["O twice-born, henceforth I will declare the order of the branches. Brahmacchṛṅga and Satsān are the broad Veda-mountain..."], and after describing the great treatise up to "ato yajuṣ-prabhedaṃ tu vidur ekaśataṃ budhāḥ॥" ["The wise know the divisions of the Yajus to be one hundred and one."], it is stated: "vedanāṃ vyasanād yattu prāg rūpaṃ mihitaṃ mahat। tāṃ tu vaikhānasīṃ śākhāṃ" — "That great, unified form of the Vedas before their division, the knowers of the Veda know as the Vaikhānasī branch." Here, by the phrase "pṛthulo vedaparvataḥ" ("the broad Veda-mountain"), the Śruti "taṃ ha trīn girirūpān avijñātājñiva darśayāñcakāra" ["He made that one appear as three mountain-peaks, as if unknown to the ignorant"] is to be contemplated. The meaning of these two verses is: Before Bhagavān Vedavyāsa divided the Vedas, in that prior state, the condition upheld by the Lord denoted by the word 'Vikhanas' (Brahmā) was the unified condition. That condition of the Vedas became known as the Vaikhānasī branch. Thereafter, the Lord taught those Vedas to Brahmā after his creation, as stated in the Śruti: "yo brahmāṇaṃ vidadhāti pūrvaṃ yo vai vedāṃśca prahiṇoti tasmai" (Śvetāśvataropaniṣad 6.18) ["He who created Brahmā in the beginning, who indeed sent forth the Vedas to him."].


ब्रह्माण्डे श्री-रङ्ग-माहात्म्ये - **“श्री-वत्साङ्कः श्रियः कान्तः तस्मै वेदान् उपादिशत् इति भगवान् स्वयम् एव ब्रह्माणं वेदान् अध्यापयामासेत्य् उक्तम् ॥ तां तु वैखानसीं शाखाम् आदाव् अध्यापयन् मुनीन् ऋषीणां सो ऽब्रवीत् सूत्रं धर्मं समनुबोधयन्” ॥** इति विखनो-नाम्नः सर्वेश्वराद् अधीत-वैखानस-शाखः स खलु कमल-भूरादाव् एव स्व-मानस-जातान् मुनीन् स्वयम् एवाध्यापयन् तेषाम् ऋषीणां धर्मं समनुबोधयन् सूत्रम् अप्य् अब्रवीत् । एवं सूत्रं कृत्वा पद्म-भूर् वैखानसीं शाखां सूत्रे विनियुक्तवान् । एवं सर्वान् वेदान् स्व-सूत्रे विनियुज्य - **“पद्म-भूः परमो धाता तस्मिन्न् आराधन-त्रयम्”** इति । अब्रवीन् निगमार्थानाम् आतारं प्रविभाराशः । इति । तस्मिन् सूत्रे भगवद्-आराधनं वेदार्थानाम् आचाराश् च पृथग् उक्तान् इति स्कान्द-वचनानोक्तम् । आराधन-त्रयम् इति त्रैकालाभ्यर्चनं विवक्षितम् । ततो ब्रह्मा स्व-मानस-जातान् मुनीन् सर्वान् वेदान् अध्यापयित्वा सूत्रम् अपि कृत्वा तस्मिन् वेदम् अपि विनियुज्य भगवद्-आराधनं तत्-करण-योग्यतापादकान् आचारांश् चोपदिदेशेत्य् अर्थः **“ऋषयो ऽपि बहून् सूत्रान् चक्रस् तस्यानुसास्तः । केचिद् वर्णाश्रमाचारान् वदन्ति स्मृति-रूपकान् ।”** इत्य् अनेन ब्रह्मणस् सकाशाद् अधीत-वेदाः श्रुत-सूत्र-धर्माः ते ऽपि ऋषयः तद्-अनुसारतः पूर्वोपदिष्ट-वेद-सूत्रार्थानुसारेण कांश्चित् सूत्र-विशेषान् वर्णाश्रमाचाराणां स्फुरणाय काश्चन स्मृतीर् अप्य् अकुर्वन्न् इत्य् उक्तम् ।


In the Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa, Śrī Raṅgamāhātmya: "śrīvatsāṅka śriyaḥ kāntaḥ tasmai vedān upādiśat" ["The Lord, the beloved of Śrī, bearing the Śrīvatsa mark, instructed him (Brahmā) in the Vedas."] Thus, it is stated that the Lord Himself taught the Vedas to Brahmā. "tāṃ tu vaikhānasīṃ śākhām ādāv adhyāpayan munīn ṛṣīṇāṃ so 'bravīt sūtraṃ dharmaṃ samanu bodhayan" ["He, in the beginning, teaching that Vaikhānasī branch to the sages and ṛṣis, spoke the sūtra to them, imparting the dharma."] Thus, Brahmā, denoted by the name 'Vikhana', having learned the Vaikhānasī branch from the Supreme Lord of all, himself, in the very beginning, taught the sages born from his mind, and while imparting dharma to those ṛṣis, also proclaimed the sūtra. Having thus composed the sūtra, Padmabhū (Brahmā) incorporated the Vaikhānasī branch into the sūtra. Thus, having incorporated all the Vedas into his sūtra, "padmabhūḥ paramo dhātā tasminn ārādhanatrayam" ["Padmabhū, the supreme creator, established the threefold worship in it."] and "avaṣīn nigamārthānām ātāraṃ pravibhārāśaḥ" ["He became the conveyer and distributor of the meanings of the Vedas."]. Thus, it is stated in the Skānda Purāṇa that in that sūtra, the worship of the Lord and the codes of conduct based on the meaning of the Vedas were separately explained. 'Ārādhanatrayam' (threefold worship) refers to the three daily periods of worship (traikālābhyarcanam). Thereafter, Brahmā, having taught all the Vedas to the sages born from his mind, and having composed the sūtra, incorporated the Veda into it, and instructed (them) in the worship of the Lord and the codes of conduct that enable one to perform it — this is the meaning. "Ṛṣayo 'pi bahūn sūtrān cakras tasyānuśāstāḥ । kecid varṇāśramācārān vadanti smṛti-rūpakān ॥" ["The ṛṣis also, following his instruction, composed many sūtras. Some declared the codes of conduct for varṇas and āśramas in the form of smṛtis."] By this, it is stated that the Vedas, Śruti, sūtras, and dharma learned from Brahmā — those ṛṣis too, following that (instruction), in accordance with the meaning of the Veda and sūtra previously taught, composed some specific sūtras and formulated some smṛtis for the manifestation of the codes of conduct for varṇas and āśramas.


**“तां तु वैखानसीं शाखाम् आदाव् अध्यापयन् मुनीन्”** इत्य् उक्तत्वात् । वेदाध्ययनम् आरभ्य स्व-सूत्र-प्रवचन-भगवत्-समाराधन-सकल-धर्मापदेश-पर्यन्तं सर्वेषाम् ऋषीणां विशेषेण कृतत्वात् सर्वेषां सहाध्ययनं सिद्धम् । सूत्रस्य वैखानस-शब्द-वाच्यत्वं विखनसा प्रोक्तत्वात् । तद् उक्तम् - विखनसा तु सुसम्प्रोक्तं सूत्रं वैखानसं स्मृतम् । एषां चतुर्विधानां तु मूलं तत्-सूत्रम् एव चेतिः इति । अस्य चतुर्विधत्वं भगवत्-समाराधन-प्रतिपादकत्वञ्च । भार्गवे वासाधिकारे उक्तं सूत्रं विखनसा प्रोक्तं पुरा चैव चतुर्विधम् । हरेः पूजा-विधानस्य चक्रे सूत्रञ् चतुर्विधम् । चातुर्विध्यं मानसात्मालयागृह्याभेदेन इत्य् अनेन वाक्येन ज्ञायते । अन्य **“यस् तु वैखानसे सूत्रे विष्ण्व्-अर्चाम् आभाति वैदिकः । गृहे देवालये चैव भक्त्या नारायणं प्रभुम् ॥ सायं प्रातश् च होमान्ते भक्त्यैवाभ्यर्चयेद् इति ॥** एवम् अस्मिन् शास्त्रे एवं रूपैर् बहुर् वचनैः भगवद्-अर्चनं चतुर्विधं प्रोक्तम् । तत्र मानसार्चनं (प्रथमम्) आत्मार्चनं द्वितीयम् । तद्-द्वितयं केवलं ज्ञानेनैव कार्यम् । आलयार्चा गृहार्चेति द्वितयम् । एतद्-द्वितयं मन्त्र-पूतैः समाराधनार्है द्रव्यैर् भक्त्युपबृंहितैः कार्यम् । एतच्-चतुर्विधं वैखानस-सूत्रिभिर् अनुष्ठेयम् । तस्मात् प्रतिदिवसं प्रथमं तैर् एव केवलं ज्ञानेनैव कार्यम् । मानसार्चनम् आत्मार्चनञ्च कृत्वा पश्चाद् आलयार्चनं गृहार्चनञ्च कार्यम् । स्व-सूत्रोक्त-भगवद्-अर्चन-चतुष्टयम् एतद् वैखानस-सूत्रिणां प्रतिदिवसं कर्तव्यम् इत्य् अभिधायक-वचनम् अप्य् अस्ति । **“स्व-सूत्र-विहितं यद् यत् तत् तत् सर्वं समाहितः । कुर्याद् अन्यतमे लुप्ते कृतम् अप्य् अकृतं भवेद् इति”** खिलाधिकार-पुरातन्त्र-वासाधिकरण-क्रियाधिकरण-वर्णाधिकरण-चित्राधिकरण-यज्ञाधिकरण-भृगुः-पूर्व-तन्त्रम्-आत्रेयं-विष्णु-तन्त्रम्-उत्तर-तन्त्रं च - एते ग्रन्थाः यथा-क्रमम् । ग्रन्थ-संख्या-विशेषेण प्रति-मार्गं प्रवक्ष्यते ॥ खिलं खिलाधिकारञ्च पुरातन्त्रं तथैव च । वासाधिकरणञ्चैव क्रियाधिकरणाह्वयम् ॥ वर्णाधिकरणं ज्ञेयं चित्राधिकरणं तथा । निरुक्तञ्च प्रकीर्णञ्च यज्ञाधिकरणं भृगुः ॥ पूर्व-तन्त्रम् अथात्रेयं विष्णु-तन्त्रम् अथात्रिणा । उत्तराख्यं महा-तन्त्रं प्रोक्तं तन्त्र-चतुष्टयम् ॥ जयानन्दश् च संज्ञानं वीरश् च विजयस् तथा । विजितं विमलं ज्ञानं मरीचिस् संहिताष्टकम् ॥ सत्य-काण्डं तर्क-काण्डं ज्ञान-काण्डञ्च काश्यपः । किञ्च खिलं खिलाधिकारं पुरातन्त्रं वासाधिकरणं क्रियाधिकरणं वर्णाधिकरणं चित्राधिकरणं निरुक्तं प्रकीर्णं यज्ञाधिकरणम् इति दश ग्रन्थाः निर्मिताः उत्तर-तन्त्र-विष्णु-तन्त्र-आत्रेय-तन्त्र-पूर्व-तन्त्राणीति चतुर्विध-तन्त्राण्य् अत्रिणा प्रोक्तानि प्राणिनाम् उपकाराय शिष्येभ्यः प्रतिपादितानि । सत्य-काण्ड-तर्क-काण्ड-ज्ञान-काण्ड-भेदेन निज-प्रबन्धः काश्यपेनाबन्धि । मरीचिना जयानन्द-संज्ञान-विजय-वीर-विजित-विमल-ज्ञान-संहिता इत्य् अष्ट-संहिताः । तस्माद् एतच्-छास्त्रं पञ्चविंशति-संहिता-सहितम् । एवम् आदितो विखनसो नाम्ना ब्रह्मणा प्रवर्तितम् । तन्-मानस-पुत्रैर् भृग्वादिभिः स्व-कृतैर् ग्रन्थैर् विस्तारितम् इदं सूत्रं मन्वादिभिर् उपजीवितत्वात् सर्व-सूत्र-स्मृति-मूल-भूतम् । तथाहि तत्र तावन् मनुः । पुष्प-मूल-फलैर् वा ऽपि केवलैर् वर्तयेत् सदा । काल-पक्वैः स्वयं शीर्णैर् वैखानस-मते स्थितः ॥ **“वासन्तश् शारदैर् मेध्यैर् मुन्य्-अन्नैस् स्वयम् आहृतैः । पुरोडाशांश् चैव विधिवन् निर्वपेत् पृथग् इति ॥ वैखानस-मते स्थित इत्य् अस्य विघनसाचार्य-मते स्थित”** इति मरीचिना व्याख्यातम् । यद् वै किञ्चन मनुरवदत् तद् भेषजम् इति हि श्रुतिः । मन्व्य्-अर्थ-विपरीतार्था या स्मृतिः सा न शस्यते । इति ब्रह्माण्डादिषु पुराणेषूक्तम् आह च गौतमः ।


Due to the statement, "tāṃ tu vaikhānasīṃ śākhām ādāv adhyāpayan munīn" ["In the beginning, teaching that Vaikhānasī branch to the sages..."], since it is stated that they were taught, and because all the ṛṣis, starting from Vedic study up to the exposition of all dharma including the proclamation of their own sūtra and the worship of the Lord, were specifically engaged (by Brahmā), the joint study of all of them is established. The sūtra is denoted by the word 'Vaikhānasa' because it was proclaimed by 'Vikhanas'. This is stated: "vikhanasā tu susamproktaṃ sūtraṃ vaikhānasaṃ smṛtam। eṣāṃ caturvidhānāṃ tu mūlaṃ tat sūtram eva ca" ["The sūtra proclaimed by Vikhanas is remembered as Vaikhānasa. That sūtra is indeed the root for these four types."] Its fourfold nature and its function of establishing the worship of the Lord are also stated. In the Bhārgava section on residence, it is said: "sūtraṃ vikhanasā proktaṃ purā caiva caturvidham। hareḥ pūjā-vidhānasya cakre sūtraṃ caturvidham" ["The sūtra, proclaimed by Vikhanas in ancient times, is fourfold. He made the sūtra fourfold regarding the procedure of Hari's worship."] The fourfold division is understood from statements as being according to: mental, ātmic, temple, and domestic (mānasa, ātma, ālaya, gṛhya). Another (statement): "yaś tu vaikhānase sūtre viṣṇv arcām ābha vaidikaḥ। gṛhe devālaye caiva bhaktyā nārāyaṇaṃ prabhum॥ sāyaṃ prātaś ca homānte bhaktyaivābhyarcayed iti" ["He who, according to the Vaikhānasa sūtra, with devotion worships Lord Nārāyaṇa, the Vedic deity, in the house and in the temple... should worship with devotion in the evening, morning, and after the fire-offering."] Thus, in this scripture, through many such statements, the worship of the Lord is taught as fourfold: Mental worship (Mānasa-arcana) Self-worship (Ātma-arcana) — these two are to be performed by knowledge alone. Temple worship (Ālaya-arcana) Domestic worship (Gṛhya-arcana) — these two are to be performed with substances purified by mantras and accompanied by devotion. These four kinds are to be observed by the followers of the Vaikhānasa sūtra. Therefore, every day, they should first perform only the mental and self-worship through knowledge alone, and afterwards perform the temple and domestic worship. There is also a statement prescribing that this fourfold worship of the Lord as enjoined in their own sūtra is to be performed daily by the followers of the Vaikhānasa sūtra: "sva-sūtra-vihitaṃ yadyat tat tat sarvam samāhitaḥ। kuryād anyatame lupte kṛtam apy akṛtaṃ bhavet" ["Whatever is enjoined by one's own sūtra, all that should be performed with concentration. If any one (of the four) is omitted, what is done becomes undone."] The Khila (supplementary rites), Ādi-karaṇa, etc., will be described in order. The number and specific nature of the texts will be explained according to each path. The Khila, Khilādhikāra, Purā-tantra along with that, Vāsādhikaraṇa, Kriyādhikaraṇa thus named, Varṇādhikaraṇa to be known, Citrādhikaraṇa as well, Nirukta and Prakīrṇa, Yajñādhikaraṇa — (by) Bhṛgu. Pūrva-tantra, then Ātreya, Viṣṇu-tantra, then by Atri, the Uttara and Mahā-tantra — thus the fourfold Tantra is proclaimed. Jayānanda, Saṃjñāna, Vīra, Vijaya, Vijita, Vimala, Jñāna — Marīci's eight Saṃhitās. Satya-kaṇḍa, Tarka-kaṇḍa, Jñāna-kaṇḍa and by Kāśyapa... Furthermore, Khila, Khilādhikāra, Rā-tantra, Vāsādhikaraṇa, Vākyādhikaraṇa, Varṇādhikaraṇa, Citrādhikaraṇa, Nirukta, Prakīrṇa, Yajñādhikaraṇa — these ten texts were composed. The fourfold Tantras — Uttara-tantra, Viṣṇu-tantra, Ātreya-tantra, Pūrva-tantra — were proclaimed by Atri and taught to disciples for the benefit of beings. Satya-kāṇḍa, Tarka-kāṇḍa, Jñāna-kāṇḍa — his own treatise was composed by Kāśyapa. By Marīci, the eight Saṃhitās — Jayānanda, Saṃjñāna, Vijaya, Vīra, Vijita, Satyajñāna Saṃhitā. Therefore, this scripture, along with twenty-five Saṃhitās... Thus, from the beginning, this was promulgated by Brahmā named Vikhanas. Expanded by his mental sons like Bhṛgu with their own compositions, this sūtra, being followed by Manu and others, is the root of all sūtras and smṛtis. For instance, there itself, Manu states: "puṣpa-mūla-phalair vā 'pi kevalair vartayet sadā। kāla-pakkvaiḥ svayaṃ śīrṇair vaikhānasamate sthitaḥ" ["One established in the Vaikhānasa doctrine should always sustain himself solely on flowers, roots, and fruits that are ripened by time and have fallen by themselves."] And: "vāsantaś śāradair medhyair muny-annaiḥ svayam āhṛtaiḥ। puroḍāśāṃś caiva vidhivan nirvapet pṛthag iti" ["With pure, spring and autumnal (produce), with food fit for sages, gathered by oneself, and with rice-cakes, he should offer separately, according to rule."] "vaikhānasamate sthitaḥ" — "established in the doctrine of Vikhanasācārya" — thus explained by Marīci. A brahmacārī, a brahmacārī, a householder, or a mendicant (bhikṣu) is a Vaikhānasa. The commentary of Maskarin on this: "One who observes brahmacarya is a brahmacārī. A householder (gṛhastha) is from the association with a wife, which is 'gṛha' (home). Otherwise, there would be the unwanted conclusion that even those living in a house (but celibate) would be householders. One who lives on alms is a bhikṣu. That which is proclaimed by Vikhanas is Vaikhānasa; one who lives according to that is (a Vaikhānasa)." Again, specifically regarding the duties of a vānaprastha, Śaunaka says: "With the śrāmaṇaka (fire), he should touch water morning and evening." Similarly, Śaṅkha and Likhita: "Having established the fire with the śrāmaṇaka (rite), he should sustain himself with kuruviṇḍa, veṇu, śyāmāka, nīvāra, etc." And Hārīta says: "One who has set out for the forest is a vānaprastha. Forest produce, of the forest, or Vaikhānasa — his departure. A vānaprastha should avoid village-grown herbs. The place for the three fires is the śrāmaṇaka. Or, having established the fire with the śrāmaṇaka, wearing bark, hemp, skin, kuśa, muñja, or vidaka cloth, the vānaprastha, according to the rule proclaimed by the Vaikhānasas, is of two kinds: one who goes with permission and one who goes without permission," etc. Here, "according to the rule proclaimed by the Vaikhānasas" means according to the rule proclaimed in the Vaikhānasa Sūtra. This very thing was also stated by the venerable Vasiṣṭha: "Having established the fire with the śrāmaṇika, one who has established the sacred fires, living at the foot of a tree..." This is explained by Yajñasvāmin: "Śrāmaṇika is the name of the Vaikhānasa Sūtra." In the instruction to Śuka, Vyāsa says: "Some with roots, some with fruits, some with flowers, those of firm vows sustain themselves according to rule, known as followers of the Vaikhānasa doctrine." This very thing the venerable Bodhāyana said: "A vānaprastha, by the conduct prescribed in the Vaikhānasa scriptures, is a Vaikhānasa; in the forest, a root-and-fruit eater, controlled, touching water at the times of the savanas (morning, noon, evening), having established the fire with the śrāmaṇaka, avoiding village food." In the Jaiminīya Sūtra, in the section on the seasonal Cāturmāsya, it is said: "As in the Bāradvāja (Sūtra) first, and as in the Vaikhānasa first..." In both sūtras, the commentary says: "Among the twelve priests, one should choose the sacrificer who is a follower of the Bāradvāja or the Vaikhānasa Sūtra." Thus, many statements from Smṛti, Itihāsa, and Purāṇa will be shown that demonstrate the supreme authority of the Vaikhānasa Sūtra. In the Chāndogya Brāhmaṇa: "The Vaikhānasa is the Achāvāka Sāman; the Sāman becomes Vaikhānasa. The Vaikhānasas are the Ārṣeyas (descended from ṛṣis). They were dear to Indra." After stating that the essence of the Achāvāka Sāman is Vikhana, it is reiterated that this Sāman is named Vaikhānasa. Then it says: "The Vaikhānasas are the Ārṣeyas. They were dear to Indra." By being named Vaikhānasas, the priests of the Achāvāka Sāman are Vaikhānasas. It is said they were very dear to the Lord. Here, 'Ārṣeyas' refers to those who arose as ṛṣis, as stated in "ṛṣaya udatiṣṭhan". Here the word 'Indra' refers to the Lord, as in the Śruti: "sa indraḥ so 'kṣaraḥ paramaḥ svarāṭ" and in that Upaniṣad, by the word 'Indra', the Lord is meant. 'Vaikhānasas' refers to the followers of the Vaikhānasa Sūtra. "They were dear" indicates that the followers of the Vaikhānasa Sūtra were beloved of the Lord, the Supreme Ruler. The meaning is that the Vaikhānasa Sūtra followers, by performing the fourfold worship of the Lord as enjoined in the Vaikhānasa Sūtra and possessing the perfect knowledge conducive to such worship, are dear to the Lord, the Supreme Ruler. For it is said by the Lord: "The wise man is exceedingly dear to Me, and he is dear to Me." Because of possessing perfect knowledge. Therefore, since the followers of the Vaikhānasa Sūtra also possess perfect knowledge, they are dear to the Lord. Such Vaikhānasa Sūtra followers are spoken of in all Itihāsas and Purāṇas. In the Śrīmad Araṇya Kāṇḍa (of Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa): "When Śarabhaṅga attained heaven, hosts of sages came together. They rushed towards Kākutstha Rāma of blazing splendor. Vaikhānasas, Vālakhilyas, Sampraśāka, Mārīcīpā, many Aśmakuṭṭas, leaf-eaters and ascetics, those with teeth as mortars, and others who dive (in water), those who lie on swords, those who lie on bare ground, likewise those who take a vow of silence, or so... sages who subsist on water, others who eat air, those who dwell in the sky, those who sleep on the ground, those observing vows and fasts, the controlled, those wearing hides and rags, those engaged in japa, always engaged in austerity, endowed with the fivefold austerity — all blessed with Brāhmaṇic splendor, firm in yoga, composed — the ascetics came to Rāma at Śarabhaṅga's hermitage."


इत्य् उक्तम् । अत्र वैखानसा इति वानप्रस्था एव उच्यन्ते । **“सो ऽयं ब्राह्मण-भूयिष्ठो वानप्रस्थो महा-गण”** इति प्रथमोपस्थितानां वैखानसानां वानप्रस्थत्वाद् एव वानप्रस्थ-गण इत्य् उक्तम् । तेषां वानप्रस्थत्वं वानप्रस्थ-धर्म-प्रतिपादक-वैखानस-सूत्रत्वात् । तस्मात् तत्रोक्ता वैखानसाः वैखानस-सूत्रिण एव । ब्राह्मण-भूयिष्ठ इत्य् उक्तत्वात् उपरिष्टाद् अपि सतां प्रति ब्राह्मणेभ्यो विशेषत इति रामेणोक्तत्वाच् च । तत्रागतानाम् ऋषीणां ब्राह्मी श्रीर् विद्यत इत्य् उक्तम् । ब्राह्मी श्रीर् नाम ”ऋचस् सामानि यजूंषि” । सा हि श्रीर् अमृता सताम् इत्य् उक्ता श्रीः । तथा-विध-श्रीमत्त्वं नाम सदा वेद-त्रयाध्ययन-कृतम् । तस्माद् वैखानसादीनां तेषां सर्वेषाम् ऋषीणां सदा वेद-त्रय-सहाध्ययनं विद्यत इति सिद्धम् । श्रीमत्-किष्किन्धा-काण्डे - **“तत्र वैखानसा नाम वालखिल्याश् च नामतः । महर्षयः प्रकाशन्ते सूर्य-वर्णास् तपस्विनः ॥” (श्रीमद् वाल्मीकि रामायण ४.४०.५८-५९)** इत्य् अत्रोक्तानां सूर्य-समान-तेजसां तपस्विनां वैखानसानां वैखानस-नामत्वं तत्-सूत्रितत्वाद् एवेति ज्ञायते । तत्रैव - **सिद्ध-वैखानसाश् चैव वालखिल्या तपोधनाः । वन्द्यास् ते तु तपस्-सिद्धास् तपसा वीत-कल्मषाः ॥”**


Thus it is stated. Here, those mentioned as Vaikhānasas are indeed vānaprasthas (forest-dwelling ascetics). It is said, "so 'yaṃ brāhmaṇa-bhūyiṣṭho vānaprastho mahā-gaṇaḥ" ["This great group, consisting mostly of brāhmaṇas, is the vānaprastha group."] Since the first-mentioned Vaikhānasas are vānaprasthas, they are called the 'vānaprastha group'. Their status as vānaprasthas is because the Vaikhānasa Sūtra expounds the duties of a vānaprastha. Therefore, the Vaikhānasas mentioned there are indeed the followers of the Vaikhānasa Sūtra. Since it is stated they are "mostly brāhmaṇas" and later Rāma also says they are distinguished among brāhmaṇas, it is established. It is said that the ṛṣis who gathered there possessed "Brāhmī Śrī". 'Brāhmī Śrī' is: "ṛcaḥ sāmāni yajūṃṣi" — the Vedas. That Śrī, said to be immortal for the virtuous, is such splendour. That kind of splendour is indeed the result of constantly studying the three Vedas. Therefore, it is established that for all those ṛṣis, the Vaikhānasas and others, there was always joint study of the three Vedas. In the Śrīmat Kiṣkindhākāṇḍa: "tatra vaikhānasā nāma vālakhilyāś ca nāmataḥ। maharṣayaḥ prakāśante sūrya-varṇās tapasvinaḥ ॥" ["There, those named Vaikhānasas and Vālakhilyas, the great sages, shine with the color of the sun, being ascetics."] Here, the fact that those ascetics (tapasvins), shining like the sun, are named 'Vaikhānasas' is understood to be precisely because they follow the (Vaikhānasa) Sūtra. In the same place: "siddha-vaikhānasāś caiva vālakhilyā tapodhanāḥ। vandhyās te tu tapas-siddhās tapasā vīta-kalmaṣāḥ ॥" ["The perfected Vaikhānasas and the Vālakhilyas, rich in austerity; though childless, they are perfected by austerity, their sins destroyed by austerity."]


इत्य् अत्र वैखानसादीनां सर्वत्र वन्द्यत्वम् उक्तम् । ब्रह्माण्डे पाराशर्यायां संहितायाम् - **“वैखानस-प्रवरैश् च वालखिल्यादि-योगिभिः”** इति । क्षीराब्धि-नाथ-नित्य-सेवा-करैर् एवम् उक्तम् । **“य एषो ऽन्तरादित्ये हिरण्मयः पुरुषो दृश्यते” (छान्दोग्योपनिषद्‌ १.६.६),** हिरण्य-श्मश्रुर् हिरण्य-केश आप्रणखात् सर्व एव सुवर्णस् तस्य यथा कप्यासं पुण्डरीकम् एवम् अक्षिणी इति इत्यस्य पुण्डरीकाक्षत्वम् उक्तम् । पुण्डरीकाक्षो नारायण एव । तस्माद् आदित्यम् अभिध्यायन् इत्य् अत्र श्रुता दिव्य-शब्देन ध्येयत्वेनोक्तः पुरुषो नारायण एव । तद् उक्तं – - **“ध्येयस् सदा सवितृ-मण्डल-मध्य-नारायणस् सरसिजासन-सन्नि-विष्टः केयूरवान् मकर-कुण्डलवान् किरीटी हारी हिरण्मय-वपुर् धृत-शङ्ख-चक्रः” (नरसिंह पुराण ६२.१७)** इति । अत्र हिरण्मय-वपुर् इत्य् अनेन **“आप्रणखात् सर्व एव सुवर्ण” (छान्दोग्योपनिषद्‌ १.६.६)** इति पूर्वोक्त-पुरुषो नारायण एवेति ज्ञायते । तस्माद् एतच्-छुति-द्वयोपादानेन पुरुष-सूक्त-नारायणानुवाकाव् अनुसन्धेयौ । एवं सर्व-कर्मानुष्ठान-योग्यतापादक-सन्ध्यावन्दनादिकं भगवत्-समाराधनादिकं भगवत्-समाराधनम् इति सिद्धम् । किञ्च नैतावत्, भगवतः सकल-वेद-वेद्यत्वं सकल-कर्म-समाराध्यञ्च सर्वे वेदा यत्रैकं भवन्ति सर्वे होतारो यत्रैकं भवन्तीति श्रुत्यैवोच्यते । अयम् अर्थो भगवतैवोच्यते - **“वेदैश् च सर्वैर् अहम् एव वेद्य” (श्रीमद्भगवद्गीता १५.१५), “अहं हि सर्व-यज्ञानां भोक्ता च प्रभुर् एव च” (श्रीमद्भगवद्गीता ९.२४) ये ऽप्य् अन्य-देवता-भक्ता यजन्ते श्रद्धयान्विताः । ते ऽपि माम् एव कौन्तेय यजन्त्य् अविधि-पूर्वकम् इति च ॥” (श्रीमद्भगवद्गीता ९.२३)**


Thus, here, the worshipfulness of the Vaikhānasas and others everywhere is stated. In the Pārāśarya Saṃhitā of the Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa: "vaikhānasapravaraiś ca vālakhilyādi yogibhiḥ" ["by those foremost among Vaikhānasas and by yogis like the Vālakhilyas."] This is also stated by those engaged in the eternal service of the Lord of the Ocean of Milk. "ya eṣo 'ntar-āditye hiraṇmayaḥ puruṣo dṛśyate" (Chāndogya Upaniṣad 1.6.6) ["This golden Person who is seen within the sun..."] "hiraṇya-śmaśrur hiraṇya-keśa āpraṇakhāt sarva eva suvarṇaḥ" ["with golden beard, golden hair, golden entirely up to the very nails..."] "tasya yathā kapyāsaṃ puṇḍarīkam evam akṣiṇī" ["His two eyes are like a red lotus."] — thus His being 'Puṇḍarīkākṣa' (lotus-eyed) is stated. Puṇḍarīkākṣa is Nārāyaṇa alone. Therefore, the Person stated in the Śruti passage "ādityam abhidhyāyan" ["meditating on the sun"] as the object of meditation by the word 'divya' (divine) is Nārāyaṇa alone. This is stated: "dhyeyaḥ sadā savitṛ-maṇḍala-madhyastho nārāyaṇaḥ sarasijāsana-sanniviṣṭaḥ keyūravān makara-kuṇḍalavān kirīṭī hārī hiraṇmaya vapur dhṛta-śaṅkha-cakraḥ" (Narasiṃha Purāṇa 62.17) ["He who is always to be meditated upon is Nārāyaṇa, seated on the lotus, situated in the center of the solar orb, wearing armlets, makara-shaped earrings, a crown, garlands; His form is golden, bearing the conch and discus."] Here, by the phrase "hiraṇmaya vapuḥ" ("golden form"), it is understood that the previously mentioned Person "āpraṇakhāt sarva eva suvarṇaḥ" is Nārāyaṇa alone. Therefore, these two Śruti passages should be contemplated along with the Puruṣasūkta and the Nārāyaṇānuvāka. Thus, it is established that rituals like sandhyā-vandanā, which confer eligibility for performing all rites, are (in essence) the worship of the Lord. And not only this; that the Lord is knowable by all the Vedas and is the object of worship of all rites is stated by the Śruti itself: "sarve veda yat padam āmananti" ["All Vedas speak of His state."]; "sa ekaḥ, sa traiyaḥ, sa sarvāṇi hotrāṇi bhavati" ["He is one; He is threefold; He becomes all the priests."]; "yatraikaṃ bhavanti" ["Where they become one."]. This very meaning is stated by the Lord Himself: "vedaiś ca sarvair aham eva vedyah" (Śrīmad Bhagavadgītā 15.15) ["By all the Vedas, I alone am to be known."]; "ahaṃ hi sarva-yajñānāṃ bhoktā ca prabhur eva ca" (Śrīmad Bhagavadgītā 9.24) ["For I alone am the enjoyer and also the Lord of all sacrifices."]; "ye 'py anya-devatā-bhaktā yajante śraddhayānvitāḥ। te 'pi mām eva kaunteya yajanty avidyā-pūrvakam॥" (Śrīmad Bhagavadgītā 9.23) ["Even those who are devotees of other deities and worship them with faith, they too worship Me alone, O Kaunteya, but by the wrong method."].


किञ्च - **“ये यजन्ति पितॄन् देवान् ब्राह्मणान् सहुताशनान् । सर्व-भूतान्तरात्मानं विष्णुम् एव यजन्ति ते” ॥** इति स्मृतिर् अपि वदति । अत्र श्रृति-स्मृत्युक्तानां सर्वेषां यज्ञादि-कर्मणां भगवत्-समाराधन-रूपत्वम् इत्यस्य कश्चिद् अभिप्रायः । अन्यस् तु वर्णाश्रमाचारवद्भिः पुरुषैर् एव विष्णुर् आराध्यते, नान्यैः । तस्मात् सर्वैः स्वाश्रमोचित-स्व-सूत्रोक्त-कर्म-निष्ठैर् भगवान् एव समाराध्यते । किञ्च बोधायनादि-सूत्रेषु विशेषतो भगवत्-समाराधनं विधीयते । वैखानस-सूत्रे ऽपि विस्तरेण भगवत्-समाराधनम् उक्तम् । भगवच्-छास्त्रे ऽपि चोक्तम् । किञ्च तत्र सर्वेषां स्व-शास्त्र-विहितम् इदं कर्तव्यं, तत्र श्रुति-स्मृति-विहित-कर्मानुष्ठान-मात्र-कर्तुर् महर्षेर् अभिप्रायः वर्णाश्रमोचित-कर्मानुष्ठानम् एव भगवत्-समाराधनम् । तदशक्त उक्तम् एतद्-अतिरेकेण बोधायन-शौनक-वैखानस-सूत्रोक्त-भगवच्-छास्त्रोक्त-भगवत्-समाराधनं चिकीर्षुभिस् तैस् तैर् अनुक्तम् अन्यतो ग्राह्यम् इति न्यायेन तत्-तच्-छास्त्रोक्त-क्रमेण भगवत्-समाराधनं कर्तव्यम् इति । तत्र वैखानस-सूत्रे भगवत्-समाराधनस्य चातुर्विध्यम् उक्तम् । तत्तन्-मानसात्म-गृहालयार्चा-भेदेन सन्ध्यावन्दनादि-नित्य-कर्माग्निहोत्र-सकल-कर्मानुष्ठान-गृहार्चनालयार्चना-भेदेन चतुर्विधम् एतच्-चतुर्विधं सर्वैर् ब्राह्मणैर् अनुष्ठीयते । तस्मात् सर्वेषां विहितानुष्ठाने ऽतीव माहात्म्यं भवत्य् एव । आलयार्चकानां कश्चिद् दोषो विद्यत इति केचिद् वदन्तीति कैश्चिद् उच्यते चेद् बोधायन-शौनकादि-सूत्रोक्तालयार्चकानाम् अपि भवत्य् एव । भगवच्-छास्त्रोक्तालयार्चकानाम् अपि च स एव स्यात् । तस्माच् चतुर्विधेषु भगवत्-समाराधनेषु नियमेन निष्ठावतां न कश्चिद् दोषो विद्यत इति वदन्ति । तद् उक्तं ब्रह्माण्डे शिव-शेखर-तन्त्रे च देवेन देवीं प्रत्युच्यमाने प्रदेशे - **“अथ देवलकान् वक्ष्ये श्रृणु देवि समाहिता । कर्म-देवलकाः केचित् कल्प-देवलकाः परे ॥ शुद्ध-देवलकास् त्व् अन्ये देवलकाः स्मृताः । अन्य-सूत्रोक्त-मार्गेण यस् संवत्सर-पूजकः ॥ अदीक्षितः स्वयं तत्र कर्म-देवलकस् तु सः । अनेनैव प्रकारेण वत्सर-त्रय-पूजकः ॥ कल्प-देवलकः प्रोक्तस् स वैशैल-सुते ध्रुवम् । अननुज्ञात-आचार्यान् नागमं शुश्रुवे च यः । शीलतस् तस्य शिष्यश् च शुद्ध-देवलकाव् उभौ ।”** एवं त्रिविध-देवलकत्वम् उक्त्वा, “अत्रैव देवलकत्वं तु न विष्णु-विषयं क्वचित् रुद्र-काव्यादि-विषयं, एतद्-आगम-सम्मतम् इति । अत्र देवलकत्व-सम्भावनया विषय-भेद-प्रदर्शनेन भगवद्-आराधकानां देवलकत्वं न विद्यते । तत् तु रुद्र-काव्यादि-पूजकानाम् एव इत्य् उक्तम् । किञ्च महा-भारते देवेनोच्यमाने स्वात्मानं प्रति दक्ष-कृत-शाप (अनुवादे) दक्षो ऽध्वरे देव्या दक्ष-कृत-स्व-विषयान्-अपराधान् असह्य प्राण-परित्यागः कृतः । तद्-उपश्रुत्य निधनं सती-देव्यास् तथा-विधम् ॥ अशपं रोष-संयुक्तो दक्षं जामातृभिस् सह । तद्-उपश्रुत्य दक्षस् तु मां च सो ऽप्य् अशपत् पुनः ॥ यस्मात् त्वं मत्-कृते क्रूर ऋषीन् त्वं शप्तवानसि । तस्मात् सार्धं सुरैर् यज्ञे न त्वं भागम् अवाप्स्यसि ॥ एतैश् चैवाष्टभिर् नित्यं मुनिभिर् धर्म-संयुक्तैः । एतद्-वंश-समुद्भूतैर् अस्पृश्यस् त्वं भविष्यसि ॥


Furthermore, the Smṛti also states: "ye yajanti pitṛn devān brāhmaṇān sahu tāśanān। sarva-bhūtāntar-ātmānaṃ viṣṇum eva yajanti te॥" ["Those who sacrifice to the manes, gods, brāhmaṇas, and the fire-eater, they worship Viṣṇu alone, the inner Self of all beings."] Here, the intention of this statement from Śruti and Smṛti is that all rites like sacrifice, etc., are forms of worshipping the Lord. Another (view) is that only men observing the codes of varṇas and āśramas worship Viṣṇu, not others. Therefore, by all, through the performance of duties prescribed in their own sūtras as appropriate to their stage of life, the Lord alone is worshipped. Moreover, in sūtras like those of Bodhāyana, etc., the worship of the Lord is specifically enjoined. The Vaikhānasa Sūtra also elaborately describes the worship of the Lord. It is also stated in the scriptures devoted to the Lord. Furthermore, there, for everyone, this (worship) prescribed by their own scriptures is to be performed. There, the intention of the great sage (Manu) is that for the performer, merely performing the rites enjoined by Śruti and Smṛti, i.e., the performance of duties as per one's varṇa and āśrama, is itself the worship of the Lord. It is stated for those who desire otherwise. Beyond this, for those desiring the worship of the Lord as stated in the Vaikhānasa Sūtra of Bodhāyana, Śaunaka, etc., or as stated in the scriptures devoted to the Lord, that worship should be performed according to the procedure prescribed in those respective scriptures, following the principle that what is not stated (in the general texts) should be learned from elsewhere. In the Vaikhānasa Sūtra, the worship of the Lord is described as fourfold. That fourfold worship, by the distinctions of mental, ātmic, domestic, and temple worship, includes sandhyā-vandanā, etc., daily rites, agnihotra, performance of all rites, and domestic and temple worship. This fourfold (worship) is performed by all brāhmaṇas. Therefore, there is indeed great merit in the prescribed performance for everyone. If some say that there is some fault in those who perform temple worship, the same would apply to those who perform temple worship according to the sūtras of Bodhāyana, Śaunaka, etc., and to those who perform it according to the scriptures devoted to the Lord. Therefore, they say that for those established with discipline in the fourfold worship of the Lord, there is no fault whatsoever. This is stated in the Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa and the Śivaśekhara Tantra, in a passage where the God (Śiva) is speaking to the Goddess: "Now I shall speak about the devalakas (temple priests), listen, O Goddess, with attention. Some devalakas are 'karma' devalakas, others are 'kalpa' devalakas. Others are called 'śuddha' devalakas. He who is a worshipper for a year according to the method prescribed in another sūtra, uninitiated, he himself there is a karma devalaka. In the same way, a worshipper for three years is proclaimed a kalpa devalaka. He, O Vaiśālī's son, is certain. He who, not having obtained permission from the ācārya, has not heard the āgama, and his disciple by conduct — both are śuddha devalakas." Thus, after stating the threefold devalaka-hood, it is said: "Here, devalaka-hood is not at all applicable to Viṣṇu (worship); it pertains to the worship of Rudra, etc. This is agreed upon in the Āgamas." Here, by showing the distinction of subject matter considering the possibility of devalaka-hood, it is stated that there is no devalaka-hood for the worshippers of the Lord; that applies only to the worshippers of Rudra, etc. Furthermore, in the Mahābhārata, in a passage where the God (Śiva) is speaking about himself regarding the curse by Dakṣa (in the context of Satī's self-immolation at Dakṣa's sacrifice due to the insult to her husband Śiva), it is said: "Having heard of that kind of death of Satī, the Goddess, enraged, I cursed Dakṣa along with the sons-in-law. Hearing that, Dakṣa in turn cursed me again: 'Since you, cruel one, have cursed the sages because of me, therefore, O knower of sacrifices, you shall not obtain a share along with the gods. And always by these eight ṛṣis, endowed with dharma, born from this lineage, you shall become untouchable.'"


नैतद्-वंशेषु यः कश्चित् त्वद्-भक्तो हि जनिष्यति । अज्ञानात् त्वां हि यः कश्चिन् मुनीन्द्रो **“ब्राह्मणो ऽपि वा अनुजीवति चेत् स स्यात् तुल्यश्-शूद्रेण गर्हितः भवेद् देवलकश् चैन रुद्र-काव्युपजीवकः ॥”** इत्य् अत्र स्व-पूजकस्य शूद्र-साम्यं देवलकत्वं रुद्र-काव्युपजीवितत्वञ्च दक्ष-शापाद् अभूद् इति स्वयम् एवावदत् । तस्मात् स्व-सूत्र-विहितस्य चतुर्विध-भगवद्-आराधनस्य कर्तॄणां महात्मनां वैखानस-सूत्रिणां माहात्म्यं देवलकत्वादि-दोषाश् च तद्-वैभवानभिज्ञैर् अनुनिगृहहोत-मनोभिर् न वक्तुम् उचितम् । किञ्च ब्रह्माण्डे - **“देवतान्तर-पूजकानां स्वाराधकानाञ्च वैषम्यम् अपि भगवान् नारायणस् स्वयम् एवावदत् । अन्य-धर्म-परो विप्रो नष्टो गच्छत्य् अधो-गतिम् अत्-कर्म-निरतो विप्रो नाधः पतति कुत्रचित् त्रायन्ते खलु नात्यन्तम् अन्य-धर्मास् स्वनुष्ठिताः । अपि मत्-कर्म-विगुण-त्रायते महतो भयात् ॥”** इति पाप-शुपति-वचनम् - **“स्मर्तव्यस् सततं विष्णुर् विस्मर्तव्यो न जातुचित् सर्वे विधि-निषेधास् स्युर् एतयोर् एव किङ्कराः ॥ वेदोक्त-याग-होमादि केशवोद्देशतः कृतम् । धर्मम् आहुर् अधर्मस् तु स एव हरि-विप्रियः ॥”** इति भगवद्-व्यासोनक्तम् । **“किञ्च भगवद्-आराधकानां वैभवं श्रुत्या प्रतिपाद्यते । यः पूर्व्याय वेधसे न वीपसे । सुमज्-जानये विष्णवे ददाशति । यो जातम् अस्य महतो महि ब्रवात् । सेदुश्-श्रवोभिर् युज्यचिद् अभ्यसत्”** इति । तथा पुराण-वचनैश् च - **“आराधनानां सर्वेषां विष्णोर्-आराधनं परम् । तस्मात् परतरं प्रोक्तं तदीयाराधनं परम् ॥ स्वाराधनं विहायपि स्व-भक्तान् अर्चयन्ति ये । तेभ्यः प्रसन्नो देवेशो ददातीष्टां प्रयच्छति ॥ तस्य यज्ञ-वराहस्य विष्णोर् अमित-तेजसः । प्रणामं ये ऽपि कुर्वन्ति तेषाम् अपि नमो नमः ॥”**


"Naitad-vaṃśeṣu yaḥ kaścit tvad-bhakto hi janiṣyati। ajñānāt tvāṃ hi yaḥ kaścin munīndro..." ["None born in these lineages will be your devotee. Whoever, O chief of sages, out of ignorance..."] "brāhmaṇo 'pi vā anujīvati cet sasyāt tulyaḥ śūdreṇa garhitaḥ bhaved devalakaś caina rudrakāvy-upajīvakaḥ ॥" ["Even a brāhmaṇa, if he lives by (it), becomes equal to a śūdra, despised; he becomes a devalaka, living on the offerings for Rudra."] Here, the God (Śiva) himself stated that the equality with a śūdra and the state of being a devalaka (temple priest) living on offerings meant for Rudra for his own worshipper arose from Dakṣa's curse. Therefore, the greatness of the great-souled Vaikhānasa Sūtra followers, who perform the fourfold worship of the Lord as enjoined in their own sūtra, and faults like 'devalaka-hood' — it is not proper for those ignorant of their glory and with malicious minds to speak of. Furthermore, in the Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa, the Lord Nārāyaṇa Himself stated the distinction between worshippers of other deities and His own worshippers: "anyadharmaparo vipro naṣṭo gacchaty adhogatim at karma nirato vipro nādhaḥ patati kutracit trāyante khalu nātyantam anya dharmāḥ sv-anuṣṭhitāḥ। api mat-karma-viguṇa trāyate mahato bhayāt ॥" ["A brāhmaṇa devoted to other dharmas is lost and goes to a lower state. A brāhmaṇa engaged in My rites never falls downwards. Indeed, other dharmas, even if well-practiced, do not ultimately protect. But even a defective performance of My rites protects from great fear."] And the statement of Pāpaśupati (Śiva): "samartavyaḥ satataṃ viṣṇur vismartavyo na jātucit sarve vidhi-niṣedhāḥ syur etayor eva kiṅkarāḥ ॥" ["Viṣṇu is always to be remembered, never to be forgotten. All injunctions and prohibitions are but servants of these two."] And stated by Bhagavān Vyāsa: "vedokta-yāga-homādi keśavoddeśataḥ kṛtam। dharmam āhur adharmas tu sa eva hari-vipriyaḥ ॥" ["Sacrifices, fire-offerings, etc., mentioned in the Vedas, if done with Keśava as the goal, are called dharma. That very thing, if averse to Hari, is adharma."] Moreover, the glory of the worshippers of the Lord is established by Śruti: "yaḥ pūrvyāya vedhase na vīpase। sumajjānaye viṣṇave dadāśati। yo jātam asya mahato mahi bravāt। sed uś śravobhir yujya cid abhyasat" ["He who gives to the ancient ordainer, to the Viṣṇu of wide strides, to the great one; he who proclaims the greatness of Him who is born of that great one; he, with praises, constantly strives to attain Him."] And by Purāṇa statements: "ārādhanānāṃ sarveṣāṃ viṣṇor ārādhanam param। tasmāt parataraṃ proktaṃ tadīyārādhanam param ॥" ["Among all worships, the worship of Viṣṇu is supreme. Therefore, more supreme than that is declared the worship of His devotees."] "svārādhanam vihāyapi sva-bhaktān arcatānti ye। tebhyaḥ prasanno deveśo dhatte iṣṭāṃ prayacchati ॥" ["Those who, setting aside their own worship, worship My devotees — to them the Lord of gods, pleased, grants their desired boons."] "tasya yajña-varāhasya viṣṇor amita-tejasaḥ। praṇāmaṃ ye ऽpi kurvanti teṣām api namo नमः ॥" ["To that Yajña-Varāha (the Boar incarnation), Viṣṇu of infinite splendor — even those who bow to Him, to them again and again salutations."]


एवं विधैर् वचनैश् च, तत्रापि वैखानसानां माहात्म्यं श्रुत्या सामृतिभिश्चोच्यते । छन्दोग-ब्राह्मणे - **“वैखानसा आर्षेया इन्द्रस्य प्रिया आसन्न् इत्य् अत्र वैखानसानां तु प्रीति-विषयत्वम् उक्तम् इति पूर्वम् उक्तम् ।”** श्रीमति वराहे च - **“वैखानसानां भगवत्-प्रीति-विषयत्वम् उक्तम् । अश्वत्थः कपिला गावः तुलसी विधनस् तथा । चत्वारो मत्-प्रिया राजन् तेषां वैखानसो वरः ॥ यत्र वैखानसा विप्राः यत्र सन्ति गवां गणाः । यत्र बिल्वाः पलाशाश् च तत्र सन्निहितो हरिः । एकान्तिनश् च सुस्वस्थाः देहान्तं नान्य-याजिनः ॥ आदेहान्तम् इति यावत् । कर्तव्यम् इति देवेश त्वां यजन्ते फलं विना । प्राप्नुवन्ति च देहान्ते वासुदेवत्वम् अब्जजेति ॥”** कौर्मे च - **“मच्-चित्ता मद्-गत-प्राणा मयि सङ्गत-मानसाः । अनन्य-शरण राजन् तस्माद् वैखानसा वराः ॥ इति भगवतैवोक्तम् ब्रह्म-कैवर्ते पुष्कर-तीर्थ-वैभव-वर्णने वर्णानां ब्राह्मणश् श्रेष्ठ आश्रमाणां यतिर् यथा । निम्नगानां यथा गङ्गा देवानां भगवान् हरिः ॥ श्रुतीनाम् आदि-भूता तु श्रुतिर् एकायनो मता । तत्-सान्निध्याद् यया वा स्यान् निषण्णो भगवान् वरः ॥ व्यूहानाम् आदि-भूतस् तु वासुदेवो यथा परः । त्रि-मूर्तीनां यथा विष्णुः शिवानां वा सदाशिवः ॥ धर्माणां वैष्णवो धर्मः स्मृतीनां मानवस् स्मृतिः । विप्राणां वेद-विदुषां यथा वैखानसो वरः ॥”** यथा मुनीनां विखना आदि-भूत उदाहृतः । सूत्राणां तत्-प्रणीतं तु यथा श्रेष्ठतमं स्मृतम् ॥ यथैव पुष्करो राजन् तीर्थानाम् उत्तमोत्तमम् ॥ इति सर्वोत्कृष्ट-गणना सह पठितत्वात् वैखानसानां वैभवम् उच्यते । महा-भारते शान्ति-पर्वणि मन्त्रिणां तु **“सहस्रेभ्यः ब्रह्मचारी विशिष्यते । ब्रह्मचारी-सहंस्रेभ्यो वेदाध्यायी विशिष्यते ॥ वेदाध्यायि-सहस्रेभ्यो नित्य-याजी विशिष्यते । नित्य-याजी-सहस्त्रेभ्यो विष्णु-भक्तो विशिष्यते । विष्णु-भक्तस् सहस्रेभ्यो विप्रो वैखानसो (विशिष्यते) ॥”** इति वैखानसानां सर्वोत्कृष्टत्वम् अभिधीयते । एवं योग-याज्ञवल्क्ये वैखानसो वैदिकेषु निर्दिष्टम् आचरेत् । तं ब्राह्मणं तु दृष्ट्वैव सर्व-पाप-निबर्हणम् उच्यते । तस्मात् पाप-भीरुभिर् वैखानसास् समाद्रियन्ते श्रुति-स्मृति-पुराणोक्त-समीचीनार्थ एव च सम्यग्-ज्ञानं सतां भूयान् न भूयाद् इतरञ्च । श्रीमद्-वेङ्कटनाथेन श्रेयसे भूयसे सताम् । वेदान्ताचार्येण विवृतस् सो ऽयं सज्जन-वैभवम् ॥

इति कवि-तार्किक-सिंहस्य कृतिषु सज्जन-वैभवस् समाप्तः ॥ हरिः ओम् ॥ शुभम् अस्तु ॥


Thus, by such statements, and also therein, the greatness of the Vaikhānasas is stated by Śruti and by Smṛti. In the Chāndogya Brāhmaṇa: "vaikhānasā ārṣeyā indrasya priyā āsan" — the Vaikhānasas are the Ārṣeyas; they were dear to Indra. Here, as stated before, the Vaikhānasas are stated to be the objects of affection. And in the Śrīmat Varāha Purāṇa, the being dear to the Lord of the Vaikhānasas is stated: "aśvatthaḥ kapilā gāvaḥ tulasī vidhanās tathā। catvāro mat-priyā rājan teṣāṃ vaikhānaso varaḥ॥" ["The sacred fig tree, the tawny cows, the tulasī plant, and the Vaikhānasas — these four are dear to Me, O King; among them, the Vaikhānasa is the best."] "yatra vaikhānasā viprāḥ yatra santi gavāṃ gaṇāḥ। yatra bilvāḥ palāśāś ca tatra sannihito hariḥ॥" ["Where there are Vaikhānasa brāhmaṇas, where there are herds of cows, where there are bilva and palāśa trees, there Hari is present."] "ekāntinaś ca susvasthāḥ dehāntaṃ na anya-yājinaḥ" ["The exclusive devotees, well-established, up to the end of the body, are not sacrificers to others."] "ā dehāntam iti yāvat kartavyaṃ iti deveśa tvāṃ yajante phalaṃ vinā। prāpnuvanti ca dehānte vāsudevatvam abjaje" ["Up to the end of the body, what is to be done, O Lord of gods, they worship You without desire for fruit. And at the end of the body, they attain the state of Vāsudeva, O lotus-born."] And in the Kaurma Purāṇa: "mac-cittā mad-gata-prāṇā mayi saṅgata-mānasāḥ। ananya-śaraṇa rājan tasmād vaikhānasā varāḥ॥" ["They whose minds are on Me, whose life-force is directed to Me, whose minds are attached to Me, who have no other refuge, O King, therefore the Vaikhānasas are the best."] Thus spoken by the Lord Himself. In the Brahma-kaivarta Purāṇa, in the description of the glory of Puṣkara Tīrtha: "varṇānāṃ brāhmaṇaḥ śreṣṭha āśramāṇāṃ yatir yathā। nimnagānāṃ yathā gaṅgā devānāṃ bhagavān hariḥ॥ śrutīnām ādi-bhūtā tu śrutir ekāyano matā। tat-sābidhyād yayā vā syān niṣaṇṇo bhagavān varaḥ॥ pyūhānām ādi-bhūtas tu vāsudevo yathā paraḥ। trimūrtīnāṃ yathā viṣṇuḥ śivānāṃ vā sadāśivaḥ॥ dharmāṇāṃ vaiṣṇavo dharmaḥ smṛtīnāṃ mānavaḥ smṛtiḥ。 viprāṇāṃ veda-viduṣāṃ yathā vaikhānaso varaḥ॥" ["As the brāhmaṇa is best among varṇas, the renunciant among āśramas, as the Gaṅgā among rivers, as Lord Hari among gods; as the Śruti considered the one path is the source of all Śrutis, by which the excellent Lord is seated; as Vāsudeva is the source of all expansions, as Viṣṇu is among the Trimūrti, as Sadāśiva is among Śivas; as Vaiṣṇava dharma is among dharmas, as Manu's is among smṛtis; so among brāhmaṇas learned in the Vedas, the Vaikhānasa is the best."] "yathā munīnāṃ vikhanā ādi-bhūta udāhṛtaḥ। sūtrāṇāṃ tat-praṇītaṃ tu yathā śreṣṭhatamaṃ smṛtam॥ yathaiva puṣkaro rājan tīrthānām uttamottamam॥" ["As Vikhanā is said to be the foremost among sages; as that which he promulgated is remembered as the most excellent among sūtras; as Puṣkara, O King, is the best among tīrthas."] Because this is read along with an enumeration of all that is supremely excellent, the glory of the Vaikhānasas is stated. In the Mahābhārata, Śāntiparva, among the counsels: "sahasrebhyaḥ brahmacārī viśiṣyate। brahmacārī-sahasrebhyo vedādhyāyī viśiṣyate॥ vedādhyāyī sahasrebhyo nitya-yājī viśiṣyate। nitya-yājī sahasrebhyo viṣṇu-bhakto viśiṣyate। viṣṇu-bhaktas sahasrebhyo vipro vaikhānasaḥ॥" ["Superior to a thousand (ordinary) brahmacārīs is a brahmacārī; superior to a thousand such brahmacārīs is one who studies the Veda; superior to a thousand such Veda-students is a constant sacrificer; superior to a thousand such constant sacrificers is a devotee of Viṣṇu; superior to a thousand such devotees of Viṣṇu is a Vaikhānasa brāhmaṇa."] Thus, the supreme excellence of the Vaikhānasas is declared. Thus, in the Yājñavalkya Smṛti: "vaikhānasaḥ vaidikeṣu nirdiṣṭam ācaret। taṃ brāhmaṇaṃ tu dṛṣṭaiva sarva-pāpa-nibarhaṇam ucyate। tasmāt pāpa-bhīrubhir vaikhānasāḥ samādriyante" ["The Vaikhānasa should practice what is prescribed in the Vedic texts. That brāhmaṇa, merely upon being seen, is said to destroy all sins. Therefore, the Vaikhānasas are revered by those afraid of sin."] The correct meaning stated in Śruti, Smṛti, and Purāṇa is indeed the perfect knowledge for the virtuous; may other (knowledge) not be. This glory of the virtuous vaikhānasas, explained for the supreme benefit of the virtuous by Śrīmad Veṅkaṭanātha, the Vedāntācārya ia completed.
