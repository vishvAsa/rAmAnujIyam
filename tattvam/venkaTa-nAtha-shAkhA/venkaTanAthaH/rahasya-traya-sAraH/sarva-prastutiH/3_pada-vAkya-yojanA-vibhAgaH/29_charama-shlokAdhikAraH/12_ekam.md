</details>

</details>


<details><summary>English</summary>

(1) While the singular form mām (me) itself **indicates** the idea of 'alone' (ekam),  
</details>

<details><summary>Español</summary>

(1) Mientras que la forma singular mām (yo) misma **indica** la idea de 'solo' (ekam),
</details>


<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

‘‘मां’’ ऎऩ्गिऱ पदत्तिले एकवचनत्ताले एकत्वम् तोऱ्ऱानिऱ्कप् 
</details>

<details><summary>४२तमाहोबिल-यतिः</summary>

प्रधानमायुळ्ळ आकारम् करुणै, परिकरमायुळ्ळ आकारङ्गळ् ज्ञानशक्तिस्वामित्वादिगळ्। इऩि एकशब्दत्तिऱ्कु व्याख्यानम् पण्णत् तुडङ्गुगिऱार् मां ऎऩ्गिऱ पदत्तिले इत्यादिना । 
</details>


<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

पिऩ्बुम् एक-शब्दम् 

> ’’तऩ्ऩैत् तन्द कऱ्पकम्+++(=कल्पकः → कल्प-वृक्षः)+++’’  
(तिरुवाय्मॊऴि २-७-११)  

ऎऩ्गिऱ बडिये  

> प्राप्यऩ् आऩवऩ् ताऩे  
प्रापकऩ् आऩ बडियाले  
उपाय-फलैक्यत्तैच् चॊल्लुगिऱद्  

ऎऩ्ऱुम् योजिप्पर्गळ्। 
</details>

<details><summary>नीलमेघः (सं)</summary>

पुनर् अप्य् उपात्त एक-शब्दः  

> "स्वात्म-दः कल्पकः+++(→ कल्प-वृक्षः)+++" 

इत्य्-उक्त-रीत्या 

> प्राप्यस्यैव प्रापकत्वसत्त्वाद्  
उपाय-फलैक्यम् आचष्ट 

इति [च] योजयन्ति । 
</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

the further **use** of the word ekam  
**is**, according to some, to **disclose** that  

He who **is** the object of **attainment**  
**is** Himself the means or upāya for that **attainment**  

like the (celestial) kalpaka tree **giving** itself (to those that **ask**). 

Thus (these commentators) **suggest** that  
the **use** of "ekam" after "mām" **is** to suggest the identity of the object of **attainment**  
and the means of **attaining** it. 
</details>

<details><summary>Español</summary>

el **uso** posterior de la palabra "ekam"  
**es**, según algunos, **revelar** que  

El que **es** el objeto de **logro**  
**es** Él mismo el medio o upāya para ese **logro**  

como el árbol (celestial) kalpaka **entregándose** (a aquellos que **piden**).

Por lo tanto (estos comentaristas) **sugieren** que  
el **uso** de "ekam" después de "mām" **es** para **sugerir** la identidad del objeto de **logro**  
y los medios para **alcanzarlo**.
</details>


<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

पिऩ्बुम् एकशब्दम् ’’तऩ्ऩैत्तन्द कऱ्पगम्’’(तिरुवाय्मॊऴि २-७-११)  ऎऩ्गिऱबडिये प्राप्यऩाऩवऩ् ताऩे प्रापकऩाऩबडियाले उपायफलैक्यत्तैच् चॊल्लुगिऱदॆऩ्ऱुम् योजिप्पर्गळ्। 
</details>

<details><summary>४२तमाहोबिल-यतिः</summary>

ऎऩक्के तऩ्ऩैत् तन्द कऱ्पगमिति । तऩ्ऩै ऎऩ्ऱु दानकर्मत्वम् सॊल्लुगैयाले ईप्सिततमऩाऩ तऩक्कु प्राप्यत्वमुम्, तन्द ऎऩ्ऱु दानकर्तृत्वम् सॊल्लुगैयाले प्रापकत्वमुम् तोऱ्ऱुगिऱदु ऎऩ्ऱुम् योजिप्पार्गळ् ऎऩ्ऱु उम्मैयाले वक्ष्यमाणयोजनान्तरङ्गळ् सङ्गृहीतङ्गळ्। 
</details>


### भागवत-वचनम्

यास्यसि ह्य् अकुतोभयम्)  
(श्रीभागवतम्
 ११-१२-१४) 

ऎऩ्गिऱ समान-प्रकरण-वाक्यमुम् अनुगुणम्।

<details><summary>नीलमेघः (सं)</summary>

अस्य 

> ‘‘माम् एकम् एव शरणम्’’  
(आत्मानं सर्वदेहिनां ।  
याहि सर्वात्म-भावेन  
यास्यसि ह्य् अकुतोभयम्)  
(श्रीभागवतम्
 ११-१२-१४) 

इति समान-प्रकरण-वाक्यम् अप्य् अनुगुणम् । 
</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

This **interpretation finds support** in the sentence "mām ekam eva" in a similar context :- 

> " By all means, **seek** the **protection** only of me alone  
who **am** the inner self of all beings,  
You will then **have no** fear of any kind." 
</details>

<details><summary>Español</summary>

Esta **interpretación encuentra apoyo** en la **oración** "mām ekam eva" en un contexto similar - 

> "Por supuesto, **busca** la **protección** sólo de mí solo  
que **soy** el yo interior de todos los seres,  
Entonces **no tendrás** miedo de ningún tipo".
</details>

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

इदुक्कु ‘‘मामेकमेव शरणं’’(श्रीभागवतम् ११-१२-१४) ऎऩ्गिऱ समानप्रकरणवाक्यमुमनुगुणम्। 
</details>


<details><summary>४२तमाहोबिल-यतिः</summary>

इन्द योजऩैक्कु समानप्रकरणस्थप्रमाणान्तरवाक्यानुगुण्यमुण्डॆऩ्गिऱार् इदुक्कु मामेकमेव शरणमिति ।  

> ‘‘माम् एकम् एव शरणम्  
आत्मानं सर्वदेहिनां ।  
याहि सर्वात्म-भावेन  
यास्यसि ह्य् अकुतोभयम्’’ 

ऎऩ्बदु इन्द श्लोकम्। 
</details>


<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

एव-कारम् उण्ड् आय् इरुक्क  
एक-शब्दङ् किडन्दाल्  
इदुक्क् अवधारणम् ऒऴिय  
वेऱे बॊरुळ् कॊळ्ळुगैय् उचितम् इऱे। 
</details>

<details><summary>नीलमेघः (सं)</summary>

एव-कारे सत्य् एव  
एक-शब्दे सति  
अस्य अवधारण-व्यतिरिक्तार्थस्याभ्युपगम उचितः किल ।  
[[P378]]
</details>


<details><summary>English</summary>

Since the word "eva" (only) **is** already **present**,  
it **is** but proper to **seek** some other **purpose**  
for the **existence** or **use** of the word "ekam" in addition to only.  

(This **purpose is** the **declaration** of the oneness of the means and the end).
</details>

<details><summary>Español</summary>

Dado que la palabra "eva" (sólo) **está** ya **presente**,  
**es** pero apropiado **buscar** algún otro **propósito**  
por la **existencia** o **uso** de la palabra "ekam" además de únicamente.  

(Este **propósito es** la **declaración** de la unidad de los medios y el fin).
</details>


<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

एवकारमुण्डायिरुक्क एकशब्दङ्गिडन्दालिदुक्कवधारणमॊऴिय वेऱेबॊरुळ् कॊळ्ळुगैयुचितमिऱे। 
</details>

<details><summary>४२तमाहोबिल-यतिः</summary>

‘‘एकमेव शरणं’’ ऎऩ्गिऱविडत्तिल् ताऩ् प्राप्यप्रापकैक्यम् सॊल्लवेण्डियदु आवश्यकमो ऎऩ्ऩ वरुळिच्चॆय्गिऱार् एवकारमुण्डायिरुक्क इति । अङ्गु अवधारणार्थकशब्दमिरुप्पदाले एकशब्दत्ताले फलोपायैक्यम् सॊल्वदे उचितमॆऩ्ऱु करुत्तु।
</details>

<details><summary>Español</summary>

**es**, en esta frase, un mero sinónimo de "ningún otro"

<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

</details>

<details><summary>English</summary>

and others say that (it) **means** 'only' or 'alone' (as in the ślokas),

**will surmount** the **insurmountable** māyā" 

and

</details>

<details><summary>Español</summary>

y otros **dicen** que **significa** 'sólo' o 'solo' (como en los ślokas), 

> “Aquellos que **me buscan** solo (y a ningún otro) como refugio  
**superará** la **insuperable** māyā" 

y 

> "**Busca** la **protección** sólo de Él (y de ningún otro)".
</details>


<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

‘‘मामेव ये प्रपद्यन्ते’’(गीता ७-१४), ‘‘तमेव शरणङ्गच्छ’’(गीता १८-६२) इत्यादिगळिऱ्पडिये अवधारणार्थमाय्

‘‘मामेव ये’’, ‘‘तमेव शरणं’’ इत्यादिगळ् अनुगुणङ्गळॆऩ्ऱु तिरुवुळ्ळम्।

the **answer is** as **follows**:-

As the man **adopts**, for his own **protection**,  
the means **ordained** for Him by the śāstras  
for **propitiating** (the Lord),  
</details>

<details><summary>Español</summary>

la **respuesta es** la **siguiente**:-

Mientras el hombre **adopta**, para su propia **protección**,  
los medios **ordenados** para Él por los śāstras  
por **propiciar** (al Señor),
</details>


<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

on an equal footing with the Saviour  
who **is** the Siddhopāya.

en pie de igualdad con el Salvador  
quién **es** el Siddhopāya.

## श्रीमत्त्वम्
<details><summary>English</summary>

THE SAVIOUR IS BHAGAVAN WITH ŚRĪ AS HIS ATTRIBUTE: page575
</details>

<details><summary>Español</summary>

EL SALVADOR **ES** BHAGAVAN CON ŚRĪ COMO SU ATRIBUTO: página 575
</details>




There are some commentators who state that the meaning of the word eka (a single one) declares that the Saviour is only one (namely, Bhagavān) and that Śrī has nothing to do with salvation). But their contention does not stand to reason. Just as qualities and forms qualify the Lord as His attributes, so also Śrī is an attribute of His (inseparable from Him). She is the ruler over all beings and is dear to Bhagavān and is declared in the śrutis as sharing in His duties and as ever bent on protecting those that seek her help. Though it is stated (in the Chandogya Upaniṣad and other Vedic texts) that the material cause of the Universe is only one (sat), yet other things that are requisite as material causes are understood as existing as His attributes (viz. cit and acit in their subtle and unmanifested forms) on the authority of the pramāṇas. So also in this context, Śrī should be understood, although not distinctly stated. Even when a single form of upāsana or meditation is enjoined, the qualities that are connected with the object of meditation enter also into it. Similarly though a single deity is enjoined as the Saviour to be sought, Śrī, who is His attribute, should also be considered as being associated with Him (in the act of salvation). The Siddhopāya (namely, Bhagavān ) is (of course) single but His qualities remain with Him (though not explicitly stated). In the same way, in the opinion of those who see with the eye of the Śāstra, Lakṣmī (His spouse) is associated inseparably with Him as an attribute, Light and the object emitting light (are inseparable from each other) and when one is mentioned , the other is also presint by association. Likewise when either (Bhagavān or Śrī) is mentioned, the other is also present by virtue of their being always with each other. Sages have declared in the smṛtis that she helps (jīvas) to cross the sea of saṁsāra and both of them (Bhagavān and Śrī) have themselves spoken to the same effect in such (samhitas) treatises as sātvatam. In Dvaya it is considered appropriate that kaiṅkarya or service in the state of attainment (mokṣa) should extend to Śrī (as to Bhagavān) So Śrī is equally connected with Him in both the states, that of the endeavour for attaining mokṣa (the first half of Dvaya) and that after attainment (the second half of it). Therefore, on the authority of many Vedic texts and Śrutis which cannot be stated to have some other purport, the single one who is to be sought as the Saviour is the Lord with His attribute Śrī who is His consort.
