We have great pleasure in presenting to the public, as our maiden venture in publication, Sri Yamunacharya's "SIDDHITRAYA". This book was originally published in the year 1942, on the Shashtiabdapoorthi cele- brations of Sri Kozhiyalam Swami with a concise Sanskrit commentary by Sri U. Ve. Abhinava Desika Uttamur Swami. At a later date an English translation was also published jointly by Sri R. Ramanujacharya, Professor of Philosophy and Dean of Annamalai University and Mimamsa Kesari Sri K. Srinivasacharya, Lecturer, Annamalai University. 

It is our Vice\-Chairman Sri V. T. Rangaswamy Iyengar, who suggested the re\-printing of the above book as the original publication was out of print. He also suggested that we apply for financial aid from the Ministry of Education, Govt. of India and he himself came forward to bear the balance in cost of the publication. Mainly through his efforts, we get a sanction of less than half the cost of the book from the Govt. of India. 

Sri U. Ve. Abhinava Desika Uttamur Swami agreed to provide a more elaborate Sanskrit Commentary and also agreed to undertake the Printing of the Sanskrit version in his own press Our reverential thanks are due to him. 

Sri R. Ramanujachari and Sri K. Srinivasacharya also gave us permi- ssion to reprint the English commentary and Sri R. Ramanujachari readily agreed to re\-edit the English version and our thanks are also due to him. 

Our thanks are also due to Sri Raghavasimham Press and India Printing Works for undertaking the printing of the English version. 

Our thanks are also due to Muthu & Co. for undertaking the binding work of this book.   
2 

The importance of the present publication, SIDDHITRAYA, will be seen from the introduction to the English version by Sri R. Ramanujachari. As earlier said, Sri V. T. Rangaswami Iyengar, our Vice\-Chairman, during his intensive study of all the Granthas on Visishtadvaita Siddhanta, found that 'SIDDHITRAYA' of Sri Yamunacharya which gave the basis for Sri Bhagavad Ramanuja in writing the famous commentary, 'SRI BHASHYA' on Brahma Sutras, and which still occupies the undisputed and unassailable rock basement for all the SRI SUKTHIS of the later Acharyas including Sri Vedanta Desika, needs reprinting. 

It is only fitting that this being the very first available book on Visishtadvaita such an important book should be our maiden venture in publication and any amount of thanks will not be sufficient to show our gratitude to Sri V. T. Rangaswamy Iyengar in not only suggesting the publication but also undertaking to finance the entire cost. 

It is regrettable to note that Visishtadvaita Siddhanta lacks the publi- city it deserves in foreign countries and we are sure that the efforts of Sri V. T. Rangaswamy Iyengar will fill a long felt need of publicity among the English knowing scholars of the world. We only pray to the Lord Almighty, Sriman Narayana, to give Sri V. T. Rangaswamy Iyengar, long life and continued interest and health to achieve the task he has undertaken to bring to print other books like Sri Baghavad Gita, etc. with English commentaries. 

Finally our reverential thanks are also due to our Chairman, Sri U. Ve. Abhinava Desika Uttamur Swami, for not only guiding us in all our activities but also for publishing all the works of ancient Acharyas like Sri Vedanta Desika with versatile commentaries in Sanskrit and Tamil so that our task in translating to English will be easy. May our Lord Sriman Narayana give him long life and continued good health.
