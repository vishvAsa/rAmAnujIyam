The 5 pakshas on Sri in Ramanuja sampradAya

“आहुस्स्वैरनुरूपरूपविभवैर्गाढोपगूढानि ते”

On the pertinent question of to what likeness lakshmi is to nArAyaNa - there seems to have been 5 views among the successors of rAmAnuja - these are summarized by Desika in his chatusshloki bhashya - of which adiyen has attempted to loosely translate the relevant tracts:

1st

The 1st paksha:
Let it be that Lakshmi has a form appropriate to Vishnu with respect to his svarUpa-rUpa-gUna etc. How do we interpret statements like सर्वभूतस्था द्विज , यथा सर्वगतो विष्णुस्तथैवेयं द्विजोत्तम​ that she is everywhere or all pervasive?
Elsewhere there are statements like She resides in valuable materials like turmeric,gold etc - therefore she isnt all pervasive by nature - but atomic.
To this we reply:
For reconciling both the types of statements - we tell that these auspicious objects are under Her special abhimAna or regard.
Statements like व्यापिनी सर्वगा , सुसूक्ष्मा which tell She is present everywhere - is subtle - refer to her all pervasive nature being able to go anywhere without any barriers - much like ether.
If one may tell She is atomic in nature but by her shakti or powers she is able to pervade all - such a view is indeed a hidden form of Jainism. Such people tell:
By what means Ishvara due to his aghaTita ghaTanA sAmarthya i.e able to do impossible things - becomes atomic inside jIvas - in the same way She being a aNu is able to become vibhu by her special powers. This view too is unacceptable.
Due to limiting adjuncts alone do we accept the atomic nature of Ishvara for purposes of meditation - by his very nature he is all pervasive only.
If it be said that by aghaTita ghaTanA shakti it is possible to have atomic and all pervasive nature - then why do you object the views of advaitins and bhedAbheda vAdins who have similar arguments.
If it be said that at times lakshmi becomes vibhu - then by similar arguments we can also tell this is true for all jIvas - and they will be subject to svarUpa vikAra - which is unacceptable for us. Also the brahma sUtra criticizing Jainas - न पर्यायादपि अविरोध​: विकारिभ्य​: becomes meaningless.
Some others hold that all pervasiveness of lakshmi refers to Her residing on bhagavAn’s vigraham always -by the shloka “काचान्या त्वामृते देवि!” - by the Lord’s eternal wish has she been granted such a position similar to other nitya sUris like ananta, garuDa being granted other works - we respond:
If it has been said for bhagavAn’s vigraha alone we can accept - however She is said to be closely tied to bhagavAn’s svarUpa also. This is in general true of all chetanas and achetanas - in such a case what is the point of making a stotram or praise for lakshmi - what is the speciality in Her that sets her aside from other insentient and sentient objects? Only specialities can form the object of a praise.
If it be said that anurUpatva or similarity is the praise - we may analyse what exactly is anurUpatva -
I. If it be said that by svarUpa they are similar - as lakshmi is atomic and vishnu all pervasive - as both are different - this cant be stuti.
Ii. If it be said that by being the sub ordinate of bhagavAn - she becomes the property of His giving Him glory - this is again common for all insentient and sentient objects- so it cant be stuti.
Iii. If it be told that Both are omniscient since time eternal - this is common for nityasUris also - hence it cannot be stuti. Furthermore as it is told that lakshmi is the sheShi or the one getting glory and being master of nitya sUris - one cannot make a stuti on the basis of Her having some qualities in common with the lower nitya sUris. Else one may also make a stotra based on her dravyatva - chetanatva etc which is meaningless.
Iv. By the Vishnu purANa statement - by You and Vishnu this world is pervaded - the pervasion is the one which is said to be the object of praise “त्वयैतद् विष्णुना चाम्ब जगद्व्याप्तं चराचरम्” - this does not suit Her if she is a jIva or a ishvara.

  1. If she is a jIva - we observe that all jIvas are said to be aNu or atomic in nature - this would lead to atiprasanga or inapplicability of definition of jIvas to Her. Therefore the pervasion can be told in terms of having infinite vigrahas or forms as said in “सौम्याऽसौम्यै: जगद्रूपै: त्वयैतद्देवि पूरितम्” - You pervade this whole universe by your beautiful as well as gory forms. The meaning for “जगद्व्याप्तं चराचरम्”, “यथा सर्वगतो विष्णु:” should be taken similarly.
  2. If she is a Ishvara: This contradicts the statement that bhagavAn has no equal or superior: “न तत्समश्चाभ्यधिकश्च दृश्यते”. If it be told that both are the same entity - then there is no point in calling 1 the husband and the other - wife - telling anurUpatva also is useless.

To this we(Deshika) reply:

The argument that using some attributes common to another entity for praise is not proper is meaningless. One may as well use common attributes for an object of praise. For ex: omniscience is common to both bhagavAn and the nityasUris - however we still use it to praise Him.

2nd

The second paksha:
In response to the objection in the first paksha iv a. Another sect says: Lakshmi is indeed all pervasive but belongs to the jIva class.
The sections of the shruti which tell that jIvas are atomic refer to those jIvas which are in bondage. In the same place it is said - स चानन्त्याय कल्पते - He becomes fit for being limitless - it refers to the dharmabhUta jnAna becoming infinite as jIvas are nirvikAra or having no change. Elsewhere pervasion due to dharmabhUtajnAna is told - सर्वमेव आविशन्ति - he pervades all and so is it said in the brahma sUtra - प्रदीपवदावेश​: . As nityas belong to the class of jIvas - they too are aNu as there are no other extra statements pointing out they are all pervasive - but also there are statements which support the same. In the case of Lakshmi this is not so - there are statements which one cannot interpret otherwise in the case of Her. In the case of yathA sarvagato viShnu:- one cannot tell that it refers to being able to incarnate everywhere or being the ruler of all. Nor is it appropriate to tell that she pervades everywhere like a mukta does through their dharmabhUtajnAna - as there is no shruti which opposes such a pervasion in case of Her. If it be told that 2 all pervasive entities cannot exist together - we tell that it does in case of time - the dharmabhUtajnAnas of nityas, muktas and Ishvara which co-exist. Therefore the core attribute of all jIvas is being subservient and sentient - being atomic in nature holds good for all jIvas other than lakshmi.
Another interpretation for aNu could be being limited - as being subservient to Vishnu limits Lakshmi’s freedom - in that sense one may tell She too is aNu. On the question of whether such a meaning has been used for aNu anywhere - we see this in janmAdyadhikaraNa in srI bhAshya - anantapada - deZakAlavastuparicchedarahita svarUpamAha. saguNatvAt svarUpasya svarUpeNa guNaischAnantyam.

Here by svarUpa and guNa - the nityas have been distinguished from Brahman. In the case of lakshmi - even though she is all pervasive by svarUpa- due to being limited by qualities such as pAratantrya to Her Lord - She is distinguished from Brahman.

3rd

The 3rd paksha:
Due to lakshmi being sentient - she is different from acit tattva. Due to being all pervasive - she is different from jIvas too. As she is subservient - she is different from Ishvara also. One may object that if such an interpretation is kept - it is as absurd as calling an object sadasadvilakSaNa - or being different from existence as well as non existence. This is because it is said that sentient beings are divided as jIva and Ishvara only - and there is not another extra entity. The answer is that this division was kept in the 1st place without having the objective of examining Lakshmi’s nature. In reality we see that the wife has a different treatment than her husband as well as children. In the same way lakshmi too is different from all. Being such - her nature is under the control of nArAyaNa. The greatness of bhagavAn comes from lakshmi in the same way as the lustre is to a gemstone and fragrance is to a flower.
Therefore here also we may tell that the svarUpa of lakshmi is similar to bhagavAn.

4th

The 4th paksha:
Some others say : The nature and existence of Lakshmi may be so - even then she belongs to the class of ishvara and not jIva as she is the sheshi of the entire universe. Here sarvasheshitva is for nArAyaNa alone. Lakshmi is the sheshi of all except bhagavAn. Similarity is as said before in the 3rd paksha. Attributes like jagatkAraNatva - mokShapradatva which are unique for Brahman belong to Vishnu alone. Therefore the samAdhikadaridratA or being unexcelled or equalled by anyone of Vishnu remains unaffected.
The above 3 pakshas (2nd,3rd and 4th) are not very different from each other. They just have differences in terms of calling Her as a jIva - or a 4th entity or Ishvari. In all the above 4 pakshas the glories of lakshmi come from the eternal wish of Vishnu alone - this is due to statements such as “न तस्येशे कश्चन”. Therefore all the 4 pakshas tell that absolute controllership is in the hands of bhagavAn alone.

5th

The 5th paksha:
The 4th pakshavAdin tells - Lakshmi also is the meaning of the word - नार - and Vishnu is the support for Her. If it be said Qualities like being the chief Goal are present with Vishnu alone by His nature - for Her it is by His will alone that she gets such a status - we tell that it is not contradictory to her being equal to Him - as even when the forms are the same - the actions done by each may vary. As bhagavAn does everything for the sake of lakshmi - there is no contradiction in telling that lakshmi too has contribution to jagatkAraNatva. Even in works started together - Vishnu being told as the main cause too is explained due to worldly parlance of calling out the husband even when there is contribution of the wife - the wife is said to be included in the works of the husband.
The qualities of the wife being subservient to the husband too are due to the mutual will of each other. She too wishes the same there is no contradiction.
Statements such as “सर्वभूतेश्वर​” apply to both of Them - hence the qualities like subservience of Hers are due to mutual will of each other - so do some say.
As the will of each other are not contradictory - but in fact in mutual harmony - all statements of the vedas become reconciled.

Summary

Among 5 of the above views - only 1 has all necessary supporting statements. In any case the rulership of the world being applicable to the One with Lakshmi has been firmly established. Here even intelligent people may harbour doubts on some points.

However as long as there is clarity on the useful aspects of the nature of Lakshmi - the means and the goal will definitely be achieved. In all the aspects of Vishnu such as rulership - ease of accessibility - the means and the goal - the appropriate connection of Lakshmi with Her Lord has been shown.
