Regards…….Congratulations…..

“स गुरुः यः अधिगततत्त्वः शिष्यहिताय उद्यतः सततम्” (“Sa [[guruh|guruḥ]] yaḥ [[adhigatatatvaḥ|adhigatatattvaḥ]] [[śisyahitāya|śiṣyahitāya]] udyataḥ satatam”) -

“He who knows the principle and is ready for the continuous welfare of the disciple is truly a Guru” - this is the saying of Adi Guru Acharya Shankara. Such Gurus are available in life only through Sukrita. Similarly, it is also through Sukrita that a worthy disciple is available to the Guru. That is why the Upanishad orders ("……Prāpya Varān Nibodhata").

‘A worthy disciple for a master, a great master for a disciple’ - this is a rare combination. Such a rare “Guru-Śiṣya Samagama” can be found among “Panditaratnam K.S.Varadacharya and Shrimad Jagadguru Shankaracharya Sonda Swarnavalli Shri Gangadharendra Saraswati Mahaswamiji”. Panditaratnam K.S.Varadacharya of Mysuru was the one who imparted the secret of knowledge to the disciples as an authority. Shri Shri Gangadharendra Saraswati Mahaswamiji is the one who has acquired the knowledge from Shriyuta Varadacharya and has elevated himself to the pinnacle of achievement and also involved in [[Atmōddhāra|Ātmōddhāra]] and [[Dharmōddhāra|Dharmōddhāra]]. Such a [[Guru-Sişya|Guru-Śiṣya]] Tradition is the ideology of Sanatana Dharma. [[P339]]

Panditaratnam K.S.Varadacharya was a scholar from his teaching-speech-research-writing works. He gave the world, the fragrance of knowledge like a flower hidden behind the leaves. It is a fortune to have such a scholar as teacher to our Swamiji before and after his [[Sanyasashrama|Sannyāsāśrama]]. When Swamiji decided to reveal the greatness of his [[Sadhguru|Sadguru]] to the world, the ‘Corona pandemic’ spread everywhere and the world came to a standstill. By the time the world became active again, Panditaratnam had departed the mortal world at the age of 100. Undeterred by his resolve, our Swamiji decided to pay his admiration to the Acharya in Mysuru, the [[Karmabhumi|Karmabhūmi]] of Panditaratnam K. S. Varadacharya.

Swamiji had arrived to Mangaladhama (Brahmin Street) of Shrirangapattanam, near Mysuru for holy work on 26th January 2022, also, organized a meeting with his and Acharya’s disciples, scholars and dignitaries. Consensus after optimistic discussion “Panditaratnam K.S.Varadacharya Centenary Celebration Committee” was formed. Heeding to the unanimous prayers of all, Swamiji agreed to become the honorary president of the committee and provide guidance. The committee was extended to have three more honorary presidents, a president, a working president, eight vice presidents, a treasurer, an organizer, a chief secretary and two secretaries. Shri [[B.V.llaiy|B.V. Ilaiy]] Alwar [[Swamyji|Swamiji]], who is active in cultural and social field of Mysuru was selected as the Working President and [[P340]] Vidwan A.M.Chandrasekhar, the secretary of Veda Shastra Poshini Sabha, Mysuru was selected as the Treasurer of the Committee.

It was decided to make the Centenary Committee a unit of the Vedashastra Poshini Sabha and to open a separate bank account, so that, the financial affairs of the Committee would be conducted in a regular and transparent manner. A savings account was opened in Karnataka Bank, Ramanuja Road, Mysuru. It was also decided that the bank account will be jointly operated by the Treasurer Vidwan A.M.Chandrasekhara and the Chief Secretary Vidwan Heramba R Bhat. Further, regular online meetings were held every week under the presidency of Swamiji, and the outline of the program was decided.

The committee decided that this centenary celebration shall include an opening ceremony, release of a commemorative volume, a seminar consisting of three academic sessions namely “Matatraya Samanwaya”, “Jivana Samanwaya” and “[[Darśan|Darśana]] Samanwaya”, felicitation of four senior Vedic-Shastra scholars, a valedictory and recitations of Chaturveda and Tamil Divine Essays. Four sub-committees were formed namely Resource Mobilization Committee, Commemorative Volume Editorial Committee, Seminar Organising Committee and Parayana Organising Committee to facilitate the implementation of the above resolutions. Shri.H.V.Rajeev, Chairman, MUDA, Mysuru, Vidwan Dr. H. V. Nagaraja Rao, renowned scholar, [[P341]] Vidwan Dr. T. V. Satyanarayana, Eminent Scholar, Ve. Venkataramana Bhatta (Magodu), Purohit, were selected as head of the sub-committees respectively to accomplish the centenary celebration successfully.

Further, it was scheduled to organise the Veda and Prabandha Parayanas from 19th to 23rd October 2022, for five days at Shri Sitaraghava Prarthana Mandira, Krishnamurthypuram, Mysuru. The release of Commemorative Volume, Seminars, Awards for Scholars etc. were scheduled on 22nd and 23rd, October 2022 at Sharada Vilas Centenary Hall, Mysuru.

Decision was made to arrange for accommodation and catering for the scholars, priests and the invited dignitaries, breakfast for the volunteers, lunch and afternoon snacks for the spectators participating in the programs for five days. In regular meeting presided over by our Swamiji, all these matters were thoroughly discussed and ensured that specific responsibilities were given to the members of the committee. As a result of this preliminary preparation, the centenary celebration was a success beyond expectations and praised by all.

Heartfelt thanks and congratulations to all the committee members who contributed to the unprecedented success of the program.

There is no doubt that the success of the program is due to the determination, guidance1, divine presence and blessings of Shri Shri Gangadharendra Saraswati [[P342]]

The enthusiasm of Shri [[llaiy|Ilaiy]] Alwar Swamiji, the working president of the committee, and his ability to encourage, unite and lead us to accomplish this great task will not be forgotten. The centenary celebration of Panditaratnam K.S.Varadacharya, which was unprecedentedly and meaningfully accomplished, will remain a record forever from the Commemorative Volume which was released during the inauguration held on 22nd October 2022 by Shri Vishweshwara Hegde Kageri, Hon’ble Speaker of the Legislative Assembly, Government of Karnataka. Presently, the work of including rare photographs of the centenary celebration and unpublished & valuable articles of Varadacharya in this volume are under progress. The size of the present volume is more than a thousand pages, and for convenience, it was decided to divide this volume and publish Varadacharya’s articles as a separate book with the title “Sathyanveśanam”. Regards and Congratulations to One and All.
