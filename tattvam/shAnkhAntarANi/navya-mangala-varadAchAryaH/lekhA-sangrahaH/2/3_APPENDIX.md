"स गुरुः यः अधिगततत्त्वः शिष्यहिताय उद्यतः सततम्" ("Sa guruh yaḥ adhigatatatvaḥ śisyahitāya udyataḥ satatam") \- 

"He who knows the principle and is ready for the continuous welfare of the disciple is truly a Guru" \- this is the saying of Adi Guru Acharya Shankara. Such Gurus are available in life only through Sukrita. Similarly, it is also through Sukrita that a worthy disciple is available to the Guru. That is why the Upanishad orders ("......Prāpya Varan Nibōdhata"). 

\`A worthy disciple for a master, a great master for a disciple' \- this is a rare combination. Such a rare "Guru- Śişya Samagama" can be found among "Panditaratnam K.S.Varadacharya and Shrimad Jagadguru Shankaracharya Sonda Swarnavalli Shri Gangadharendra Saraswati Mahaswamiji". Panditaratnam K.S.Varadacharya of Mysuru was the one who imparted the secret of knowledge to the disciples as an authority. Shri Shri Gangadharendra Saraswati Mahaswamiji is the one who has acquired the knowledge from Shriyuta Varadacharya and has elevated himself to the pinnacle of achievement and also involved in Atmōddhāra and Dharmōddhāra. Such a Guru\-Sişya Tradition is the ideology of Sanatana Dharma.
